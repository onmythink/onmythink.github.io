THE LEAPING FROG: WORD PROBLEM

Problem:

Solve the word problem involving series and sequence. A frog wants to reach the end of a bamboo tree that has a height of a 78 meters. The frog leaps 4 meters during the day and slips 2 meters down during the night. How many days will it take the frog to reach the top of the bamboo?​

Answers with Step-by-step explanation:

In order to solve this word problem involving series and sequence, we can use our analytic and critical thinking skills to obtain the required data. We can also form a function in order to solve this puzzle. But we can also use a little bit of reverse engineering. As given in the problem, the frog leaps 4 meters during the day and slips 2 meters down during the night. This means that in a day, the frog can reach 2 meters. Since the top of the bamboo is 78 meters, this is the height that the frog needs to climb. The frog can keep doing this same routine for over 35 days.

On the 35th day, the frog has already leaped 70 meters.

On the 36th day, the frog has already leaped 72 meters.

On the 37th day, the frog has already leaped 74 meters.

On the daytime of 38th day, since the frog leaps constantly by 4 meters, he has already climbed the 78-meter tall bamboo. So the correct answer is 37 and a half days or we can just round this up to 38 days.
