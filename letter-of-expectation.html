30. In a letter to a friend, George Washington wrote, “I greatly apprehend that my countrymen will expect too much from me.” What do these words mean? Why might Washington have felt this way?


The words "I greatly apprehend that my countrymen will expect too much from me" mean that George Washington is worried that his fellow citizens have very high expectations of him, and he is not sure if he can meet them. Washington is concerned that people will put too much pressure on him to be a perfect leader, which may result in his failure to meet their expectations. He may have felt this way because he knew that his leadership was crucial to the success of the new country, and he was well aware of the challenges and difficulties that lay ahead. Additionally, Washington was a humble man who did not seek power or fame, and he may have been uncomfortable with the idea of people placing him on a pedestal.
