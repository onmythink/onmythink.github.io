7. how to use isometric drawing (Pa sagot po)​


Answer:

To use isometric drawing, start by creating a 30-degree angle guide on your paper or canvas by drawing two 30-degree lines that intersect at a point. Next, use this guide to draw the basic shape of the object you want to represent, keeping in mind that all lines drawn parallel to the angle guide will appear to be at the same distance away from the viewer, while lines drawn perpendicular to the angle guide will appear to be closer or farther away. To add depth and realism to your drawing, you can use hatching or shading to indicate areas that are in shadow or at different angles to the light source. Practice and experimentation will help you to master the technique.

13. Define Isometric Drawing.​


Answer:

Isometric projection is a method for visually representing three-dimensional objects in two dimensions in technical and engineering drawings. It is an axonometric projection in which the three coordinate axes appear equally foreshortened and the angle between any two of them is 120 degrees.

19. why we need to know on how to draw isometric and orthographic drawing in carpentry? ​


Answer:

Isometric drawing, also called isometric projection, method of graphic representation of three-dimensional objects, used by engineers, technical illustrators, and, occasionally, architects. The technique is intended to combine the illusion of depth, as in a perspective rendering, with the undistorted presentation of the object’s principal dimensions—that is, those parallel to a chosen set of three mutually perpendicular coordinate axes.
