3. venn diagram of ionic vs covalent bonding


Explanation:

IONIC BOND

Ionic bond, also called electrovalent bond, type of linkage formed from the electrostatic attraction between oppositely charged ions in a chemical compound. Such a bond forms when the valence (outermost) electrons of one atom are transferred permanently to another atom.

COVALENT BOND

A covalent bond is a chemical bond that involves the sharing of electron pairs between atoms. These electron pairs are known as shared pairs or bonding pairs, and the stable balance of attractive and repulsive forces between atoms, when they share electrons, is known as covalent bonding.

9. Ionic vs Covalent Bonding differences and similarities?


Answer:

Similarities between ionic and covalent bonds

1) In both bonds, electrons are involved to form ionic & covalent bond.

2) Both bonded molecules have some forces of attraction.

3) Almost both bonded molecules obey duplet & octet rule.

4) Both ionic & covalent bonds give stability to elements.

5) In both bonds valence electrons take part in bonding.

Differences between ionic and covalent bonds

1) In ionic bond transfer of electrons is obeyed & in covalent bond sharing of electrons is obeyed.

2) Ionic bond is formed between metals & non-metals but covalent bond is formed between non-metals.

3) Ionic bond is formed between different elements, not formed with same elements, but covalent bond is formed between both similar and different elements.

4) Ionic bond is formed with the electronegativity difference more than 1.7 & covalent bonded molecules electronegativity difference is less than 1.7.

5) Ionic bonds have don't sigma and pi-bonds but covalent bonded molecules have both sigma & pi-bond.

6) Ionic bonded molecules show no resonance but covalent bonded molecules show resonance.

7) In ionic bonded molecules there is no hyperconjugation, but covalent bonded molecules have this feature.

8) Ionic bonded molecules have strong electrostatic attraction but covalent bonded molecules have weak electrostatic attraction.

9) Ionic bonded molecules are soluble in polar solvents but covalent bonded molecules are soluble in non-polar solvents.

10) Ionic bonded molecules are not organic in nature, they are inorganic in nature. But covalent bonded molecules have inorganic & organic nature.

11) Ionic bonded molecules show no isomerism but covalent bonded molecules have this feature.

12) There are no single, double, or triple bonds in ionic bonded molecules but covalent bonded molecules may be single, double or triple bonded.

13) Ionic bonded molecules show no catenation but covalent bonded molecules in organic form show catenation ( ability to form long chain).

11. What is the first thing to do if you experience a tire blowout


Answer:

If you experience a tire blowout while driving, it's important to remain calm and take the following steps:

1. Ease off the gas pedal and allow your car to slow down gradually.

2. Hold the steering wheel firmly and keep your car in a straight line. Avoid braking or making any sudden movements.

3. Once you have slowed down and are in a safe location, carefully pull off the road and turn on your hazard lights.

4. If you have a spare tire, jack, and lug wrench in your vehicle, you can change the tire yourself. If you don't have these tools or don't feel comfortable changing the tire, call for roadside assistance or a tow truck to take you to a nearby tire repair shop.

It's important to note that a tire blowout can be dangerous, so it's important to react quickly and calmly to the situation.

27. what is a Brazilian culture


The Brazilian culture is one of the world’s most varied and diverse. This is due to its being a melting pot of nationalities, as a result of centuries of European domination as well as slavery, which brought hordes of African migrants across Brazil’s borders to live in and influence the local cultures with their ancient customs and ideas. The European settlers also brought ideas, innovations and belief systems with them, shaping the local societies significantly. All of these different influences have meant that the modern-day Brazilian culture is unique and very complex.

At present, Brazil has a population of about 190 million people. Of these, more than half are white (which includes Portuguese, Italian, Polish etc... individuals), just fewer than 40% are mixed black and white and less than 10% are black.

Approximately 80% of the population ascribes to the Roman Catholic faith. This is due to the intense Portuguese occupation of centuries ago. These European settlers taught the indigenous tribes Catholicism, built churches and established traditions and customs that originated in this church.

Also due to the mass Portuguese settlements during the 16th, 17th and 18th centuries, this language is the official language of Brazil. There are small numbers of indigenous people and immigrants who still speak their own tongues, but these are certainly amng the vast minority.

Brazilians, as a nation, focus much importance on the family structure and the values that are entrenched within that institution. Families are usually large, and even extended family mmbers are close with one another, providing much-needed help and support to each other whenever and however necessary.
