In calculus, we study an operation on functions like f(x) called integration denoted [tex] \sf{ \displaystyle{ \sf\int f(x) \: dx.}}[/tex] Let a and k represent real numbers, then freshmen calculus students learn two rules of integration.First, [tex] \displaystyle{ \sf{ \int(f(x) + g(x)) \: dx = \int f(x) \: dx + \int g(x) \: dx}}.[/tex]Second, [tex]\displaystyle{\sf{ \int \dfrac{k}{x + a} \: \: dx = k \: \: In |x + a| .}}[/tex]Use these two facts to perform the integration below[tex] \quad \qquad \bullet \quad\displaystyle{\sf{\int \dfrac{2x - 1}{x^2 - x - 6} \: dx}}[/tex]​


FUNDAMENTAL THEOREM OF CALCULUS

[tex] \qquad \qquad \sf{=========================}[/tex]

[tex]{\underline{\huge \mathbb{ A} {\large \mathrm { NSWER : }}}} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \qquad \large \boxed{ \bold{ \: \ln |x+2|+\ln |x-3|+\text{C} \: }}\\[/tex]

[tex]\sf{=========================}[/tex]

[tex]{\underline{\huge \mathbb{S} {\large \mathrm { OLUTION: }}}} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \textsf{Fundamental Theorem of Calculus} : [/tex]

[tex]\quad\underline{\boxed{ \bold{\displaystyle \bold{\int \text{f}(x)\:\text{d}x=\text{F}(x)+\text{C} \iff \text{f}(x)=\dfrac{\text{d}}{\text{d}x}(\text{F}(x))}}}} \quad \bigstar \\ [/tex]

If differentiating takes you from one function to another, then integrating the second function will take you back to the first with a constant of integration.

[tex] \textsf{Given indefinite integral:}[/tex]

[tex] \qquad \qquad \mapsto \: \underline{ \boxed{ \bold{ \: \displaystyle \bold{ \int \dfrac{2x-1}{x^2-x-6}\: \text{d}x \: }}}} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \textsf{Factor the denominator:}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned} \sf\implies x^2-x-6 \: & \sf \: = x^2-3x+2x-6\\& \sf \: = x(x-3)+2(x-3)\\& \sf \: = (x+2)(x-3)\end{aligned} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies \displaystyle \sf \int \dfrac{2x-1}{x^2-x-6}\: \text{d}x=\int \dfrac{2x-1}{(x+2)(x-3)}\: \text{d}x \\ [/tex]

Take partial fractions of the given fraction by writing out the fraction as an identity:

[tex]\begin{aligned} \sf\implies \dfrac{2x-1}{(x+2)(x-3)} \: & \sf \equiv \dfrac{A}{x+2}+\dfrac{B}{x-3}\\\\ \sf\implies \dfrac{2x-1}{(x+2)(x-3)} \: & \sf \equiv \dfrac{A(x-3)}{(x+2)(x-3)}+\dfrac{B(x+2)}{(x+2)(x-3)}\\\\ \sf\implies 2x-1 \: & \sf \equiv A(x-3)+B(x+2)\end{aligned} \\ [/tex]

Calculate the values of A and B using substitution:

[tex]\textsf{When } \sf{x=-2 \implies -5 =A(-5)+B(0) \implies A=1}\\[/tex]

[tex]\textsf{When } \sf{x=3 \;\;\: \implies 5 =A(0)+B(5) \;\;\;\;\;\: \implies B=1} \\ [/tex]

Substitute the found values of A and B into the identity:

[tex] \sf\implies \dfrac{2x-1}{(x+2)(x-3)} \equiv \dfrac{1}{x+2}+\dfrac{1}{x-3} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \large \sf \pmb{Therefore:} [/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned}\implies \displaystyle \sf \int \dfrac{2x-1}{(x+2)(x-3)} \: \text{d}x \: & =\: \sf \int \dfrac{1}{x+2}+\dfrac{1}{x-3}\: \text{d}x\\\\ \: & =\: \sf \int \dfrac{1}{x+2} \: \text{d}x+ \int \dfrac{1}{x-3}\: \text{d}x\\\\ \: & = \: \underline{\bold{\ln |x+2|+\ln |x-3|+\text{C}}}\end{aligned} \\ [/tex]
