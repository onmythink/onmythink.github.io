7. How Modern technology can be a tool for empowerment in art production?​


Modern Technology and Art Production

Modern technology refers to the upgraded or advanced products, services, and infrastructure we experience in the modern era.

Art production, in general, refers to creating artistic works. It could be traditional or digital, a painting, or a live performance.

Question

We are asked to answer this question: "How Modern technology can be a tool for empowerment in art production?"

Answer

         Modern technology has played a significant role in boosting the arts. Based on a study done by the Pew Research Center's Internet & American Life Project from May 30 to July 20, 2012, several arts groups assert this idea as well. Traditionalism or modernity can be seen in artistic works. In any case, we cannot deny that the modern technology that is readily available now has a direct or indirect impact on both groups' performance.

         The majority of art techniques and instruments are works of technology. The cameras, video editing software, and audio editing software (for music). In the fashion industry, designers integrated technology and arts to design unique dresses such as the spray paint silk dress during the Coperni PFW runway in 2022. Constant advertising on social media platforms, which encouraged many individuals to pursue careers in the arts, particularly during the epidemic, may also be to reason for the enormous growth of artists from all over the world. Recently, there has been a growth in the production of fascinating art using AI technology. You only need a few words to create a powerful piece of digital art! Digital and artistic integration opens up a variety of alternatives for everyone throughout the world. Indeed, technology and the arts are inextricably linked.

         However, others claim that technology has a detrimental influence on artistic performances as well. For instance, the abrupt ringing of cell phones might interfere with a live performance. Some innovative performances become viral but don't always get good feedback, which might undermine the artist's reputation. As a result, social media has developed into a hub for criticism on this subject. Many people might opt to watch the recorded footage that has been uploaded online rather than attending the in-person performance. It's possible that spectators would want more "intense" shows using advanced technologies, which might put the performers and staff at risk. Technology may also be used to restrict access to many forms of art unless a fee is paid.

         We could expand the arts and generate more distinctive ideas for future use if we employ technology properly. We cannot, however, ignore the possibility that it may potentially have unfavorable effects on both artists and spectators. But it is our responsibility as millennials to take the initiative and dismantle barriers by highlighting the benefits of the arts. Let's be enthusiastic to unearth new artistic and technological gems.

Reference

Wormald, B. (2019, December 31). Section 6: Overall impact of technology on the arts. Pew Research Center: Internet, Science &amp; Tech. Retrieved December 10, 2022, from https :// www .pewresearch. org/internet/2013/01/04/section-6-overall-impact-of-technology-on-the-arts/

24. what are the advantages and disadvantages of the traditional and modern laboratory tools


Advantages:

The benefit of conventional laboratory instruments is that they have a lower chance of breakage.

The value of modern laboratory instruments is that the mixture can be seen in full and that scientists can completely understand it.

Disadvantages:

The downside to modern laboratory equipment is that it's glass, and it can be destroyed quickly by simply dropping it.

The downside of conventional laboratory equipment is that, because it is not glass, the mixture can not be completely seen from outside.
