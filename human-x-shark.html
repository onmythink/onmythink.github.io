Learning Task 1: Symbiotic Interaction

Commensalism is an ecological interaction where one species gain benefits while those of the other species neither benefit nor are harmed. Mutualism describes the ecological interaction between two species where each species benefit from one another. Predation is an ecological interaction where a predator kills and eats its prey. Parasitism is a symbiotic relationship between species, where the parasite causes harm to its host. These relationships are called symbiotic interactions in the environment.
