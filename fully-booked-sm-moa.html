If I were asked to produce a thesis book, I would need to consult various sources to fully comply with the requirements. Here are three possible tertiary sources that could be helpful:

Academic Journals: These are peer-reviewed publications that contain research articles on various topics related to the thesis. Journals like ScienceDirect, JSTOR, and PLOS One can provide me with relevant and up-to-date information that I can use in my thesis.

Databases: Academic databases like ProQuest, EBSCO, and Google Scholar can provide me with access to a wide range of articles, books, and other sources related to my thesis topic. These databases can be used to search for relevant information, including primary and secondary sources.

Encyclopedias: While encyclopedias may not be as comprehensive as academic journals and databases, they can still provide valuable background information on a particular topic. 14. what is purpose of MOA?


Answer:

The purpose of an MOA is to have a written formal understanding of the agreement between parties. An MOA details the obligations and commitments of the parties and allocates and minimizes each party's risks. It can also be referred to as a contract and is legally binding.
