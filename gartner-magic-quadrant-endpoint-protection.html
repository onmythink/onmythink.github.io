Answer:

A circle can be represented by the equation (x - h)² + (y - k)² = r², where (h, k) is the center of the circle and r is the radius.

Since we know that the center of the circle is at the second quadrant, and the endpoints are (-4, 3) and (1, -2), we can use the midpoint formula to find the center of the circle which is

(h,k) = ((x1+x2)/2,(y1+y2)/2)

so the center of the circle is ( (1 - 4) /2, ( -2 - 3)/2) = (-1.5, -2.5)

Now we can use the distance formula to find the radius of the circle

r = √((x2-h)² + (y2-k)²)

so

r = √((1 - (-1.5))² + ((-2) - (-2.5))²) = √(2.5² + 0.5²) = √6.5 = 2.5

so the equation of the circle is

(x + 1.5)² + (y + 2.5)² = 2.5²

(x + 1.5)² + (y + 2.5)² = 6.25

That's the equation of a circle whose center is at (-1.5, -2.5) and has a radius of 2.5
