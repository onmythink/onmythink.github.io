Step-by-step explanation:

First, determine the amount of ground turkey that is used for the first 6 burgers by multiplying the number of burgers with the amount used.

        n = (3/8 lb)(6 burgers) = 2.25 pounds

Subtract this from the total amount of ground turkey by subtracting the answer from 3.

     W = 3 - 2.25 lbs

     W = 0.75 lbs

Divide W by 1/4 to determine the final answer,

      m = 0.75 lbs/ 1/4 = 3

13. A cook has 2 3/4 pounds of ground beef. How many quarter pound burgers can he make​


Problems Involving Fractions:

The cook can make 11 quarter pound burgers.

Solutions:

Given that the cook has 2 3/4 pounds of ground beef.

Remember that a quarter when expressed as a fraction is equivalent to 1/4.

Thus, to get the number of quarter pound burgers that can be made out of 2 3/4 pounds of ground beef, we will divide 2 3/4 by 1/4.

2 3/4 ÷ 1/4 = N

Express 2 3/4 as a single fraction or an improper fraction.

2 3/4 = (4)(2) + 3/ 4 = 11/4

Proceed with the division.

11/4 ÷ 1/4 = N

Get the reciprocal of 1/4 and multiply it with 11/4.

Reciprocal of 1/4 is 4/1.

11/4 x 4/1 = 44/4 = 11

Therefore, there are 11 quarter pound burgers that can be made from 2 3/4 pounds of ground beef.

PROBLEM SOLVING

Justin is having a barbeque to celebrate his upcoming birthday. He will be cooking burgers, which cost $5.75 per pound, and hot dogs, which cost $4.25 per pound. In total, he has $30 to spend on burgers and hotdogs. The following equation models the situation, in which b represents the pounds of burgers and h represents the pounds of hot dogs. (Solution Needed)

Solution :-

Given equation :

[tex] \qquad \quad \sf \implies \boxed{ \bold{ \: 5.75b + 4.25h = 30 \: }} \: \: \bigstar \\ [/tex]

Part (A)

To solve the equation for b, subtract 4.25h from both sides :

[tex] \sf\implies 5.75b + 4.25h -4.25h= 30-4.25h \\ [/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies 5.75b = 30-4.25h \\ [/tex]

Divide both sides by 5.75 :

[tex] \sf\implies \dfrac{5.75b}{5.75} = \dfrac{30-4.25h}{5.75} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies b= \dfrac{30-4.25h}{5.75} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies b= \dfrac{30}{5.75}-\dfrac{4.25h}{5.75} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies \bold{b=\dfrac{120}{23}-\dfrac{17}{23}h} \\ [/tex]

Part (B)

To solve the equation for h, subtract 5.75b from both sides :

[tex] \sf\implies 5.75b + 4.25h -5.75b= 30-5.75b \\ [/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies 4.25h = 30-5.75b \\ [/tex]

Divide both sides by 4.25 :

[tex] \sf\implies \dfrac{4.25h}{4.25} = \dfrac{30-5.75b}{4.25} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies h= \dfrac{30-5.75b}{4.25} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies h= \dfrac{30}{4.25}-\dfrac{5.75b}{4.25} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies \bold{h=\dfrac{120}{17}-\dfrac{23}{17}b} [/tex]
