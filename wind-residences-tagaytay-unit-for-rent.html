21. what other member of the solar system was witnessed by the residents of the United States? ​


Answer:

The discovery of pluto

Explanation:

Nearly eighty years ago an astronomer working at the Lowell Observatory in the United States made a discovery that would ultimately initiate a dramatic change in the way we look at our Solar System. The young astronomer was Clyde Tombaugh, an observing assistant working at the observatory made famous by the great astronomer Percival Lowell. Tombaugh was continuing the search for an elusive planet – planet X – that Lowell had believed (incorrectly) to be responsible for perturbing the orbits of Uranus and Neptune. 

Within a year, after spending numerous nights at the telescope exposing photographic plates and months tediously scanning them for signs of a planet, Tombaugh saw what he was looking for. At around 4pm on the afternoon of 18 February 1930 Tombaugh began comparing two plates taken in January that year showing a region in the constellation of Gemini. As he flicked from one plate to the other, trying to see if something moved slightly between the two (the tell-tale sign of the planet he was hunting), he spotted something. In one part of the frame a small object flitted a few millimetres as he switched between the two plates. Tombaugh had found his new planet! (Stern & Mitton, 2005)

28. Your current monthly share " s " for renting an apartment unit varies inversely as the number of occupants " n". If there are three students living in a unit and each student is sharing P2000 for the rent, how much share would each one pay, if two more students will join the group next month?.


The formula for inverse variation is: s * n = k, where s is the share that each person pays, n is the number of people, and k is a constant.

Since the three students currently living in the unit are paying a total of P2000 for rent, and there are 3 of them, we can set up the equation as follows: s * 3 = 2000.

Solving for s, we find that each student is currently paying s = 2000/3 = P667.

If two more students join the group next month, the total number of occupants will be 3 + 2 = 5.

Substituting this value for n into the equation, we get: s * 5 = 2000

Solving for s, we find that each student will have to pay s = 2000/5 = P400 per month.

Therefore, if two more students join the group, each student will have to pay P400 per month, which is P267 less than they are currently paying.


29. Kats has rented out 12 units of one- and two-bedroom apartments. The one-bedroom costs 3600 pesos to rent and the two-bedroom costs 4500 to rent out. All units were rented out to a total cost of 49,500 pesos. What is the number of one- and two-bedroom apartments rented out? Answer first the number of one-bedroom apartments, then the number of two-bedroom apartments that were rented out.


One-Bedroom unit: x
Two-bedroom unit: y

Equation (1):

x + y = 12
y = 12-x

Equation (2):

3,600x + 4,500y = 49,500

Solve the number of one-bedroom units by substitution:

3,600x + 4,500y = 49,500
3,600x + 4,500 (12 - x) = 49,500
3,600x -4,500x + 54,000 = 49,500
-900x = 49,500 - 54,000
900x/900 = 4,500/900
x =  5  ⇒  Number of units of One-bedroom

Solve for y, the two-bedroom unit:
y = 12-x
y = 12 - 5
y = 7  ⇒   Number of units of Two-bedroom

Check:

Number of Units:
x + y = 12
5 + 7 = 12
12 = 12 (True)

Rent amount:
3,600x + 4,500y = 49,500
3,600 (5) + 4,500 (7) = 49,500
18,000 + 31,500 = 49,500
49,500 = 49,500   (True)
