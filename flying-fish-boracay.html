SCIENTIFIC NAME: Exocoetidae

TYPE: Fish

DIET: Omnivore

GROUP NAME: School

SIZE: Up to 18 inches

Flying fish can be seen jumping out of warm ocean waters worldwide. Their streamlined torpedo shape helps them gather enough underwater speed to break the surface, and their large, wing-like pectoral fins get them airborne.

Flying fish are thought to have evolved this remarkable gliding ability to escape predators, of which they have many. Their pursuers include mackerel, tuna, swordfish, marlin, and other larger fish. For their sustenance, flying fish feed on a variety of foods, including plankton.

27. 1. How do coral reefs contribute to the survival of fishes? A. fishes fly in coral reefs B. fishes play in coral reefs C. fishes swim in coral reefs D. fishes get food from coral reefs​


1. How do coral reefs contribute to the survival of fishes?

C. fishes swim in coral reefs

Explanation:

As one of the organisms that live in seawater ecosystems, coral reefs come from a group of small animals shaped like tubes that are in symbiosis with a collection of algae. For life, the benefits of coral reefs are numerous. Coral reefs can be a good shelter for fish and marine animals. So do not be surprised, many fish and marine animals that live in coral reefs.

The existence of coral reefs can be a very beautiful natural wealth. In addition, coral reefs can also provide many benefits for life. Therefore, we must maintain its sustainability. Coral reefs provide a place to live, find food, and breed for various marine biota. Damage to coral reefs will directly affect the survival and sustainability of various animals and plants in the sea. Coral reefs are a place to live and breed various marine biota.

Coral reefs are a group of coral animals that have a symbiosis with a type of algae called zooxanthellae. Coral reefs belong to the phylum Cnidaria class Anthozoa which has tentacles. The Anthozoa class consists of two subclasses, namely Hexacorallia (or Zoantharia) and Octocorallia, both of which are distinguished by their origin in morphology and physiology. Coral colonies are formed by thousands of small animals called polyps. In its simplest form, corals consist of just one polyp which has a tube-like body shape with a mouth located at the top and is surrounded by tentacles.

However, in most species, one individual coral polyp will develop. into many individuals called colonies. These animals have unique shapes and various colors and can produce CaCO3. Coral reefs are habitats for various species of marine plants, marine animals, and other marine microorganisms that are not yet known.
