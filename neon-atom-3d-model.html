2. based on the quantum mechanical model of an atom, explain why 3d has higher energy than 3p​


Answer:

in the quantum-mechanical model of an atom, electrons in the same atom that have the same principal quantum number (n) or principal energy level are said to occupy an electron shell of the atom. Orbitals define regions in space where you are likely to find electrons.

Answer:

The energy of an electron depends on the effective nuclear charge, Zeff. Because Zeff is larger for the 3s electrons, they have a lower energy (are more stable) than the 3p, which, in turn, are lower in energy than the 3d.

5. neon (Ne) atoms present in neon lights do not combine to form Ne molecules but weakly attract to one another through​


Ne atoms do attract one another weakly through dispersion forces

The physical properties and the shape of a molecule are caused by forces between the molecules. There are different kinds of forces between molecules, such as;

Dipole-dipole forces: These happen when two polar molecules come together. HCl is one example.Forces between an ion and a polar molecule are called ion-dipole forces. For instance, NaCl dissolves in water because the sodium or chloride ions interact with the water molecule.Dispersion forces: It is the weakest interaction that non-polar molecules like diatomic gases ([tex]H_{2} , N_{2} , Cl_{2}[/tex], etc) and monoatomic gases have (He, Ar, etc.)Dipole forces that include dipoles: These happen when an ion is near a molecule that is not polar. For instance, the nitrate ion and the I2 molecule.Hydrogen bonding happens when a hydrogen atom bonds with an element that is more electronegative (N, O, F). For example HF molecules.

So, Ne atoms do have a weak pull on each other because of dispersion forces. Dispersion forces are the correct answer.

18. WILL MARK BRAINLIEST!1. How many moles of neon atoms are there in 2.0 x 10^23 atoms of neon?2. How many carbon atoms are there in 0.50 mol of C?3. How many hydrogen atoms are there in 4.0 mol of H2O?


Answer:

1. n Ne = 0.33 mol

2. N C = 3.0 × 10²³ atoms

3. N H = 4.8 × 10²⁴ atoms

Explanation:

1. How many moles of neon atoms are there in 2.0 x 10^23 atoms of neon?

Solve for the number of moles of Ne atoms.

n Ne = 2.0 × 10²³ atoms × (1 mol / 6.0221 × 10²³ atoms)

n Ne = 0.33 mol

2. How many carbon atoms are there in 0.50 mol of C?

Solve for the number of carbon atoms.

N C = 0.50 mol × (6.0221 × 10²³ atoms / 1 mol)

N C = 3.0 × 10²³ atoms

3. How many hydrogen atoms are there in 4.0 mol of H2O?

Solve for the number of atoms of H.

N H = 4.0 mol H2O × (2 mol H / 1 mol H2O) × (6.0221 × 10²³ H atoms / 1 mol H)

N H = 4.8 × 10²⁴ atoms

25. A mixture of helium and neon gases is held in a container at 2.4 atmospheres. If the mixture contains twice as many helium atoms as neon atoms, what is the partial pressure of helium?


Answer:

4.8 atm

Explanation:

The partial pressure of one individual gas within the overall mixtures according to Dalton's Law of Partial Pressure is described as  :

Pi=Ptotal xi

PHe = Ptotal xHe

According to the problem, the mixture contains twice as many Helium atoms as Neon atoms, so x=2

*atm= atmosphere

Given:

Ptotal =2.4 atm

xHe= 2

Solution:

PHe = PtotalxHe

PHe =(2.4)*2

PHe = 4.8 atm
