should only use quotations in your papers when you need to keep the original language used in your source. For instance, you may use a quote when you’re stating a definition or a statistic, when you’re analyzing the language of a novel, or when a source is worded particularly well. Other times, you will want to paraphrase, meaning you’ll put the author’s ideas in your own words.

Why Paraphrase?

· If your paper has too many quotes in it, it will be disjointed and hard to read. Paraphrasing will allow you to write a paper that flows smoothly.

· Paraphrasing also helps you take the time to develop a good understanding of the source you’re reading.

When you’re paraphrasing, you cannot just take the original sentence and plug in a few synonyms. Instead, you must fully rewrite the sentence with your own words and your own sentence structure. Taking another author’s wording or sentence structure without providing quotation marks is plagiarism even if you include an in-text citation at the end of your sentence.

Original Quote
