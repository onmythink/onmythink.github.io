8. base on the article how vulberable our PWDs in time of pandemic


Persons with disabilities (PWDs) living in cities during the COVID-19 pandemic response may be four times more likely to be injured or die than non-disabled persons, not because of their “vulnerable” position but because urban health policy, planning and practice has not considered their needs. In this article, the adverse health impacts on PWDs during the COVID-19 pandemic reveals the “everyday emergencies” in cities for PWDs and that these can be avoided through more inclusive community planning, a whole-of-government commitment to equal access, and implementation of universal design strategies. Importantly, COVID-19 can place PWDs at a higher risk of infection since some may already have compromised immune and respiratory systems and policy responses, such as social distancing, can lead to life-threatening disruptions in care for those that rely on home heath or personal assistants. Living in cities may already present health-damaging challenges for PWDs, such as through lack of access to services and employment, physical barriers on streets and transportation, and smart-city technologies that are not made universally accessible. We suggest that the current pandemic be viewed as an opportunity for significant urban health reforms on the scale of the sanitary and governance reforms that followed ninetieth century urban epidemics. This perspective offers insights for ensuring the twenty-first century response to COVID-19 focuses on promoting more inclusive and healthy cities for all

12. 1, What is Republic Act No. 10745 and what is it's purpose?2, Name some of the benefits and privileges provided to Filipino citizens with disabilities under this law.3, Which establishments offer a 20% discount to PWDs?4, What types of medical services are covered under the 20% discount?5, How many positions are private corporations with more than 100 employees required to reserve for PWDs?6, What is the purpose of express lanes in commercial and government establishments?7, What types of educational assistance are available to PWDs?8, How are PWDs protection against ridicule and vilification?9, What is the value of the benefits provide to qualified PhilHealth members or dependents in need of lower limb prosthesis?10, What is the role of designated public attorneys under this law in protecting the rights of PWDs?​


Answer:

1. Republic Act No. 10745, also known as the "Comprehensive law on the Protection of Children with Disabilities," is a law that aims to provide equal opportunities and full participation for children with disabilities by promoting their rights and welfare.

2. Some of the benefits and privileges provided to Filipino citizens with disabilities under this law include a 20% discount on medical and dental services, transportation fare, and admission fees to recreational centers and public museums. PWDs are also entitled to tax incentives and priority in express lanes.

3. Establishments such as hotels, restaurants, and recreational centers are required to offer a 20% discount to PWDs.

4. The 20% discount applies to medical and dental services, including diagnostic and laboratory fees, professional fees, and other healthcare-related expenses.

5. Private corporations with more than 100 employees are required to reserve at least 1% of their workforce for PWDs.

6. Express lanes in commercial and government establishments are intended to give PWDs priority and ease of access to services such as payment transactions, registration, and application processes.

7. Educational assistance for PWDs includes the provision of special education services, individualized education plans, and accessible learning materials.

8. PWDs are protected against ridicule and vilification through provisions that penalize individuals who engage in discriminatory behavior or make derogatory remarks towards PWDs.

9. The PhilHealth benefit package for PWDs in need of lower limb prosthesis is up to P270,000 per prosthesis, which covers the cost of the prosthesis and necessary medical procedures.

10. Designated public attorneys under this law are tasked with protecting the rights of PWDs and ensuring that their rights and welfare are not violated. They provide legal assistance to PWDs who are victims of abuse or discrimination and advocate for their rights in legal proceedings.
