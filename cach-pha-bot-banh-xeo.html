5. level1 cache is the smallest but the fastest among the different cache levels​. true or false


True

Cache is graded as Level 1 (L1), Level 2 (L2) and Level 3 (L3):

L1 is usually part of the CPU chip itself and is both the smallest and the fastest to access. Its size is often restricted to between 8 KB and 64 KB.

L2 and L3 caches are bigger than L1. They are extra caches built between the CPU and the RAM. Sometimes L2 is built into the CPU with L1. L2 and L3 caches take slightly longer to access than L1. The more L2 and L3 memory available, the faster a computer can run.

24. Explain why asteroids that cross Earth's orbit are called as PHA?​


POTENTIALLY HAZARDOUS ASTEROIDS

Potentially Hazardous Asteroid (PHA) are asteroids that can possibly harm planet Earth especially if it will come less than .05 AU or Astronomical Unit (AU refers to the measurement distance of Earth from the Sun) from our planet.

Asteroids that cross Earth's orbital path are called as Earth-crossers, and if an asteroid is near enough to harm our planet, it is then called as PHA or Potentially Hazardous Asteroid.

What is an asteroid?

Asteroids are one of the interstellar bodies that also revolves around the sun, they are relatively small to large chunks of celestial rocky bodies that have usually dark surfaces.
