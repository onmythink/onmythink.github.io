15. how do we apply the emboss filter?​


The emboss filter is used to create a 3D effect on an image by giving it the appearance of being raised or indented. The filter can be applied in image editing software such as Adobe Photoshop.

To apply the emboss filter in Photoshop, follow these steps:

1. Open the image you want to apply the filter to in Photoshop.

2. Go to the "Filter" menu and select "Stylize" and then "Emboss."

3. In the Emboss dialog box, you can adjust the angle, height, and amount of the emboss effect.

4. Click "OK" to apply the filter to the image.

Note: The process of applying emboss filter may vary depending on the image editing software you are using.

1-5Woodturning

Woodturning lathes are commonly used to create cylindrical profiles out of wood. Furniture legs, lamp posts, baseball bats, bowls, and other ornamental forms are among the products created on a wood lathe.

Hand carving

To turn a raw piece of wood into a finished piece of furniture, carvers use a variety of procedures and tools. Chisels, knives, gouges, and hammers are the most common carving tools, coupled with sanding, painting, or other procedures to properly finish work.

Pyrography

Pyrography has been practiced for hundreds of years. It's an age-old method that involves burning wood and other materials with a heated metal pen, leaving a colorful pattern behind. Pyrography, sometimes known as woodburning, is a great way for experienced sketchers to experiment with a new medium.

Staining

Staining is a technique for enhancing contrast in material, usually on a tiny scale. Histology, as well as the medical sciences of histopathology, hematology, and cytopathology, which focus on the study and diagnosis of disease at a microscopic level, regularly use stains and dyes.

Gilding

Gilding is a technique for giving an object a gold finish for a fraction of the cost of actual gold. Furthermore, a solid gold piece would be too delicate or heavy for everyday use. In addition, unlike silver, a gilded surface does not tarnish.

1-5Engraving

Engraving is an intaglio printmaking technique that involves cutting lines into a metal plate to hold the ink. The plate for engraving might be composed of copper or zinc. Only the purposeful lines will be printed once the metal plate has been polished to remove all scratches and defects.

Etching

Etching is the technique of cutting into the unprotected sections of a metal surface using strong acid or mordant to create an intaglio design in the metal. Other chemicals may be employed on different sorts of materials in modern manufacturing.

Texturing and Embossing

First, embossing or texturing your custom plastic extrusion improves its visual or functional appeal. Second, Form tech ensures that your pattern is constant in appearance and feel throughout each production run.

Adding metal enhancing media

Metal enhancing media, such as metal paints, Copic markers, and antiquing kits, can be used to give the metal product color. Metal paints can be brushed on or sprayed on.
