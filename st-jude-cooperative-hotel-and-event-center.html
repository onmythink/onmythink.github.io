DUMAGUETE CITY – The Negros Oriental Inter-Agency Task Force for the Management of Emerging Infectious Diseases (IATF-EID) is mulling measures against health workers who refuse to receive the vaccine for the coronavirus disease 2019 (Covid-19).

Assistant Provincial Health Officer Dr. Liland Estacion, Covid-19 incident commander of the provincial IATF, in a virtual press briefing Tuesday said the task force will meet soon to discuss possible steps to be taken against these health workers.

“I will announce later if there is a resolution from the PIATF after the medical committee has met,” she said in mixed English and Cebuano dialect.

Estacion said the total number of unvaccinated health workers in the province is 6,670 but it could not be ascertained how many of them decided not to be inoculated.

As of Tuesday, a total of 9,081 medical front-liners received the first dose, and 3,729 for the second dose.

“To our health workers, how can we serve our constituents if we have not been vaccinated; because if not, we might be called super-spreaders,” she said.

Expressing frustration over the slow vaccine rollout in the different local government units (LGUs), Estacion also said the PIATF has asked for reports for consolidation before another meeting that would also tackle the province’s dilemma on how to speed up the vaccination process.

The rural health units and city and municipal health offices and hospitals in the LGUs are very slow in the administration of the vaccines, and some of them have even failed to come and pick up their respective allocations, she noted.

“Our consumption rate is so slow and therefore we are also getting lesser vaccine deliveries here compared to other areas,” she said.

Negros Oriental is now slowly moving to the A2 priority sector comprising senior citizens, Estacion said.

As of Tuesday, a total of 133,842 senior citizens have been master-listed, with 187 inoculated.

Estacion has given the go-signal to skip the health workers who continue to refuse the Covid-19 jabs and move on to the senior citizens who seem to be more receptive of the vaccine. (PNA)
