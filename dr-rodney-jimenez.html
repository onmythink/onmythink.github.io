5. Mrs. Jimenez paid P336.00 for 3½ dozen of eggs. How much would 2 dozens of such eggs cost?​


Answer:

P54

Step-by-step explanation:

Mrs. Jimenez paid P94.50 for 3 1/2 dozen of eggs.

A dozen is equal to 12 eggs.

Half a dozen is equal to 6 eggs. (12/2 = 6)

Three dozen is equal 36 eggs. (12 x 3 = 36)

Three and a haft (3 1/2) dozen is equal to 42 eggs. (36 + 6 = 42)

How much would 2 dozen of egg cost?

To get the cost of the 2 dozen of eggs, simply divide the amount Mrs. Jimenez paid by the number of eggs she bought which is 3 1/2 dozen or 42 eggs.

94.50 / 42 = 2.25

Each egg costs P2.25

To get the price of 2 dozen of eggs or 24 eggs, simply multiply the number of eggs by the cost per egg which is P2.25

24 x 2.25 = 54

Two dozen of eggs costs P54
