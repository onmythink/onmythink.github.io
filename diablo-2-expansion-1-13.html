In binomial

(x+y)^n

the expansion is in the form of

[tex]a_0x^n+a_1x^{n-1}y+a_2x^{n-1}y^2..+a_{n-1}xy^{n-1}+a_ny^n[/tex]

[tex] \\ [/tex]

Looking for the 9th term , given

(1+2x)^10

n = 10 , so we will focus for the term

Let x = 1 , y = 2x

[tex]a_nxy^{n-1}[/tex][tex]a_{10}(1)(2x)^{10-1}[/tex][tex]a_{10}(1)(2x)^9[/tex][tex]a_{10}512x^9[/tex]

[tex] \\ [/tex]

To find the coefficient , Use the combination formula

k = 9 and n = 10

[tex]_nC^k = 10! / 9!(n-1)![/tex][tex]_{10}C^9 = 10! / 9!(10-9)![/tex][tex]_{10}C^9 = 10! / 9!(1)![/tex][tex]_{10}C^9 = 10×9! / 9![/tex][tex]_{10}C^9 = 10[/tex]
