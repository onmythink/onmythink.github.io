10. What can you infer about graphite from the fact that you can scratch it with just your fingernail?A.) It's not very lustrous.B.) It's not very hardC.) Its streak is black coloredD.) It has fracture.​


QUESTION

What can you infer about graphite from the fact that you can scratch it with just your fingernail?

A.) It's not very lustrous.

B.) It's not very hard

C.) Its streak is black colored

D.) It has fracture.

ANSWER

Among the given choices above the correct answer is letter B.) It's not very hard.

This is because graphite is a kind of mineral that is soft compared to other kinds of mineral, this is the reason why graphite can be easily scratched by fingernails, even though fingernails are relatively soft.

Also, the hardness of a mineral is tested by scratching, and hardness of graphite is less than one on the Mohs scale.

What is the difference between cutting and scratching technique?

Cuts and scratches are areas of damage on the surface of the skin. A cut is a line of damage that can go through the skin and into the muscle tissues below, whereas a scratch is surface damage that does not penetrate the lower tissues.

A cut or laceration penetrates the skin, which is about 1/8 inch (3 mm) thick. Cuts that gape with or without movement may need closure to prevent scarring. A scratch or scrape does not break all the way through the top layer of the skin.I hope it helps...ㆁωㆁThank you
