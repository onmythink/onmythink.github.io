10. Montealegre family has a catering business. In one birthday party, they charged their customer Php 11,767.50 for a party of 90 persons and a service charge of Php 2,500.50. How much was the average cost per person?


About the Story Relating to Averages

From this it is understood that the catering bill for 90 people is Php 11,767.50, and the service fee for 90 people is Php 2,500.50, so

behind:

Total Bill (x)=Php 11,767.50

Service Fee (y)=Php 2,500.50

Number of people (n)=90.

Asked:

Average cost per person (x)=?

Answer:

Average billing cost per person=11,767.50/90=130.75.

Average service cost per person=Php 2,500.50/90=27.79 (rounded amount).

So the average billing cost per person is 130.75+27.79=158.54

Or the average cost per person is:

x =(x+y)/n

x =(11.767.50+2.50.50)/90=14.268.00/90=158.54

So the bill for all these costs is Php 14,268.00 which is charged to the customer.

So that the party was attended by 90 people, so if there is a bill for each person who comes Php 158.54 and this value has been rounded up.

As for the details, if the overall food bill is Php 11,767.50 then each person pays for their own meal at Php 130.75. The gain from Php 11,767.50 is divided by 90 people.

And it turns out that the catering also doesn't give free service fees, they total everything for 90 people for Php 2,500.50. So if this fee is charged to each individual at the party, then each service is Php 27.79 with a value that has been rounded up.

So the burden of each person at the party is the amount of the food bill per person and the amount of service per person which is Php 130.75 + Php 27.79=Php 158.54.

It is a way of finding the average load for individual parties separately. You can find it directly using the formula above, which is to add up all billing and service costs and then divide by 90 people.
