4. A person with mass of 60 kg standing on the dwarf planet Ceres would weigh 16.2 N. What is the strength of gravity on the surface of Ceres? ​


✎The Problem:

A person with mass of 60 kg standing on the dwarf planet Ceres would weigh 16.2 N. What is the strength of gravity on the surface of Ceres?

✎The Concept:

Weight is the amount of gravitational force exerted by a celestial body on an object with mass. The formula can be expressed as:

Weight = Mass × Gravity

✎The Solution:

Given that

Weight: 16.2 NMass: 60 kgGravity: ?

The problem involves modifying the formula for weight. It can now be expressed as:

Gravity = Weight / Mass

So:

Gravity = 16.2 N / 60 kg

Gravity = 0.27 m/s²

Cerebus = 40 km/hr

Minibus = 60 km/hr

Step-by-step explanation:

minibus

d = 150

r = x+20

ceres bus

d = 100

r = x

Since they have the same time,

[tex]t=\frac{d}{r}[/tex]

[tex]\frac{150}{x+20} =\frac{100}{x}[/tex]

cross multiply,

150x = 100(x+20)

150x = 100x + 2000

Transpose,

50x = 2000

x = 40

minibus = x+20 = 40 +20 = 60

Therefore,

Cerebus = 40 km/hr
