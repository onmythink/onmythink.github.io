Answer:

Introduction of Davao del Sur:

Davao del Sur is a province located in the southern part of the Philippines, specifically in the Davao Region. It is known for its rich natural resources, diverse culture, and vibrant local economy. The provincial capital is Digos City, and the province is bordered by Davao City to the north, Davao Occidental to the south, Sultan Kudarat and South Cotabato to the west, and the Davao Gulf to the east. Davao del Sur is home to various indigenous tribes, including the Bagobo, Manobo, and B'laan, who contribute to the province's cultural heritage.

Types of Rocks in Davao del Sur:

Davao del Sur has a diverse geological landscape, resulting in a variety of rock types found in the province. Some of the common rock types in Davao del Sur include:

Sedimentary rocks: These are formed from the accumulation of sediments, such as sand, mud, and organic materials, which over time become compacted and cemented. Sedimentary rocks found in Davao del Sur include sandstone, shale, and limestone.

Igneous rocks: These are formed from the solidification of molten materials, such as magma or lava. Igneous rocks found in Davao del Sur include basalt, andesite, and diorite.

Metamorphic rocks: These are formed from the transformation of existing rocks through heat, pressure, and chemical reactions. Metamorphic rocks found in Davao del Sur include schist, gneiss, and marble.

Kinds of Minerals in Davao del Sur: Davao del Sur is also known for its mineral resources, which contribute to its economic growth and development. Some of the common minerals found in Davao del Sur include:

Gold: Davao del Sur is known for its gold reserves, with several small-scale and large-scale mining operations in the province. Gold is commonly found in quartz veins and alluvial deposits in the rivers and streams of Davao del Sur.

Copper: Copper deposits can also be found in Davao del Sur, with some mining operations extracting copper ores from the province.

Coal: Davao del Sur has coal deposits, particularly in the municipalities of Malita and Kiblawan. Coal is used as a source of energy and is utilized for both domestic and industrial purposes.

Natural Resources of Davao del Sur: Apart from rocks and minerals, Davao del Sur is also rich in other natural resources that contribute to the province's economy. Some of the natural resources found in Davao del Sur include:

Forest resources: Davao del Sur has vast forest areas that are home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. These forests are a source of timber, non-timber forest products, and provide habitat for various wildlife species.

Agricultural resources: Davao del Sur has fertile lands suitable for agriculture, with crops such as rice, corn, coconut, banana, and durian being major agricultural products of the province. Fishing and aquaculture are also important economic activities due to the province's proximity to the Davao Gulf.

Water resources: Davao del Sur is endowed with rivers, lakes, and springs that provide water for irrigation, domestic use, and hydropower generation. The province also has several hydroelectric power plants that contribute to the region's energy needs.

22. mrs vino ordered decorative candles worth 6 800 00 after the discount the cost was reduced to 6 120 00 what was the rate of discount


Answer:

10 percent.

Step-by-step explanation:

To know the rate of discount given to Mrs. Vino, we have to get first the total percentage of the amount paid by Mrs. Vino by dividing the reduced cost to the original cost of the decorative candles. After getting the answer, we must subtract it from 100 %.

Solution:

[tex]\frac{6, 120.00}{2, 800.00}[/tex] × 100 = 90%

100% - 90% = 10%

Rate of Discount:

Answer:

The rate of discount is 10% or ten percent.

Step-by-step explanation:

To solve for the rate of discount, compute first for the amount discounted by subtracting the discounted price with the original price of the candles such that 6 800 00 - 6 120 00 = 680 00. If the amount discounted is 680 00, divide this by the original price of the candles to get the rate of discount such that 680 00/6 8000 00 = 0.1. To change this into percentage, multiply it by 100 then 0.1 x 100% = 10%. Therefore, the rate of discount is 10%.
