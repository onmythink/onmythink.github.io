8. The town of Brookfield sent out a survey to its residents to determine what new landmark they should build in the City Center. Of the residents who were surveyed 2/5, of the residents voted for a park, 1/4 of the residents voted for a garden, and the remaining residents voted for a zoo. If a total of 14,000 residents were surveyed, how many residents voted for a zoo?


The proportion of residents who voted for a park is 2/5, and the proportion who voted for a garden is 1/4. Therefore, the proportion of residents who voted for a zoo is:

1 - (2/5 + 1/4) = 1 - (8/20 + 5/20) = 1 - (13/20) = 7/20

If 7/20 of the residents voted for a zoo, and a total of 14,000 residents were surveyed, the number of residents who voted for a zoo is:

(7/20) x 14,000 = 4,900

Therefore, 4,900 residents voted for a zoo.
