There are different types of physician. They are categorized based on their specialization. Based on the pictures, below are the explanation of different types of physician.

1. Cardiologists

Cardiologists specialize in the diagnosis, treatment, and management of cardiovascular disorders, such as arrhythmias, hypertension, and hyperlipidemia. Despite the fact that they are medical specialists, they may concentrate on interventional procedures in their practice.

2. Pediatricians

Doctors that specialize in treating children, adolescents, and infants diagnose and treat a wide range of illnesses and injuries. Pediatricians diagnose common children illnesses like asthma, allergies, and croup in addition to practicing preventive medicine.

3. Obstetricians and gynecologists

Women's reproductive systems are treated medically by obstetricians and gynecologists (OB/GYNs).

4. Pulmonologist

A pulmonologist is a physician who specializes in the respiratory system.

5. Geriatricians

Geriatricians are primary care doctors who have additional training in treating older adults, especially those 65 and up.

6. Neurologist

Neurologists are physicians who diagnose and treat diseases of the nervous system, brain, spinal cord, peripheral nerves, muscles, and blood vessels.

7. Urologists

A urologist is a physician who focuses on disorders of the reproductive system and conditions that affect men, women, and children's urinary tracts.

8. Dermatologist

Dermatologists are expert medical doctors and skin surgeons with the unique skills and experience to offer the best care for the organ that cares for you.

9. Opthalmologist

An ophthalmologist is a medical or osteopathic doctor who specializes in eye and vision care.

10. Gastroenterologist

A gastroenterologist is a doctor who treats digestive disorders.

25. diagnostic explanation​


Answer:

A diagnostic test is any approach used to gather clinical information for the purpose of making a clinical decision (i.e., diagnosis). Some examples of diagnostic tests include X-rays, biopsies, pregnancy tests, medical histories, and results from physical examinations.

26. responsibilities of the physician​


Answer:

Physicians examine patients, take medical histories, prescribe medications, and order, perform, and interpret diagnostic tests. They often counsel patients on diet, hygiene, and preventive healthcare.

Physician work to care for patients in a variety of way, from treating chronic illnesses to advising about preventative health care.


27. physician recommendation​


Physician Recommendation

- Physician recommendations are frequently treated as binary, in which a physician either does or does not recommend a single treatment option. In reality, however, treatment recommendations are often more nuanced, and physicians can provide recommendations of varying strength for multiple treatments.

29. diagnostic in health​


Answer:

The process of identifying a disease, condition, or injury from its signs and symptoms. A health history, physical exam, and tests, such as blood tests, imaging tests, and biopsies, may be used to help make a diagnosis.


30. diagnostic process​


The steps of the diagnostic process fall into three broad categories: Initial Diagnostic Assessment – Patient history, physical exam, evaluation of the patient's chief complaint and symptoms, forming a differential diagnosis, and ordering of diagnostic tests.
