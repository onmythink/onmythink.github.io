Answer:

To find out how many vests the seamstress can make, we need to divide the total number of yards by the amount of material required for each vest.

15 yards ÷ 5/8 yard per vest = 15 * 8/5 = 24 vests

So, the seamstress can make 24 vests from the 15 yards of material.

We know the seamstress has 15 yards of material, so the equation we need to solve is:

5/8 * x = 15

To solve for x, we'll divide both sides of the equation by 5/8:

x = 15 / (5/8)

To simplify the fraction on the right side, we'll multiply both the numerator and denominator by 8/5:

x = 15 * (8/5) / (5/8) * (8/5)

x = 15 * (8/5) / (5/8) * (8/5)

x = 120 / 5

x = 24

So the seamstress can make 24 vests from the 15 yards of material.
