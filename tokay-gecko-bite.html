21. What type of asexual reproduction is leopard gecko regrowing its tail?


Answer:

Some lizards (such as the leopard gecko) can grow new tails when they lose them (autotomy). One great example of regeneration in living organisms is the Planarium, a type of flatworm which scientists love to study.

Reproduce asexually

Some species of gecko reproduce asexually, when the female produces fertile eggs without mating with a male. All geckos, except some species found in New Zealand, lay eggs. Some species lay one egg in each clutch while others lay two. Eggs are deposited under rocks, tree bark, and even behind window shutters.
