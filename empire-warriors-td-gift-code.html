1. The code of conduct for Japanese warrior classes


Answer:

Bushido

Bushido was the code of conduct for Japan's warrior classes from perhaps as early as the eighth century through modern times. The word "bushido" comes from the Japanese roots "bushi" meaning "warrior," and "do" meaning "path" or "way." It translates literally to "way of the warrior."

18. O and A are the midpoint TD and DY respectivelywhich of the statement is true ?a.DA=AY c.OD=ADb.OA=TY b.OD=TD​


The statement that is true is:

b. OA = TY

This is because O and A are the midpoint of TD and DY respectively, so:

OA = (OD + AY)/2

But we also know that AY = DY, so:

OA = (OD + DY)/2

And since TY is also the midpoint of TD, we know that:

TD = 2 * TY

Therefore:

OD + DY = TD

Substituting this into the previous equation, we get:

OA = TD/2 = TY

So, OA = TY, which is option b.
