2. What is the angular velocity of an object traveling in a circle of radius 0.75m with a linear speed of 3.5 m/s?A.) 4.3 rd/sB.) 4.8 rd/sC.) 4.9 rd/sD.) 4.7 rd/s


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & r = \text{0.75 m} \\ & v = \text{3.5 m/s} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the angular velocity.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \omega & = \frac{v}{r} \\ & = \frac{\text{3.5 m/s}}{\text{0.75 m}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{4.7 rd/s}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the answer is D.) 4.7 rd/s.

9. What is the linear speed of a child on a merry-go-round of radius 3.0 m that has an angular velocity of 4.0 rd/s?A.) 0.75 m/sB.) 10 m/sC.) 12 m/sD.) 13.0 m/s


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & r = \text{3.0 m} \\ & \omega = \text{4.0 rd/s} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the linear speed.

[tex]\begin{aligned} v & = \omega r \\ & = \text{(4.0 rd/s)(3.0 m)} \\ & = \boxed{\text{12 m/s}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the answer is C.) 12 m/s.

21. Activity 4: Prove YourselfProve by Converse of the Hinge Theorem. RGiven: ∠RSD ≅ ∠RDS, > SProve: m∠DRT > m∠SRT ​


Answer:

Solution:

From diagram,

<RSD = <RDS

DT> ST and DR = SR

RT = RT (common side

Now by using hinge theorem,

If two sides of one triangle are congruent, respectively, to two sides of a second triangle, and the included angle of the first triangle is larger than the included angle of the second, then the third side of the first triangle is longer than the third side of the second.

So DT > ST

Then <DRT = <SRT
