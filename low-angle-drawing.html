Step-by-step explanation:

Having drawn the situation, we will see that is an example of a word problem involving trigonometric functions.

In the illustration we will see that a right triangle is formed where the given angle is 30 degrees. The adjacent side of the angle is 1.2 miles. In the problem, we are looking for the opposite side of the angle. Therefore, we need to use the formula for tangent.

tan (angle) = [tex]\frac{opposite}{adjacent}[/tex]

However, since we are looking for the opposite side, we need to derive it from the tangent formula. We will now get:

opposite = tan (angle) × adjacent

The only thing left to do is to substitute the given in our formula.

tan 30° × 1.2 mi ≈  0.693 mi (rounder off to the nearest thousandths)
