23. What would be the parent's genotype for color, if a black offspring were produced from two white sheep parent?​


If we’re assuming simple Mendelian genetics, the two parent sheep would have to be heterozygous for their genotype. In this case, the white color would be dominant to black (parents are then, let’s say, Rr for color). You can think of it like each parent has only one allele to give (either the R or the r). If both happen to give the r, then the offspring would be black.

The probably of the next offspring being black would be 25 percent. If you fill out a punnett square for this problem, there would be a 25 percent chance of a homozygous dominant offspring, a 50 percent chance of a heterozygous offspring, and a 25 percent chance of a homozygous recessive offspring. In this case, white is the dominant allele so the heterozygous offspring will be white, along with the homozygous dominant, which makes up 75 percent of the possibilities. The remaining 25 percent is black since both recessive alleles are needed to show the recessive trait.

Usually real life genetics is much more complicated but this is a good way to understand how it works on a basic level.

It is easier to see on the Punnett square.

25. A black sheep is bred with a white sheep. All of their offsprings have a phenotype of gray coat. What is the most likely pettern of inheritance for coats colour in sheep


Answer:

Based on the information provided, the most likely pattern of inheritance for coat color in sheep is incomplete dominance.

Incomplete dominance is a pattern of inheritance where the heterozygous offspring show a phenotype that is intermediate between the two homozygous parents. In this case, the black sheep would be homozygous for the dominant allele for black coat color (BB), and the white sheep would be homozygous for the recessive allele for white coat color (bb).

When these two individuals are crossed, their offspring are all heterozygous (Bb) and show an intermediate phenotype of gray coat color, which is a blend of black and white. This suggests that the allele for black coat color is not completely dominant over the allele for white coat color, but rather the two alleles have a partially additive effect on the phenotype.

Therefore, incomplete dominance is the most likely pattern of inheritance for coat color in sheep based on the given information.
