Answer:

The transformation of energy from a tanning bed to a nuclear power plant is an example of electrical energy being transformed into thermal energy and then into electrical energy again. The tanning bed produces ultraviolet light which is transformed into heat energy that warms up the skin. In a nuclear power plant, the heat generated by nuclear reactions is used to produce steam, which then drives turbines to generate electricity.
