[tex]\red{••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••}[/tex]

[tex]\underline{\mathbb{ANSWERS:}}[/tex]

[tex]\qquad\Large\rm» \:\: a. \: \green{\overline{EN}=31\:units}[/tex]

[tex]\qquad\Large\rm» \:\: b. \: \: \green{x=4}[/tex]

[tex]\red{••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••}[/tex]

[tex]\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTIONS:}}[/tex]

- One theorem suggests that the length of the median in the trapezoid that is parallel to the bases is half the sum of the length of its bases.

[tex]RS = \frac{TO+EN}{2} \\ [/tex]Option A.

[tex]28 = \frac{25+EN}{2} \\ [/tex]

[tex]28 \cdot2 = \frac{25+EN}{ \cancel2} \cdot \cancel2 \\ [/tex]

[tex]56 = 25 + EN[/tex]

[tex]56 - 25 = EN[/tex]

[tex]31 = EN[/tex]

[tex]\therefore[/tex] The length of the segment EN is 31 units.

[tex]\rm[/tex]

Option B.

[tex]17 = \frac{(3x + 4) + (5x - 2)}{2} \\ [/tex]

[tex]17 = \frac{3x + 4+ 5x - 2}{2} \\ [/tex]

[tex]17 = 4x + 1[/tex]

[tex] \frac{16}{4} = \frac{ \cancel4x}{ \cancel4} \\ [/tex]

[tex]\therefore[/tex] The value of x is 4.
