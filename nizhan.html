11. Fred is 4 times as old as Nathan and is also 27 years older than Nathan. Let f be Fred's age and let n be Nathan's age.


Answer:

Fred is 36 years old and Nathan is 9 years old.

Step-by-step explanation:

Let's find first Nathan's age:

[tex]4n = n + 27 \\ \\ 4n - n = 27 \\ \\ 3n = 27 \\ \\ \frac{3n}{3} = \frac{27}{3} \\ \\ n = 9[/tex]

Now, for Fred's age:

[tex]f = 4n \\ \\ f = 4(9) \\ \\ f = 36[/tex]

Fred = 36

Nathan = 9

13. Explanation of Enabling Michanisms


Enabling mechanisms are tools, processes, or systems that facilitate the achievement of a goal or objective. They provide the necessary support and resources to enable individuals or organizations to perform tasks or activities more effectively and efficiently.

In the context of organizational change, for example, enabling mechanisms might include training programs, communication systems, or performance management systems that help employees adapt to new ways of working. In the context of technology adoption, enabling mechanisms might include user manuals, technical support, or online tutorials that help users learn how to use new tools or software.

Overall, enabling mechanisms play a critical role in supporting the successful implementation of new initiatives or changes by providing the necessary resources and support to help individuals or organizations achieve their goals.

Explanation:

Nathan traveled for 45 minutes (from 6:30 to 7:15), and if he was traveling at a speed of 15 kilometers per minute, we can find the total distance he covered using the formula:

20. Fred is 4 times as old as Nathan and is also 27 years older than Nathan. Let f be Fred's age and let n be Nathan's age. Which system of equations represents this situation?


Answer:

Linear equation represents the problem and the system of equations can be solved through substitution method

Step-by-step explanation:

Let f be Fred's age

n be Nathan's age

f = 4n -eqn 1

f = n + 27 -eqn 2

The problem represents linear equations. On this case you can use substitution method in solving the system of equations

substitute equation 2 to 1

n + 27 = 4n

27 = 4n - n

27 = 3n

n = 27/3

n = 9

Substitute n=9 to either equation 1 or 2

f = 4(9)

f = 36

or

f = 9 + 27

f = 36
