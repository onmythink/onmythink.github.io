12. a magnet is strongest at​


Answer:

The main strength of a magnet is at the poles, the North and South poles, which lie at each end of a magnet, or each side of a circular magnet.

Explanation:

The magnetic field generated by any magnet is always strongest at either pole. The magnetic force is equally as strong at both the north and south pole.

What this indicates is that the magnetic field varies strongly over a short distance compared to the variation closer to the middle of the magnet. Because the magnetic field changes so dramatically, a dipole feels more force
