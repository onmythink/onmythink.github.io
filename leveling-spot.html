1. a multi level parking lot has 6 levels and there a total of 1,327 parking sports.They are 162 parking spots on the first level. the rest of the parking spots are distributed equally on other 5 levels.How many parking spots are there on the top level? ​


Answer:

253

Step-by-step explanation:

Given that the multi-level parking lot has 1327 parking spots with 6 levels.

It is also given that there are 162 parking spots in the first level.

Then, the remaining five levels have a total of 1327 - 162 = 1265 parking spots left.

Since they are distributed equally, then they have 1265 / 5 = 253 parking spots on each level.

Thus, there are 253 parking spots on the top level.

10. A farmer has brown cows with white spots (genotype BBss) and brown cows with no spots (genotype BBSS). If the farmer crossed a brown spotted bull with a brown spotted cow, what percentage of the cows would be black? 25% 0% 50% 100%


If the farmer crossed a brown spotted bull with a brown spotted cow, the percentage of the cows would be black is 0%.  

To explain this, let us clarify some concepts first.

Having a genotype of BB means that brown is a dominant allele giving black a genotype of bb which is a recessive allele.  

In this question, we are only be concerned with a single allele, the cow color and pattern is already two alleles. Thus, we will not be concerned about the cow coat pattern whether it would be spotted or not since the question is only concerned with cow color.  

Looking at the genotype of the brown cow, we can say that brown cow, having the genotype of BB is dominant over a black cow that should be having the genotype of bb.

Thus if you were going to cross breed a brown spotted bull with a brown spotted cow the probability of having a black is zero percent.  

In this problem, we assume that the cow and the bull has a homozygous dominant allele for their coat color which is brown. In this case, the probability of having black is zero. If we cross them, we have

BB x BB = 4 BB or 4 brown

However, if they were both heterozygous dominant Bb, the chance is 25%. If we cross them, we have

Bb x Bb = 1 BB, 2 Bb, 1 bb (3 brown, 1 black)

26. On Fridays, the ratio of occupied spots to empty spots in the parking lot is 10:13. If the number of cars that parked there on that day is 400, find the number of empty spots in the parking


Answer:

520 Empty Spots

Step-by-step explanation:

We start with understanding RATIO.

Ratio = In simple terms, a ratio shows the relationship of two entities. It is written in this format A : B.

Say you have 5 apples and 7 bananas in a basket.

: The ratio of apples to bananas is 5 : 7

: The ratio of bananas to apples is 7 : 5

Example 1:

In a basket, you have a ratio of apples to oranges of 2 : 6, this means that in every 2 apples there are 6 oranges. Now, there are 10 apples inside. Can you find how many oranges are there?

Solution:

Ratio of apples to oranges = 2 : 6

Total number of apples = 10

Total number of oranges = ?

1st Step: Find how many sets of 2 apples are there in 10 apples

= 10/2

= 5 sets of 2 apples

2nd Step: Multiply the the total sets of 2 apples to the equivalent number of oranges as per the given ratio

= 5 x 6

= 30

: there are a total of 30 oranges in the basket

(That's it! Simple isn't it?)

Now let's get it.

List all the given in the problem

* the ratio of occupied spots to the empty spots = 10 : 13

* Total number of cars = 400

* Total number of empty spots = ?

SOLUTION

From the given ratio 10 : 13, this means that for every 10 occupied spots or 10 cars there are 13 empty spots.

Step 1: Find how many sets of 10 cars are there in 400 cars.

= 400/10

= 40

There are 40 sets of 10 in 400 cars.

Step 2: Find the empty spots by multiplying the total sets of 10 to the equivalent empty spots as represented by the given ratio

= 40 x 13

= 520

There are 520 empty spots in the parking lot.
