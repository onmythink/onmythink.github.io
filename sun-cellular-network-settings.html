Photosynthesis is the process by which carbon dioxide and water are converted into oxygen and glucose. The plant consumes glucose as food, and oxygen is produced as a by-product. During cellular respiration, oxygen and glucose are converted to water and carbon dioxide. Water and carbon dioxide are by-products of the process, while ATP is the energy that is converted. The products of one system are the reactants of the other, thanks to the link between photosynthesis and cellular respiration. Photosynthesis is the process of producing glucose and oxygen from sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide. Carbon dioxide and water are produced by cellular respiration, which consumes glucose and oxygen. To further underscore this point, the equation for photosynthesis is the inverse of the equation for cellular respiration. Answer:

Here are the answers to the questions you provided:

What is the process by which an organism obtains energy from organic compounds?

A. Photosynthesis

B. Natural selection

C. Cellular respiration

D. Metamorphosis

Answer: C. Cellular respiration. Cellular respiration is the process by which an organism obtains energy from organic compounds, such as glucose, through a series of chemical reactions.

Where does a heterotroph obtain its energy?

A. Microorganism

B. Sun's solar energy

C. Eating other organisms

D. Sun and eating other organisms

Answer: C. Eating other organisms. Heterotrophs are organisms that cannot produce their own food and must obtain energy by consuming other organisms. This is in contrast to autotrophs, which are able to produce their own food through processes such as photosynthesis.

Which of the following occurs in both photosynthesis and cellular respiration with short explanation

A. Production of ATP

B. Conversion of energy from one form to another

C. Movement of molecules across a membrane

D. All of the above

Answer: D. All of the above. Both photosynthesis and cellular respiration involve the production of ATP, the conversion of energy from one form to another, and the movement of molecules across a membrane. In photosynthesis, energy from the sun is converted into chemical energy stored in organic compounds such as glucose, while in cellular respiration, chemical energy stored in organic compounds is converted into ATP, which can be used by cells to power various cellular processes. Both processes also involve the movement of molecules across membranes, as photosynthesis occurs in the thylakoid membranes of the chloroplasts and cellular respiration occurs in the mitochondria.
