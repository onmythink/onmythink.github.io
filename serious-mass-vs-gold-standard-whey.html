23. A karat/carat of gold is 1/24 part of the mass. That is to say 16 carat is 16 parts gold and 8 parts other alloying metals. Calculate the mole percentage of gold with equal mass of copper and silver in a 14k gold alloy whose total mass is 250 grams.


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: Determine the mass of the components.

Since the alloy is a 14k gold, there are 14 parts of gold, 5 parts of copper, and 5 parts of silver (since copper and silver share the same mass according to the problem).

• For mass of gold

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of gold} & = \text{mass of alloy} \times \frac{\text{parts of gold}}{24} \\ & = \text{250 g} \times \frac{14}{24} \\ & = \text{145.833 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

• For mass of copper and silver

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of copper} = \text{mass of silver} & = \text{mass of alloy} \times \frac{\text{parts of copper}}{24} \\ & = \text{250 g} \times \frac{5}{24} \\ & = \text{52.0833 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of each component.

Note that the molar masses of Au, Cu, and Ag are 197.0 g/mol, 63.55 g/mol, and 107.9 g/mol, respectively.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & \text{moles of gold} = 145.833 \: \cancel{\text{g}} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{197.0 \: \cancel{\text{g}}} = \text{0.7403 mol} \\ & \text{moles of copper} = 52.0833 \: \cancel{\text{g}} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{63.55 \: \cancel{\text{g}}} = \text{0.8196 mol} \\ & \text{moles of silver} = 52.0833 \: \cancel{\text{g}} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{107.9 \: \cancel{\text{g}}} = \text{0.4827 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the mole percentage of gold.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mole percentage of gold} & = \frac{\text{moles of gold}}{\text{moles of gold + moles of copper + moles of silver}} \times 100\% \\ & = \frac{\text{0.7403 mol}}{\text{0.7403 mol + 0.8196 mol + 0.4827 mol}} \times 100\% \\ & = \boxed{36.24\%} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the mole percentage of gold is 36.24%.
