19. group 3 lasting virtue about how odins lost his eye


Answer:

Odin lost his eye in pursuit of wisdom and to save his countrymen. He sacrificed his physical appearance and convenience in exchange of a lasting trait that will benefit his people for the generations to come. Another virtue that Odin showcased is courage and leadership.

Explanation:

There are three virtues that can be derived from Odin's life:

The Virtue of WisdomThe Virtue of LeadershipThe Virtue of Courage

The Virtue of Wisdom

Odin is not an all-knowing god; in fact, his chief characteristic is that he’s always seeking wisdom, even at great personal cost. Odin sacrificed his eye in exchange for a deeper and Godly wisdom. He knew that earthly wisdom can be generated easily, but as a leader, and as a God, he needs a deeper kind of wisdom.  

One interpretation of this myth notes that Odin exchanges worldly vision, his physical eye, for internal vision, wisdom and discernment. While he didn’t give up his worldly sight entirely, he realized that in some cases, wisdom and discernment propel us further towards our goals than what’s on the surface.

When it comes to wisdom, you should be willing to place time, energy, attention, and even money on the altar of your goal. Consider the sacrifices to be investments in your wisdom in the long run. It will be well worth it.

Read more about wisdom here: https://brainly.ph/question/36490

The Virtue of Leadership

Characteristics like passion for wisdom and vigor in acquiring new knowledge and improving yourself are not necessarily inherent within us. It’s through action and work that we build up these properties and form the foundations of who we are.

Read more about leadership here: https://brainly.ph/question/1811315  

The Virtue of Courage

Odin knew that in order to progress, small parts of us need to die every now and then to allow new shoots of wisdom to grow in their place.
