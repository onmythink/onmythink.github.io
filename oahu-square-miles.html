10. The population density of New Jersey is about 1,195 people per square mile. Texas has a land area of 261,797 square miles. If Texas had the same population density as New Jersey, how many people would be living in that state?


Answer:

There are 312, 847, 415 people in the state of Texas.

Further Explanation

Population Density is very important in the planning and administration spheres of different regions or nations as a whole. The government will be able to know if medical facilities in a given area are enough to serve the given population without any strain on available resources.  

The population density formula is given by the ratio of the number of people living in the area to the land area in square units.  

                           [tex]PD = \frac{P}{A}[/tex]

          ; where PD is the population density

                        P is the population (number of people in a particular area)

                        A is the land area in square units

Steps in Solving for the Population Density

1. Define the area.

2. Determine the population.

3. Balance the data.

4. Calculate the population Density using the formula.  

The population density of New Jersey is 1,195 people per square mile. It assumes that the population density in Texas is also the same as in New Jersey. So, to solve the number of people, P, in Texas, substitute the value of [tex]PD = 1195[/tex] and [tex]A = 261, 797[/tex] into the population density formula, do cross multiplication, and then apply the inverse operation.

Solution:

                      [tex]\begin{aligned}1195 \:\text{p/mi}^2&=\frac{P}{261, 797\:\text{mi}^2}\\P&=1195\:\text{p/mi}^2\times261,797\:\text{mi}^2\\P&=312,847,415\:\text{people}\end{aligned}[/tex]

This means that there are 312, 847 415 people in Texas.
