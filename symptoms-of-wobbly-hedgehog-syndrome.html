2. what are the signs and symptoms of down syndrome disease? ​


Symptoms of down syndrome disease

• A flattened face, especially the bridge of the nose

• Almond-shaped eyes that slant up

• A short neck

• Small ears

• A tongue that tends to stick out of the mouth

• Tiny white spots on the iris (colored part) of the eye

• Small hands and feet

• A single line across the palm of the hand (palmar crease)

• Small pinky fingers that sometimes curve toward the thumb

• Poor muscle tone or loose joints

• Shorter in height as children and adults

3. What are the signs and symptoms of Trisomy 10 syndrome?


The disorder is characterized by unusually slow growth before and after birth); abnormally diminished muscle tone (hypotonia); mild to severe intellectual disability; and mild to severe delays in the acquisition of skills requiring coordination of mental and muscular activities

Trisomy 10 is very rarely diagnosed, especially in living persons. Most reports of trisomy 10 pertain to prenatal diagnosis of trisomy 10 in the fetus. In addition, trisomy 10 has been reported as part of partial chromosomal abnormalities in some leukemic cells and tumor specimens. Only 6 cases of mosaicism trisomy 10 have been reported so far. None of these reports pertain to living children with neurological abnormalities.

10. What are the signs and symptoms of Human Immunodeficiency Virus ( HIV) and Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS)


Answer:

The signs of Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) and Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS) vary depending on the severity or stage of the case.

Explanation:

Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome, or AIDS, is a life-threatening chronic condition caused by the Human Immunodeficiency Virus, or HIV. Once you contract the said virus, your body’s ability to fight disease causing organisms becomes weaker, thus, exposing you to more harmful radicals. Since the Human Immunodeficiency Virus can be transferred sexually, by blood, through pregnancy and childbirth, a person infected can spot the signs of infection within the first 1-2 months.  

Here are the symptoms of HIV depending on the stage of the disease:

1. Stage 1: Acute HIV Infection

• Fever

• Fatigue and Headache

• Swollen Lymph nodes, aching muscles and joint pain

• Skin Rashes

• Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea

• Sore throat and dry cough

• Cold night sweats

• Mouth ulcers

2. Stage 2: Clinical Latency

People on this stage may feel little to no symptoms. If receiving treatment, people with HIV can stay on this stage for about 10 to 15 years.

3. Stage 3: AIDS

If the patient is not receiving treatment for HIV, the virus will weaken the body and there is a possibility that the disease progresses to AIDS. This is the later stage of the HIV. Symptoms include:

• Rapid weight loss

• Pneumonia

• Anal or genital sores

• Extreme and unexplained tiredness

• Fever and night sweats

• Red, brown, purple or pink blotches on or under the skin or inside the mouth, nose or eyelids.

• Memory loss, depression, and other neurological disorders

It is extremely important that a person who suspects that he has HIV or AIDS get tested to verify that the above symptoms experienced are not other diseases. Symptoms may also vary per patient since HIV and AIDS destroy one’s immune system.
