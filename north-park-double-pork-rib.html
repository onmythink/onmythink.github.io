14. The length of a rectangular pork is 6mlonger than the worth. If the area of thepark is 135m² , Find the length and the width. Help me please ! :(​


Answer:

Width = 9 meters

Length = 15 meters

Step-by-step Explanation:

Let, width = x

length = x + 6

area = 135 m²

Translate,

area = length x width

135 = (x + 6) x

Solve,

135 = x² + 6x

Rearrange,

x² + 6x - 135 = 0

Factor,

(x + 15) (x - 9) = 0

Find the roots,

x1 = -15

x2 = 9

Since we are dealing with measurements we disregard the negative root.

Substitute,

Width = 9 m

Length = 9 + 6 = 15 m


15. Why are some ribs called true ribs and others false ribs?


Answer:

All of your ribs attach to your spine, but only the top seven pairs connect to your sternum. These are known as 'true ribs' and they are connected to your sternum by strips of cartilage. The first seven sets of ribs are considered true ribs as they have a direct attachment to the sternum.  The next three sets of ribs are considered false ribs as they are attached to the strum by costal cartilage links to the sternum

PINEAPPLE MARINADE

----Inducing a sour combination of real fruit, fruit juice, and soy sauce from the use of the barbecue, this golden, sweet marinade is great on meat and seafood, and keeps a bold aroma with the pineapples. Garlic powder sometimes replaces the garlic cloves and works for this recipe, too.

The ingredients in making pineapple marinade are the following;

1 cup pineapple juice, store-bought only

1 cup pineapple tidbits, optional

¼ cup sugar, brown only

¼ cup soy sauce, dark or light only

2 minced garlic cloves

PORK RIB MARINADE

---This marinade uses a delicious pork rub combined with corn syrup and vinegar to create a flavorful pork rib marinade. If you do not want to use corn syrup, then by all means use a good quality maple syrup, agave syrup, or even honey. However, keep in mind that using any of these will change the flavor of the original recipe.

The ingredients in making pork rib marinade are the following;

1 cup cider vinegar

1 cup water

1 tablespoon pork rub (use the same as you will use on the ribs)

2 tablespoon corn syrup
