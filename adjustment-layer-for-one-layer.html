13. Adjusting the fill percentage is called changing the _____. a. interior opacity b. tone c. blend d. layer​


Adjusting the fill percentage is called changing the interior opacity.

The interior of a shape is filled with color, pattern, texture, picture, or gradient. A gradient is a gradual transition of colors and shades from one hue to another, or from one shade of the same color to another shade of the same color. Changing the fill color of a shape only affects the object's inside or front. If you wish to change the color of a shape's effect, such as a shadow, you must modify the color of the shadow separately from the fill color. A 3-D effect gives a shape depth. You can either use the built-in combination of 3-D effects or apply specific effects to your shape.

1. Stamping allows you to merge the contents of more than one layer into a target layer while leaving the other layers intact. Note: When you save a merged document, you cannot revert back to the unmerged state; the layers are permanently merged.

2. Merge a copy of the layers onto a new layer

Then use the secret keyboard shortcut to merge a copy of your layers onto a new layer. On a Windows PC, press Shift+Ctrl+Alt+E. On a Mac, press Shift+Command+Option+E.

3. Opacity

4. By default, layers in a clipping mask are blended with the underlying layers using the blending mode of the bottommost layer in the group. However, you can choose to have the blending mode of the bottommost layer apply only to that layer, allowing you to preserve the original blending appearance of the clipped layers.

5. You can edit only one layer in Photoshop using a clipping mask. With an adjustment layer placed above another layer in the Layers Panel, right-click on the adjustment and press Create Clipping Mask to clip it to the underlying layer. Now this adjustment will only edit the one layer it's clipped to.
