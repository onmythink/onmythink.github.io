16. sterling silver contains 95% silver. If a necklace made of sterling silver weighs 15g, what is the mass of silver dissolved in the necklace?


Answer:

There are 14.25 grams of silver dissolved in the necklace.

Explanation:

What is silver?

Silver, the chemical element of atomic number 47, is a white lustrous metal valued for its decorative beauty and electrical conductivity. To get the mass of silver dissolved in a necklace, we need to multiply the percentage and the total mass of the necklace. So we will have:

Total mass of the necklace: 15 grams

Percentage of silver dissolved: 95%

1. We need to convert a percentage to a decimal. The equivalent value of 95% in decimal is 0.95.

2. Multiple the two values to get the final answer.

Let N be the mass of silver dissolved in the necklace.

N = (Mass of the necklace) * (Percentage of silver dissolved)

N = (15 grams) * (0.95)

N = 14.25 grams

22. what is solvent and solute of Sterling silver​


Answer:

In a solid solution, such as sterling silver, copper that has been heated at high temperatures is mixed with silver that also has been heated until it melts. The copper is the solute, which is the substance that will dissolve into the solvent. The silver is the solvent.
