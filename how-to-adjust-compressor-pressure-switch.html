2. when push button is closed what device will be energized ? a. cylinder b. solenoid c. compressor d. switch​


Answer:

The push button switch generally consists of a button cap, a return spring, a bridge-type moving contact, a static contact, a pillar connecting rod and a shell.

According to the opening and closing state of the contacts when the push button switch is not subjected to external force (ie static), it is divided into start push button switch (normally open button), stop push button switch (normally closed button) and composite push button switch (NO and NCd contact combination buttons). The contact of the start push button switch is closed when the button cap is pressed, and the contact is automatically disconnected and reset when released. When the stop push button switch is pressed down on the button cap, the contacts are separated, and the contacts are automatically closed and reset when released. When the composite push button switch presses the button cap, the bridge-type moving contact moves downwards, the NC contact is opened first, and then the NO contact is closed. When the button cap is released, the NO contact is first broken and reset, and then the NC contact is closed and reset

14. describe a compressor how does it work what gas is inside the compressor which has low boiling point


Describe a compressor how does it work what gas is inside the compressor which has low boiling point

Compressor is part of a freezer, refrigerator or air conditioner in its cooling cycle. It has a motor component inside made up of coiled copper wire and a pump that move the refrigerant in the form of gas through the system. It is also considered as a machine that compresses a gas into a pressure of several atmospheres. It is connected between a hallow tube known as evaporator and condenser.  The compressor works by removing heat from an object inside a refrigerator or freezer. An object with high temperature has more heat compared to an object with lower temperature. When heat is removed in an object, its temperature decreases. An object with low temperature is said to be cold or frozen.   The compressor starts to work when the temperature inside the refrigerator is sensed to be higher than the required temperature. It needs a refrigerant like freon gas to work properly. Freon is a kind of gas that can be compressed to high pressures and has a low boiling point. A refrigerant like freon is needed to have low boiling point in order for the compressor to require less amount of energy in doing its work. The freon gas is then compressed by a compressor to a high pressure resulting to an increase in temperature and becomes hot gas. Due to its high pressure, this gas moves through the condenser. The function of a compressor is to increase the pressure of a freon gas so that it will move through the condenser coil found at the back or at the bottom of a refrigerator or freezer. In this coil, freon gas with high temperature releases more heat into the surrounding air. Since the gas experiences high pressure, it will change into liquid as it cools. The freon gas converted into liquid moves to the expansion valve and carries vapor from the inside. In the evaporator, the liquid evaporates due to its low pressure and absorbs heat from the contents of refrigerator. The cycle repeats when the gas that absorbed heat reaches the compressor.

Q3. What happens to the hot gas produced by compression?

•The hot gas produced by compression in the refrigeration cycle is transferred to the condenser. The condenser then dissipates the heat from the refrigerant to the outside air. ... The refrigerant in effect turns the vapor into hot liquid as a result of the high pressure and reduction in temperature.

Q4. How does a condenser function?

•The function of the condenser in a refrigeration system is to transfer heat from the refrigerant to another medium, such as air and/or water. By rejecting heat, the gaseous refrigerant condenses to liquid inside the condenser. The major types of condensers used are (1) water-cooled, (2) air-cooled, and (3) evaporative.

•In an evaporator, the pressure is reduced and the heat is being absorbed by the refrigerant.
