25. Ken has 6 more marbles than Ben. If Ken gave his 10 marbles to Ben, How many more marbles does Ben have?" Please show the solution. :(


ALGEBRAIC WORD PROBLEM

Answer: Ben has 4 more marbles than Ken.

Step-by-step explanation:

This is an algebraic word problem. We can let a variable as Ken's and Ben's number of marbles. We can solve this problem in the perspective of Ben.

Let:

x = number of marbles Ken has

x - 6 = number of marbles Ben has

We put -6 since the word problem indicates that Ken has 6 more marbles than Ben in the beginning.

Since Ken gave 10 marbles to Ben, so:

x - 10 = number of marbles Ken has after he gave 10 marbles to Ben

x - 6 + 10 = number of marbles Ben has after Ken gave him 10 marbles

Now we can solve for Ben's number of marbles;

x - 6 + 10

x + 4 = this is now the number of marbles that Ben has.

Therefore, Ben has four more marbles than Ken after Ken gave him 10 marbles.

Sarah: x

Ben: x + 8


Ages 10 years ago:

Sarah: x - 10

Ben: x + 8 - 10 or x - 2


Equation:


x - 2 = 2 (x - 10)

x - 2 = 2x - 20

2x - x = 20 - 2

x = 18


Present ages:

Sarah: x = 18 years old

Ben: 18+8 = 26 years old
