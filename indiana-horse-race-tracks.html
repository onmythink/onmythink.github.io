4. cars that are racing in a race trackis this a example of kinetic energy or not​


Answer:

Yes

Explanation:

A race car, before the command to start the engine, is in potential energy. Race cars are moved to position for the race by pit crews pushing and pulling the car. The kinetic energy to move the car is coming from the crews – not the car itself. But once the command is given, the kinetic energy gets moving.

29. What is the kinetic energy of a 750kg race car that travels 55m/s down the race track?


KINETIC ENERGY

Problem:

» What is the kinetic energy of a 750kg race car that travels 55m/s down the race track?

Answer:The Kinetic energy of a race car is 1,134,375 J.

_ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _

Formula:

The formula to get the kinetic energy is:

[tex] \underline{ \boxed{\sf KE = \frac{1}{2} mv² }}[/tex]

where,

KE is the kinetic energy in joules (J)m is the mass in kilograms (kg), and;v is the velocity or speed in metres per second (m/s)

Given that:

mass = 750 kg velocity = 55 m/s

- To calculate the kinetic energy, we just need to substitute the given values of mass (in kilograms) and velocity (in m/s) to the formula, then solve.

Solution:

[tex]\sf KE = \frac{1}{2} mv² \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \sf \: KE = \frac{1}{2} \times (750) \times (55)^{2} \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \sf \: KE = \frac{1}{2} \times (750) \times (3,025) \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \sf \: KE = \underline{ \boxed{ \orange{ \sf \: 1,134,375 J}}}[/tex]

Therefore, the kinetic energy of a race car is 1,134,375 J.
