8. importance of transfiguration​


Answer:

- Transfiguration is an essential and significant event in the Christian faith. It represents Jesus' divinity and his ultimate power over life and death.

- The importance of transfiguration can be seen in its portrayal of Jesus as the Son of God, responsible for bringing salvation to humanity.

- Transfiguration also serves as a reminder that our physical existence is temporary, and our ultimate destination lies beyond this world.

- Moreover, it teaches us to have faith in God's plan for our lives, even when we face challenges and difficulties.
