Does Carlito commit an ethical practice in his methodology? Why? 3. Is Carlito entitled to become the author of the research without mentioning Dr. Zonso contribution? Explain. 4. Why do you need to seek permission from the Yolanda victims during the in-dep interviews with the survivors of the catastrophe?


Answer:

1. Yes, there are unethical practices in this situation. Using a methodology from a psychologist-author without securing permission to use it for his own research is an unethical practice. Also, forcing the Yolanda victims to be interviewed with the promise that solutions to their problems will be granted is an unethical practice. This is because the victims were not given a genuine opportunity to provide their informed consent and were misled about the purpose of the research.

2. Yes, Carlito committed an unethical practice in his methodology by using the methodology of Dr. Zonso without securing permission to use it for his own research. This is a violation of intellectual property rights and research ethics.

3. No, Carlito is not entitled to become the sole author of the research without mentioning Dr. Zonso's contribution. This is because Dr. Zonso's methodology was used in the research, and proper attribution should be given to the original author.

3. It is essential to seek permission from the Yolanda victims during the in-depth interviews with the survivors of the catastrophe. Informed consent is an important ethical principle in research that requires the researcher to obtain explicit, voluntary, and written consent from the participants before the research is conducted. Seeking permission from the victims is essential to respect their autonomy, privacy, and dignity as human beings. It also allows the victims to have control over their participation in the research and ensure that their rights are protected.
