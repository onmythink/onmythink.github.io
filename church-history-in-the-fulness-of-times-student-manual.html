6. Summarize your researched history about the invention of the manual sewing machine. (Maximum of 10 sentences only)


Answer:

The invention of the manual sewing machine is credited to Englishman Thomas Saint in 1790. His machine was designed to mimic the actions of hand-stitching and used a chain stitch. In 1804, French tailor Barthelemy Thimonnier created a machine that used a single thread and a hooked needle to make a chain stitch. In 1846, American inventor Elias Howe patented the first machine to use a lockstitch. This design was improved upon by Isaac Singer in 1851, and his machine became the first commercially successful sewing machine. Singer's machine was powered by a foot pedal and used a rotary hook to create a lockstitch. This design is still used today in modern manual sewing machines.
