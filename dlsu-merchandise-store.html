29. Statement I – One major difference of the operating cycle of a merchandising company and a service company is the storing of inventory. Statement II – One major difference of the operating cycle of a merchandising company and a manufacturing company is the storing of inventory. a. Both statements are true. b. Only Statement I is true. c. Only Statement II is true. d. Both statements are false.


Hello! Thank you for using Brainly for your homework needs. This is one of my favorite topics when I was in college and I hope my answer will help you.

Answer: a. Both statements are true.

Let me break down my answer so that we can understand why each statement is true.

Operating Cycle

An operating cycle is the time it takes a company or a business to provide capital to create goods or render services up to the time it takes for their customers to pay for the goods or services rendered.

So, an operating cycle is the time it takes initial cash to produce more cash (profit). Which is why it is also known as cash-to-cash cycle.

Now, many factors affect the length of an operating cycle. What are these factors?

Credit Policy. The way suppliers accept or dictate the payment terms affect how much cash a business will have to lay down at a time to start their operations. Some companies will have a 30-day grace period, allowing businesses to pay within a month after acquiring products or services. Some require cash payment upfront which means a business must have the capacity to pay instantly. While other companies allow for down payments, lay-away, multiple payment schemes which allows for more time but can sometimes be more expensive in the long run.Payment Terms. Taking the same concept as the aforementioned, we will apply it to the business itself. How long does the business allow its clients to pay them back for goods and services rendered?Order Fulfillment Policy. This refers to how quick a business can provide the final goods or services to a client. The quicker they are able to fulfill and order, the faster they can expect cash to flow in. This also means they must have inventory at hand at all times. When the fulfillment of an order takes time due to several processes, it also means inventory will move slower. An operating cycle and inventory cycle are closely intertwined and affect each other.

Now that we have all those out of the way, let us examine both statements.

One major difference of the operating cycle of a merchandising company  and a service company is the storing of inventory.

A service company may not have such an extensive inventory such as a merchandising company but they still have inventory for some products that are needed to complete a service. However, not all of their services are uniform. The inventory cycle of a service company may be shorter but within that inventory, different items may also have individually different cycles.

A merchandising company definitely have bigger inventory because the products are the main reason why the business exists. Depending on the type of product they carry, they may have a shorter or longer inventory cycle than a service company.

One major difference of the operating cycle of a merchandising company and a manufacturing company is the storing of inventory.

Merchandising companies and manufacturing companies have extremely different lengths of inventory cycle. A manufacturing company has a longer inventory cycle due to several steps in arriving at a final product and selling it.

A merchandising company already has the product in their inventory and is just waiting for a final consumer to buy it from. However, depending on the type of product they carry, it may take shorter or longer for them to refill their inventory. Products such as food and medicine take shorter periods to sell and refill while cars and gadgets may take longer since they are not are not a primary need.
