How will you define the acoustic guitar?​

A stringed instrument, the acoustic guitar is a stringed instrument. When a string is plucked, the vibration is carried from the bridge to the top of the guitar, where it reverberates. It also transferred to the instrument's side and back, vibrating through the body's air and produced music via the sound hole. Guitar is the original, all-encompassing title for this stringed instrument, and the retronym 'acoustic guitar' separates it from an electric guitar, which is powered by electronics. A guitar's body is typically a sound box, with the top side serving as a sound board for amplifying the vibration sounds of the strings. The guitar's six strings are tuned (low to high) E2 A2 D3 G3 B3 E4 in standard tuning.

The father of modern architectural acoustics is an American physicist named Wallace Clement Sabine. Born in 1868, Sabine graduated from Ohio State University in 1886 and pursued graduate study at Harvard University. By 1895, he was a young assistant physics professor in Harvard's physics department.
