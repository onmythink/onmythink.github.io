An organ system is also a collection of organs and tissues that collaborate to execute a given function.

EXAMPLES

A red blood cell is a cell in the circulatory system, the cardiac muscle of the heart is a tissue, the heart itself is an organ, and the organ system is the circulatory system.

Cells, tissues, organs, and organ systems are the four layers of organization that make up an organism. These levels divide complicated anatomical systems into groupings, making the components more understandable.

An organ system is also a group of organs that operate together to accomplish a common goal. In the human body, there are eleven separate organ systems, each with its unique set of functions.

Representative elements belong to the s- and p-blocks of the long form of the periodic table, excluding the Noble gases.

Properties of representative elements:

Group 1 and group 2 elements are metals and mostly form an ionic bond. They are good conductors of heat and electricity. They form basic oxides which are usually colorless and soluble in water.

Group 1 and group 2 elements are highly reactive. Hence, they react violently with water and oxygen.

Group 1 and group 2 elements exist in solid state.

Group 13 to group 17 elements are a combination of metals, metalloid, and non-metals. They form an ionic bond with metals and a covalent bond with other non-metals.

Most of the non-metals exist in gaseous state, but some are also solid and liquid.
