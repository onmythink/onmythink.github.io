7. excess reagend GROUP ACTIVITY For the reaction below, if 6.3 g of S is reacted with 10.0 g of O2, which one will be the limiting reagent and the excess reagent? 2S(s)+ 302 (g)2SO3 (s)​


Answer:

S is the limiting reagent and O2 is the excess reagent.

Solution:

2S(s) + 3O2(g) → 2SO3(s)

Molar Mass:

MS = 32.06 g/molMO2 = 32.00 g/mol

From S: 6.3 g S × (1 mol S/32.06 g S) × (2 mol SO3/2 mol S) × (80.06 g SO3/1 mol SO3) = 9.9 g SO3From O2: 10.0 g O2 × (1 mol O2/32.00 g O2) × (2 mol SO3/3 mol O2) × (80.06 g SO3/1 mol SO3) = 16.7 g SO3

We can see that 6.3 g of S will produce 9.9 g of SO3, while 10.0 g of O2 will produce 16.7 g of SO3. Therefore, S is the limiting reagent and O2 is the excess reagent.

In short, the Limiting reagent and Excess reagent are:

Limiting reagent: SExcess reagent: O2
