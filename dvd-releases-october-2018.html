13. What is the difference of cd, dvd, and blu-ray dvd?​


Answer:

CD, DVD, and Blu-ray are all types of optical storage media used for storing digital data. The main differences between them are their storage capacity, data transfer speed, and the type of laser used to read the data.

CD (Compact Disc) is the oldest and most basic form of optical storage media. It has a storage capacity of up to 700 MB and is primarily used for storing music and other audio files. The data transfer speed of a CD is relatively slow, at up to 1.4 MB per second.

DVD (Digital Versatile Disc) is a more advanced form of optical storage media, capable of storing much more data than a CD. A standard single-layer DVD can hold up to 4.7 GB of data, while a dual-layer DVD can hold up to 8.5 GB. DVDs are commonly used for storing movies and other video content. The data transfer speed of a DVD is faster than a CD, with speeds of up to 11 MB per second.

Blu-ray Disc is the most advanced form of optical storage media currently available. It has a storage capacity of up to 50 GB (dual-layer), which is significantly higher than a DVD. Blu-ray discs are primarily used for high-definition video content. The data transfer speed of a Blu-ray disc is much faster than a DVD, with speeds of up to 54 MB per second. To read a Blu-ray disc, a special blue-violet laser is used, which has a shorter wavelength than the red laser used for CDs and DVDs. This allows the laser to read data more accurately from the much smaller pits on the disc's surface.
