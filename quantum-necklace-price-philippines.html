1. A necklace was sold for Php3150.00 with a 40% discount.what was the original Price of the necklace?​


ANSWER


Php5250.00

SOLUTION

To determine the original price of the necklace, you can use the following formula:

Original price = selling price / (1 - discount%)

Plugging in the given values, you get:

Original price = 3150 / (1 - 40%) = 3150 / (1 - 0.4) = 3150 / 0.6 = 5250

Therefore, the original price of the necklace was Php5250.00.

4. Edna already owns 4 necklaces, and additional necklaces are priced at 2 for a dollar. Is the total number of necklaces Edna owns proportional to the amount of money Edna spends on additional necklaces?


Answer:

Yes.

Explanation:

Two terms are proportional if one term increases with the other, or as one term increases, the other decreases.

Using this basis, we know that the number of necklaces is proportional to the amount of money spent.

As the amount increases by a dollar, the necklaces increase by 2.

We can also determine if this is proportional if we can come up with a linear equation that relates the two terms.

Let x = necklaces; let y = amount spent for additional necklaces.

Using what I previously said in the third paragraph, y = x/2. But, we need to add 4 to this since there are 4 necklaces already to start with. Thus, y = x/2 + 4.

To summarize, yes; the number of necklaces is proportional to the amount spent on additional necklaces.

25. What is the fourth quantum number that indicates the direction the electron is spinning? *Principal quantum numberAngular momentum quantum numberMagnetic quantum numberSpin quantum number​


The principal quantum number is the quantum number denoted by n and which indirectly describes the size of the electron orbital. It is always assigned an integer value (e.g., n = 1, 2, 3...), but its value may never be 0. An orbital for which n = 2 is larger, for example, than an orbital for which n = 1.

The angular momentum quantum number, ℓ, is the quantum number associated with the angular momentum of an atomic electron. The angular momentum quantum number determines the shape of the electron's orbital.

The magnetic quantum number is one of four quantum numbers in atomic physics. The set is: principal quantum number, azimuthal quantum number, magnetic quantum number, and spin quantum number. Together, they describe the unique quantum state of an electron.

the spin quantum number is a quantum number which describes the intrinsic angular momentum of an electron or other particle. The phrase was originally used to describe the fourth of a set of quantum numbers, which completely describe the quantum state of an electron
