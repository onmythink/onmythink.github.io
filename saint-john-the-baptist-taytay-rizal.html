1.De La Salle originally had no idea of beginning schools for Christian education for the working class. He was gradually drawn into the work; he made himself poor to be of service to them and relied on the Providence of God to supply the basic material needs of the Brothers and the schools.

2.On May 24, 1900 the Catholic church recognized the deep holiness of De La Salle when he was canonized. On May 15, 1950 the Church considered St. La Salle as a pioneer in education and made him the Church's official patron of all Teachers.

30. Why did st. john baptist de la salle became the patron saint of teachers?


Answer:

Of noble birth, La Salle was ordained priest in 1678 and devoted himself to education of the poor. He helped to establish charity schools in Reims and subsequently formed his teachers into a religious order (1680). He was declared patron saint of all schoolteachers by Pope Pius XII in 1950.
