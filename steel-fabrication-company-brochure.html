1. Low carbon steels are generally used in the as rolled or fabricated state. What is the reason for this?


Answer:

Low-carbon steels contain up to 0.30% carbon. A majority of this class of steel is flat-rolled products like sheet or strip; usually they are in a cold-rolled and annealed condition. These steels have high formability as they contain very low carbon, usually less than 0.10% C, with up to 0.4% Mn.

Explanation:

“Low carbon steel” still covers an awful lot of ground - but it’s typically something under 1% carbon, possibly under 1% of lead or sulfur for machinability, and balance iron.

If you want to know specifically, I suggest you look up the AISI alloy numbers - “low carbon steels” are usually 10xx and 11xx steels, with leaded being 10Lxx and 11Lxx, and sulfurized steels being 10Sxx and 11Sxx.

The field of “steel alloys” is quite broad, and metallurgy is fascinating! (To me, anyhow.) Go past the 8xxx and 9xxx and start looking into “superalloys” if you really want to see something interesting! The mechanical properties of those can be outlandish…

15. The Mariel Tub Company manufactures two lines of bathtubs called model A and model B. Every tub requires blending a certain amount of steel and zinc; the company has available a total of 2500kg of steel and 600kg of zinc. Each model A bathtub requires a mixture of 12.5kg of steel and 2kg of zinc wherein each yields a profit of 9000php. Each model B tub produced can be sold for a profit of 7000php; it in turn requires 10kg of steel and 3kg of zinc. Find the best production mix of bathtubs.


Answer:

The best production mix of bathtubs between the two models is model B.

Step-by-step explanation:

We have steps to follow in answering word problems.

Analyze the Problem

Let us analyze the given problem first. Based on the problem, the company manufactures two bathtubs which requires different amount of steel and zinc for each production. We need to know which production mix of bathtubs is best.

2. What are the given?

Model A - requires 12.5 kg of steel and 2 kg of zinc with a profit of 9000 php

Model B - requires 10 kg of steel and 3 kg of zinc with a profit of 7000 php

3. Solution

Model A

(steel) 2500 ÷ 12.5 = 200

(zinc) 600 ÷ 2 = 300

* For this amount of steel and zinc, the company can produce 200 bathtubs with 9000 php profit.

Model B

(steel) 2500 ÷ 10 = 250

(zinc) 600 ÷ 3 = 200

* The company can also produce 200 bathtubs with a profit of 7000 php.

4. Give the Final Answer

Based on our answers, we can clearly tell that the best production of bathtubs is Model B, in terms of the amount of steel and zinc needed for the production with a lesser or cheaper profit needed.
