1. The area of a square courtyard is equal to the area of a rectangular courtyard whose length is eight meters more than the side of the square courtyard and whose width is six meters LESS than the side of the square courtyard. Find the dimensions of the square and rectangular courtyards.


Answer:

The side of the square is 24 meters.

The length of the rectangle is 32 meters and the width is 18 meters.

Step-by-step explanation:

Area of a square and rectangle

The area of a square can be calculated by the formula:

A = [tex]s^{2}[/tex]

where s is the sides of the square.

The area of a rectangle can be calculated by the formula:

A = l x w

where l is the length and w is the width.

In this problem, we are given three mathematical statements. We need to know the dimensions of the square and rectangle.

Step 1: Translate the mathematical statements given into working mathematical equations.

Statement 1: The area of a square courtyard is equal to the area of a rectangular courtyard

Equation 1: [tex]Area_{square} = Area_{rectangle}[/tex]

                  [tex]s^{2}[/tex] =  l x w

Statement 2: length is eight meters more than the side of the square

Equation 2: l = s + 8

Statement 3: width is six meters less than the side of the square

Equation: w = s - 6

Step 2: Substitute equations 2 and 3 to equation 1 to get the whole mathematical equation of the problem. Simplify the equation.

  [tex]s^{2}[/tex] =  l x w

  [tex]s^{2}[/tex] =  ( s + 8 ) x ( s - 6)

  [tex]s^{2}[/tex] =  

 [tex]s^{2}[/tex] -  

0 = 2s - 48

2s = 48

s = 24

Step 3: Substitute the resulting value of s to equations 2 and 3 to get the value of the length and width.

l = s + 8

l = 24 + 8

l = 32

w = s - 6

w = 24 - 6

w = 18

Therefore, the side of the square is 24 meters while the length of the rectangle is 32 meters and the width is 18 meters.

17. a ball is droped in the window of a three story townhouse what isits velocity as it hits the ground after2s? neglect air recistance with solution​


Assuming that the "three-story townhouse" is three stories high, which is approximately 30 feet or 9 meters high, we can use the formula for the velocity of an object in free fall to determine the velocity of the ball as it hits the ground.

The formula for the velocity of an object in free fall is:

v = g*t

where v is the velocity of the object, g is the acceleration due to gravity (which is approximately 9.81 m/s^2 on Earth), and t is the time it takes for the object to fall.

Since the ball falls for 2 seconds before hitting the ground, we can substitute these values into the formula to get:

v = 9.81 m/s^2 * 2 s = 19.62 m/s

Therefore, the velocity of the ball as it hits the ground is approximately 19.62 m/s, neglecting air resistance.

Assuming the ball is dropped from rest at the top of the third story townhouse, we can use the equations of motion to calculate the velocity of the ball as it hits the ground after 2 seconds, neglecting air resistance.

The distance fallen by the ball is the height of the townhouse, which we will assume to be 9 meters (three stories at an average height of 3 meters per story). Using the equation of motion:

d = 1/2 * g * t^2

where d is the distance fallen, g is the acceleration due to gravity (9.81 m/s^2), and t is the time elapsed, we can solve for t:

9 = 1/2 * 9.81 * t^2

t^2 = 9/4

t = 1.5 seconds

Now we can use another equation of motion to calculate the velocity of the ball as it hits the ground:

v = g * t

v = 9.81 * 2

v = 19.62 m/s

Therefore, the velocity of the ball as it hits the ground after 2 seconds is approximately 19.62 m/s.
