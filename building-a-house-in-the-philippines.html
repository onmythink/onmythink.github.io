16. How does the spanish infuence the philippines in term of building house like bahay na kubo? with exp ​


Answer:

With the arrival of the Spanish Colonial era, the idea of establishing more permanent communities with the church and government center as focal points became popular. Christianized people such as Tagalogs, Visayans, Ilocanos, Kapampangans, Bicolanos, Cagayanons, Mestizos, Criollos, Chinese, and others were forced to live in the lowlands. This newly formed culture, the majority of whom are of Austronesian ancestry, each with their own distinct traditions of Austronesian architecture, dating back even before the Hispanic period, collectively evolved a certain style of construction that has become synonymous From simple bamboo materials to mansions, the appearance varies. Standard, Chinese, Americas, European, and eclectic designs and ornamentations are all available. Construction using heavier, more permanent materials was also desirable due to the new community's setup. Finding European construction styles impractical in local conditions, Spanish and Filipino builders quickly adapted the bahay kubo's characteristics and combined them with the Hispano-American Antillean (Caribbean) architectural style, resulting in the indigenous bahay na bato ("stone house") or bahay luma ("stone house") ("old house" or "traditional house").

Ancestral houses of the Philippines or Heritage Houses are homes owned and preserved by the same family for several generations as part of the Filipino family culture.

1.)It corresponds to long tradition by Filipino people of venerating Ancestors and Elders. Houses could be a simple house to a mansion. The most common ones are the "Bahay na Bato". Some houses of prominent families had become points of interest or museums in their community because of its cultural, architectural or historical significance.
2-3.) These houses that are deemed of significant importance to the Filipino culture are declared Heritage House by the National Historical Commission of the Philippines (NHCP), previously known as the National Historical Institute (NHI) of the Philippines.
