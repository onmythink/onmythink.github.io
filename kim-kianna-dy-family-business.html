7. essay one of your family members is dying what will you do​


It is a heartbreaking scenario to imagine that one of my family members is dying. It is natural to feel overwhelmed, confused, and lost in such a situation. In times like these, it is important to gather myself and think about the ways in which I can make the last days of my family member more comfortable and peaceful.

First and foremost, I would ensure that my family member receives the best possible medical attention and care. I would consult with the medical professionals who are attending to my family member and follow their advice and guidance. I would also try to understand the medical condition and prognosis of my family member so that I can mentally prepare myself and support my family member accordingly.

Secondly, I would try to create a comfortable and supportive environment for my family member. I would try to fulfill their wishes and preferences, whether it is about food, music, or company. I would spend as much time as possible with my family member and let them know how much I love and care for them. I would also make sure that other family members and close friends get to spend time with my family member.

Thirdly, I would try to provide emotional support and comfort to my family member. I would listen to them attentively and allow them to express their feelings and emotions without judgment. I would offer words of encouragement, hope, and faith to my family member and try to uplift their spirits.

Lastly, I would take care of myself as well. Dealing with a dying family member can be emotionally and physically draining, and I would make sure to take breaks, rest, and seek emotional support from other family members or friends.

In conclusion, dealing with a dying family member can be one of the most challenging and emotional experiences in life. However, by being present, supportive, and caring, I can make the last days of my family member as comfortable and peaceful as possible.

8. essay one of your family members is dying what will you do​


The news of a family member's impending death is one of the most challenging experiences anyone can go through. It is a time when emotions are running high, and decisions need to be made about how to best support the person who is dying, as well as the family members who are left behind.

In such a situation, my first priority would be to offer emotional support and comfort to my family member. I would do everything in my power to make them feel as comfortable and loved as possible, ensuring that their final moments are filled with dignity and respect. This might involve staying with them at their bedside, holding their hand, and providing them with words of comfort and reassurance.

I would also work closely with their healthcare team to ensure that they receive the best possible care during this difficult time. This might involve arranging for pain management, coordinating with hospice services, and liaising with other healthcare providers to ensure that all of their medical needs are met.

In addition to providing emotional and medical support, I would also take steps to ensure that my family member's final wishes are honored. This might involve making arrangements for a funeral or memorial service, or working with their legal team to ensure that their estate is settled in accordance with their wishes.

Most importantly, I would be there for my family members during this difficult time. I would make myself available to listen to their concerns, offer support and comfort, and provide them with the space and time they need to grieve and come to terms with their loss. While it is impossible to take away the pain of losing a loved one, being there for one another can make the grieving process a little bit easier.

In conclusion, facing the death of a family member is never easy, and it requires a great deal of emotional strength and support. By providing comfort and care, working closely with healthcare providers, and honoring the wishes of the dying person, we can ensure that their final moments are filled with love, dignity, and respect. Above all, we must be there for our family members during this difficult time, offering them our love, support, and understanding as they grieve and come to terms with their loss.

17. In connection to the case titled "Friendship in Business," was the action of Mr. Dy to cancel the contact legally correct and morally justified?


Answer:

No.

Explanation:

As what I have observed while reading the case, right after the molasses was returned, the rage or anger that Mr. Dy felt was the factor that leads to the cancellation of contract which is considered as not legally correct and not morally justified action. He should not let his emotions ruin their relationship as business associates. In fact, Mr. Gutierrez has the right to return the molasses since it did not meet the indicated quality. Of course, as an owner of Philippine-Hawaiian International Sugarcane Incorporated, it is your job to ensure that the products that you are going to deliver to your clients are in a high quality. Just because Mr. Gutierrez’s payment is delayed, it doesn’t mean that you will send him a substandard or low quality product. In the very first place, it was Mr. Dy who agreed with his friend that it’s okay that the payment will be delayed. So, it’s not clearly reasonable to argue that the delay of payment was a form of breach of contract. He cannot blame anyone for his actions. In this case, the right thing that Mr. Dy should have done is to replace those molasses with better quality, and then deliver it right away to Mr. Gutierrez. Only if he did it, there will be no problem at all. The said contract and their relationship as business associates will still remain.
