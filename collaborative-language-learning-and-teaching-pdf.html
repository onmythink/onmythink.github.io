2. What is the meaning of collaborative teaching and learning?


Answer:

The phrase "collaborative learning" refers to a range of educational methods that include cooperative intellectual work on students or students and instructors. Typically, students work in pairs or trios, collaboratively seeking knowledge, answers, meanings, or producing a product. Collaborative learning activities vary considerably, but most focus on students' investigation or application of course content rather than the teacher's presentation or explanation.

Collaborative learning marks a major departure from the traditional teacher-centered or lecture-centered classroom environment seen in colleges. While the lecturing/listening/note-taking process does not entirely vanish in collaborative classrooms, it coexists with other strategies focused on students' conversation and active engagement with the course content. Teachers who use collaborative learning methods see themselves less as expert transmitters of information and more as expert designers of intellectual experiences for students—as coaches or midwives of a more spontaneous learning process.

While Collaborative teaching, also known as cooperative or team teaching, is a collaborative effort by educators to lead, educate, and mentor groups of students. Collaboration often happens amongst experts from diverse fields such as core subjects, special education, optional courses, library science, and guidance programs. At times, instructors from the same department or grade level may collaborate to address various learning levels or offer a broader range of supervised activities for students to practice skills. Collaboration is possible at all educational levels and in all topic areas.
