BYES

The data provides the number of byes required for a single-elimination tournament for different numbers of teams. For five teams, one bye is needed, while for seven teams, one bye is also required. Nine teams require seven byes, ten teams require six byes, and twelve teams require four byes.

Single Elimination Selection

Lets  assume that the competition is single elimination, which means that a team is eliminated from the competition if they lose a match. In the bracket, the byes would be assigned as follows:

5 teams- One team gets a bye and advances to the second round while the other four teams compete in the first round. The two winning teams will next compete in the semifinal round.  7 teams- When a team has a bye, they advance automatically to the semifinals. The other six teams will compete in the first round, with the winner advancing to the quarterfinals. The winners of the quarterfinals will then face off in the semi-final.9 teams- The top seven teams automatically advance to the quarterfinals due to seven byes, while the worst two teams play in the first round. Each first-round match's victor advances to the quarterfinals, where they'll take on one of the other top seven teams.10 teams-Due to the six byes, the top six teams advance immediately to the quarterfinals while the other four teams compete in the first round. The top six teams will face off in the quarterfinals following the advancement of the first-round match winners.12 teams- Due to four byes, the top four teams automatically progress to the quarterfinals while the remaining eight teams play in the first round. The victors of the first-round matches will compete against the top four teams in the quarterfinals.
