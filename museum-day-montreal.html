21. what differentiate traditional museum from digital museum?​


Traditional vs Digital Museums

Answer:

The main difference is that traditional museums can be seen personally and the artifacts are real. On the other hand, digital museums are alternatives that offer pictures, video, and interactive software of artifacts that cannot be visited or not present in the traditional museum.

Explanation:

The traditional museum presents artifacts and artworks to the public so that they may understand these objects within a setting that provides historical and cultural context to further visitors’ knowledge. Then online museums were designed to reach a larger number of people and motivate physical visits to museums, informing potential visitors who were planning their visits. They also intended to encourage the research and personal interests of visitors through collection information as well as assist school visits and research for students.

But within the traditional physical museums, artifacts are often regarded as passive since they are positioned so that people can see these objects but cannot interact with them. Older generations are accustomed to this practice; however, potential younger visitors now desire more stimulating experiences.

23. Enumerate the environmental benefits of montreal protocol. (Three sentences)​


Answer:

The Montreal Protocol is an international treaty that aims to protect the Earth's ozone layer by phasing out the production and consumption of substances that deplete it. The treaty has several environmental benefits, including:

Prevention of further ozone depletion, which reduces the amount of harmful ultraviolet radiation that reaches the Earth's surface and reduces the risk of skin cancer, cataracts, and other health problems.

Reduction of greenhouse gas emissions, as many of the substances that deplete the ozone layer are also potent greenhouse gases. By phasing out these substances, the Montreal Protocol has helped mitigate the impact of climate change.

Protection of ecosystems and biodiversity, as the ozone layer plays an important role in regulating the Earth's temperature and weather patterns. By preserving the ozone layer, the Montreal Protocol has helped protect natural habitats and preserve biodiversity.

24. last sunday the exponent museum admitted 50 adults and children and has collected 600. the price of admission is 15 for adult and 10 per children. how many adults and how many children were admitted to the museum that day?​


Answer:

Let x be the number of adults and y be the number of children.

Then,

x + y = 50

15x + 10y = 600

Dividing 15x + 10y = 600 by 5,

3x + 2y = 120

Multiplying x + y = 50 by 2,

2x + 2y = 100

Subtracting both equations,

3x + 2y - (2x + 2y) = 20

3x + 2y - 2x - 2y = 20

x = 20

Substituting x=20 to x + y = 50,

20 + y =50

y = 30

Therefore,

there are 20 adults and 30 children in the museum.
