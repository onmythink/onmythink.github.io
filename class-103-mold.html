3. An artist creates a solid sculpture made from 8.20 kg of tin. She wishes to create an identical sculpture, using the same mold used to make the original, out of solid silver. What is the mass (in kg) of the silver sculpture? (The density of tin is 7.26 ✕ 103 kg/m3, and that of silver is 10.50 ✕ 103 kg/m3.)


Answer:

The mass of the silver sculpture is 11.86 kg

Explanation:

In this problem, we are going to use the concept of density of a given material. Since we are going to use the same mold, it means it has the same volume.

The density formula is:

ρ = m/V

Solving the problem

1. Let us solve first for the volume of sculpture from the original mold of tin.

Let us use the formula for density and substitute the given data.

ρ(tin) = m/V

7.26 ✕ 10³ kg/m³ = 8.20 kg / V(tin)

Cross-multiply

V(tin) = 8.20 kg / 7.26 ✕ 10³ kg/m³

V(tin) = 0.001129 m³

V(tin) = 1.13 x 10⁻³ m³

2. Now, let us solve for the mass of the silver scultureusing the same mold.

V(tin) = V(silver)

ρ(silver) = m/V(tin)

10.50 ✕ 10³ kg/m³ = m /1.13 x 10⁻³ m³

Cross-multiply

m = (10.50 ✕ 10³ kg/m³)(1.13 x 10⁻³ m³)

m = 11.86 kg

Therefore, the mass of the silver sculpture is 11.86 kg.
