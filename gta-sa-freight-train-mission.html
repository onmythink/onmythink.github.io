[tex]\mathbb{FORMULA:}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{d=st} \: \longrightarrow \: \mathtt{t=\dfrac{d}{s}}[/tex]

where:

d = distance

s = speed

t = time

[tex]\mathbb{SOLUTION:}[/tex]

For the freight train:

s₁ = x

[tex]\mathtt{t_{1}=\dfrac{270}{x}}[/tex]

For the passenger train:

s₂ = x + 20

[tex]\mathtt{t_{2}=\dfrac{390}{x+20}}[/tex]

t₁ = t₂

[tex]\mathtt{\dfrac{270}{x}=\dfrac{390}{x+20}}[/tex]

solving for x,

[tex]\mathtt{\dfrac{270}{x}=\dfrac{390}{x+20}} \longleftarrow \: \texttt{multiply both sides by x(x+20)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{270(x+20)=390x} \longleftarrow \: \texttt{expand}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{270x+5400=390x} \longleftarrow \: \texttt{combine like terms}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{120x=5400}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{x=\dfrac{5400}{120}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{x=45}[/tex]

x + 20 = 65

[tex]\mathbb{ANSWER:}[/tex]

Freight train: [tex]\large{\boxed{\texttt{45 mi/hr}}}[/tex]

Passenger train:  [tex]\large{\boxed{\texttt{65 mi/hr}}}[/tex]

24. (motion problem) a freight train travelling at a rate of 60 kph leaves a city. three hours later, an express train whose average rate is 80 kph leaves the same city travelling in the same direction. how long will it take the express train to overtake the freight train?


Answer:

After 9 hours


Step-by-step explanation:

Define x:

Let x be the number of hours the express train need to overtake the freight train.


Find the distance the express train covered in x hours

Distance = Speed x Time

Distance = 80x km


Distance covered by the freight train in the first 3 hours

1 hour = 60 km

3 hours = 60 x 3 = 180 km


Find the distance the freight train covered in x hour

Distance = Speed x Time

Distance = 60x km


Solve x:

80x = 60x + 180

20x = 180

x = 9 hours


Answer: The express train will overtake the freight train after 9 hours.
