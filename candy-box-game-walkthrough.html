2. On a shelf were 23 boxes of 9 or 13 candies, all boxes totaling 255 candies. How many boxes were with 13 candies.​


Answer:

12 boxes of 13 candies

Step-by-step explanation:

We note with:

a = boxes of 9 candies;

b = boxes of 13 candies.

a + b = 23

9a + 13b = 255

We take the a to the left

b = 23 - a

We rewrite the second equation according to the changes above

9a + 13 (23 - a) = 255

9a + 299 - 13a = 255

13a - 9a = 299 - 255

4a = 44

a = 11

b = 23 - 11

b = 12

Final: 12 boxes of 13 candies

19. there are 225 candies in a box. the candies are hershey's, goya and muncher in terms of brands. if 20% of the candies are hershey's, 1/15 of the candies are goya. how many candies are munchers?​


Answer:

165 munchers

Step-by-step explanation:

Jusy add the number of hersheys and goyas then subtract it to the total number of candies in the box to know how many munchers are there.

Getting the number of hersheys.

[tex]225 \times 20\% \\ = 225 \times 0.2 \\ = 45[/tex]

Getting the number of goyas.

[tex]225 \times \frac{1}{15} \\ = \frac{225}{15} \\ = 15[/tex]

Now, let's get the number of munchers.

[tex]225 - (45 + 15) \\ = 225 - 60 \\ = 165[/tex]

Therefore, there are 165 candies that are muncher in the box.

22. The box of candies is shared equally among 9,12, or 18 children, thealways 6 candies left. Find the smallest number of candies in the box?​


The smallest number of candies in the box is 42.

Step-by-step explanation:

Word Problem: Least Common Multiple

The problem above is asking for the smallest number of candies in the box. To find the answer, you have to find the least common multiple of 9, 12 and 18. Then, add 6 to it, since there are always 6 candies left.

Given:

number of candies left - 6

number of children - 9, 12 and 18

Solution:

Let us find their least common multiple using the listing method. Listing method refers to writing down the multiples of every number.

9 - 9, 18, 27, 36, 45, 54

12 - 12, 24, 36, 48

18 - 18, 36, 54

The least common multiple of 9, 12 and 18 is 36.

Now, add 6 to it.

36 + 6 = 42

Final Answer:

The smallest number of candies in the box is 42.

Checking:

42 ÷ 9 = 4 r. 6

42 ÷ 12 = 4 r. 6

42 ÷ 18 = 2 r. 6
