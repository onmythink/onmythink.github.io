13. Isn't the diamond real? ​


Answer:

If you cannot see the dot or a reflection in the stone, then the diamond is real. Because a true diamond has powerful refractive qualities, light will bounce in different directions instead of a straight line. This is why you won’t be able to see letters or dots through a natural, real diamond.

HOW TO TELL IF DIAMONDS ARE REAL:

How to tell if diamonds are real can also be done by checking the setting of the stone, if it is in fact set in a piece of jewelry. 29. how did the de beers cartel try to maintain control of the price in the diamond market __________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________​


Answer:

The De Beers cartel tried to maintain control of the price in the diamond market by using various tactics such as:

1. Controlling the supply: The De Beers cartel controlled the supply of diamonds in the market by buying diamonds from other mines and stockpiling them. This helped them regulate the quantity of diamonds in the market and maintain prices.

2. Creating an illusion of scarcity: The De Beers cartel also strategically released diamonds into the market to create an illusion of scarcity. This created a perception that diamonds were rare and valuable, and people were willing to pay more for them.

3. Marketing: The De Beers cartel invested heavily in marketing to create a demand for diamonds. They created the famous slogan "A diamond is forever" to convince people that a diamond was a timeless and valuable investment.

4. Controlling distribution: The De Beers cartel also controlled the distribution of diamonds by only allowing authorized dealers to sell diamonds. This helped them control the supply chain and maintain prices.

5. Price fixing: The De Beers cartel also engaged in price fixing by setting a minimum price for diamonds. This prevented competition and ensured that prices remained stable.
