10. during the pandemic there are 400 passengers taking a bus route every day in b days there are 2000 passengers find b?algebraic equation and the solution


PROBLEM:

During the pandemic there are 400 passengers taking a bus route every day. In b days there are 2000 passengers, find b.

SOLUTION:

There are 400 passengers per day. Thus, a ratio of one is to four hundred. While, in b days, there are 2000 passengers with a ratio of b is to 2000.

• We'll apply what we have learned from the lesson about ratio and proportion.

[tex] \large \boxed{\begin{array}{c} \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \boxed{\tt \frac{1}{400} = \frac{b}{2000} }\\ \tt 1(2000) = 400b \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \tt 2000 = 400b \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \tt b = \frac{2000}{400} \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \boxed{ \tt b = 5}\end{array}}[/tex]

• Notice that we applied the cross-multiplication process.

• Our algebraic equation is 1/400 = b/2000 or simply, b = 2000/400.

ANSWER:

• In five days, there will be 2000 passengers.

22. the distance between the first and the last stop of a bus route is 2/3 km.there are 10 stops on this route.what is the average distance between the stops?​


The total distance between the first and last stop is 2/3 km.

Since there are 10 stops, there are 9 intervals between them.

The average distance between the stops is the total distance divided by the number of intervals:

Average distance = Total distance / Number of intervals

= (2/3) km / 9

= 2/27 km

Therefore, the average distance between the stops is 2/27 km.


23. A P2P bus route from point A to point B.A bus departed from point A at 12 noon with 44 passenger. An hour later, a few passenger got off at point B. The passenger who boarded the bus at point B were twice the number of passengers who got off the bus. If the bus 50 passenger now,how many passengers got off point B?​


Answer:

Let x be the number of passengers who got off the bus at point B.

Then the number of passengers who boarded the bus at point B is 2x (since they were twice the number of passengers who got off).

We know that the bus departed from point A with 44 passengers, so the total number of passengers before any stops is 44.

After an hour, the total number of passengers on the bus became 50. This means that during the hour, (50 - 44) = 6 new passengers boarded the bus.

We can now set up an equation using the information above:

44 - x + 2x + 6 = 50

Simplifying the equation, we get:

x = 4

Therefore, 4 passengers got off at point B.
