29. which bike run faster mountain bike or road bike​


Answer:

Generally speaking, a road bike is faster than a mountain bike on smooth, paved surfaces due to its lightweight frame and aerodynamic design.

Explanation:

Road bikes are specifically designed for speed and efficiency on paved roads, with narrow tires and a lightweight frame that allows for quick acceleration and high speeds.

Mountain bikes, on the other hand, are designed for off-road terrain, with wider tires and a heavier frame that offers more stability and durability on rough terrain. While mountain bikes can be ridden on paved roads, they are not as efficient as road bikes and may require more effort to maintain a high speed.

It's worth noting, however, that the speed of a bike ultimately depends on several factors, including the rider's skill level, the specific make and model of the bike, and the terrain being ridden on. In general, a road bike is better suited for speed on smooth roads, while a mountain bike is better suited for off-road terrain and more rugged conditions.
