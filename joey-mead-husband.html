9. What is Mead and The Development of Social Mind​


Answer:

"Mead and the Development of Social Mind" refers to the theories and concepts of the American philosopher and sociologist George Herbert Mead, who explored the relationship between individual self and society, and how this relationship influences the development of human consciousness and the social mind. Mead's work emphasizes the role of social interaction, language, and culture in shaping human behavior and thought.

According to Mead, individuals develop a sense of self and identity through social interaction with others. Through language and communication, individuals learn to take the perspectives of others and to understand how their own actions and behaviors affect others. Mead also emphasized the importance of symbols and symbolic interaction in shaping social reality and meaning.

Mead's ideas have been influential in the fields of sociology, psychology, and communication studies. His work has contributed to our understanding of how individuals develop a sense of self and how social structures and cultural norms influence human behavior and thought.
