24. if a certain number of grade IV students share 28 or 39 skill books in mathematics,there are remainders of 4 and 3 books what is the largest possible number of student​


The largest possible number of students is 12.

Step-by-step explanation:

Word Problem: Greatest Common Factor

The problem above is asking for the largest possible number of students. To find the answer, you have to find the greatest common factor of the numbers of books. The greatest common factor refers to the highest factor that the numbers have in common. There are two steps to find the answer.

Take away the remaining numbers of books from the total numbers of books.Find the greatest common factor of the numbers.

Solution:

Step 1: Take away the remaining numbers of books.

28 - 4 = 24

39 - 3 = 36

Step 2: Greatest Common Factor

List down the factors of each number.

24 - 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 24

36 - 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 9, 12, 36

G C F = 12

Final Answer:

The largest possible number of students is 12.

Checking:

28 ÷ 12 = 2 r. 4 ✔

39 ÷ 12 = 3 r. 3 ✔
