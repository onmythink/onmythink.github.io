Ano ang 1 Samuel 16:7?

Ngunit sinabi sa kanya ni Yahweh, “Huwag mong tingnan ang kanyang taas at kakisigan sapagkat hindi siya ang pinili ko. Si Yahweh'y hindi tumitingin nang katulad ng pagtingin ng tao. Panlabas na anyo ang tinitingnan ng tao ngunit sa puso tumitingin si Yahweh.”

Answer:

Questions:

1. Why do we call Samuel a judge, prophet, and priest of the Israelites?

-Samuel was an honest and fair judge, dispensing God's law impartially. As a prophet, he exhorted Israel to turn from idolatry and serve God alone. Despite his personal misgivings, he led Israel from the system of judges to its first monarchy.

2. Describe/who is Samuel.

- Samuel, Hebrew Shmuʾel, religious hero in the history of Israel, represented in the Old Testament in every role of leadership open to a Jewish man of his day—seer, priest, judge, prophet, and military leader. His greatest distinction was his role in the establishment of the monarchy in Israel.

3. Who is Saul?

- Saul, according to the Hebrew Bible, was the first monarch of the United Kingdom of Israel. His reign, traditionally placed in the late 11th century BCE, supposedly marked the transition of Israel and Judah from a scattered tribal society to organized statehood.

4. .What are his qualities that made him the chosen king of God for Israel?

- God Knew His Heart

5. What were the accomplishments of Saul and Samuel as leaders?

- Saul, Hebrew Shaʾul, first king of Israel. According to the biblical account found mainly in I Samuel, Saul was chosen king both by the judge Samuel and by public acclamation. Saul was similar to the charismatic judges who preceded him in the role of governing; his chief contribution, however, was to defend Israel against its many enemies, especially the Philistines.

27. 2 1. Samuel is six years older than Joan. The sum of their ages is 52. How old are Samuel and Joan?​


✏️AGES===============================

Problem: Samuel is six years older than Joan. The sum of their ages is 52. How old are Samuel and Joan?

Solution: Represent s and j as the ages of Samuel and Joan respectively. Make two equations of the given statement.

[tex] \begin{cases}s = j + 6& \green{(eq. \: 1)} \\ s + j = 52& \green{(eq. \: 2)} \end{cases}[/tex]

- Substitute s from the first equation to the second equation in terms of j which is the age of Joan.

[tex] \begin{cases}s = j + 6 \\ (j + 6) + j = 52 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}s = j + 6 \\ j + 6 + j = 52 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}s = j + 6 \\ 2j + 6 = 52 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}s = j + 6 \\ 2j = 52 - 6 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}s = j + 6 \\ 2j = 46 \end{cases}[/tex]

Wherefore it came to pass, when the time was come about, after

Hannah had conceived

Or, "at the revolutions of days" F2; at the end of a year, of a complete year, as Ben Melech, from their return from Shiloh; for it might be some time after their return that she conceived; or rather the sense is, that at nine months' end, the usual time of a woman's going with child from her conception, which is the date here given:

that she bare a son:

was brought to bed of a son:

and called his name Samuel, [saying], because I have asked him of the

Lord;

one would think rather his name should have been Saul, for the reason given; but, as Ben Gersom observes, givers of names are not always grammatically strict and critical in them, or in the etymology of them, as in the names of Reuben and Noah, in which he instances; and this may be the rather overlooked in a woman, than in a man of learning. According to Kimchi, it is as if it was Saulmeel; that is, "asked of God", and by contraction Samuel; but Hillerus F3 gives a better account of this name, and takes it to be composed of Saul-mul-el, "asked before God", "in the sight of God", "before the ark of God". This name Hannah gave her son (for sometimes the father, and sometimes the mother, gave the name) in memory of the wonderful favour and goodness of God in granting her request; and to impress her own mind with a sense of the obligation she lay under, to perform her vow, and to engage her son the more readily to give up himself to the service of God, when he reflected on his name, and the reason of it.

30. example for preparation for parenting in the verse 1 samuel 1:27?​


Answer

1 Samuel 1:27

"I prayed for this child, and the Lord has granted me what I asked of him."

Throughout years of infertility, Hannah faithfully kept on praying to God until God gave her and Elkanah a Son. They named him Samuel, who, in the years to come, became the prophet who anointed King Saul and King David. Hannah knew that without God, Samuel would have not been born. So, with this truth in her heart. She became a good parent by preparing Samuel, at a very young age, to serve and know more about the Lord in the Tabernacle at Shiloh.

Explanation

To further understand, let us discuss a little bit about Hebrew culture in the times of the Old Testament. First, It is a disgrace to have no Children because it would discontinue the bloodline. Second, being a servant in the Lord's Tabernacle is considered a great honor among the Israelites.

So, with this information, we could say that Hannah was preparing Samuel for the best life, serving and being friendly with the Lord.
