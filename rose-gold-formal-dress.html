4. The noble subanon rose up to the clouds with their servants, who came down again and told of a heaven of gold and light.


Legendary Tale of Sandayo

In the epic legend of Sandayo, the noble Subanon ascended to the sky with their attendants, who descended and described a gold and light-filled heaven. Unique in the land, their ceremonial buklog is elevated on a special, temporary flooring that must be taken down before dusk. In the buklog, everyone participates in a circle dance, which is a representation of their unity as a people and their unification with the heavenly diwata (spirits).

Through these rituals, life is asserted and reaffirmed, including not only the survival and health of the individual and the tribe but also the equilibrium and fertility of nature. This life then enables the birth of further lives, renewing the youth's longevity, vitality, and significance.

The indigenous Philippines festivals are permeated with the spiritual side of life, and Shamanistic dances can be found practically in there. This is even more apparent in the Subanon buklog when members of the community take part in the ritual of propitiation and celebration.

10. A piece of gold (density is 19.3 g/mL) is submerged into a graduated cylinder initially containing 120 mL of water. The water level rose to 145 mL. What is the mass of gold?


To determine the mass of the gold, we need to use Archimedes' principle, which states that the buoyant force on an object submerged in a fluid is equal to the weight of the fluid displaced by the object.

In this case, the buoyant force on the gold is equal to the weight of the water that was displaced when the gold was submerged in the graduated cylinder. This can be calculated by subtracting the initial volume of the water (120 mL) from the final volume of the water (145 mL), giving a volume of 25 mL of water displaced by the gold.

The mass of this water can be calculated using its density, which is 1 g/mL:

mass of water displaced = volume of water displaced x density of water

mass of water displaced = 25 mL x 1 g/mL

mass of water displaced = 25 g

Since the buoyant force on the gold is equal to the weight of the water displaced, we can calculate the weight of the gold by multiplying the mass of the displaced water by the ratio of the density of the gold to the density of the water:

weight of gold = buoyant force on gold = mass of displaced water x (density of gold / density of water)

weight of gold = 25 g x (19.3 g/mL / 1 g/mL)

weight of gold = 482.5 g

Therefore, the mass of the gold is 482.5 grams.
