14. How can you use the concepts of mean, variance and standard deviation of the probability distribution of discrete random variable in convincing a gambler to consider quitting from gambling?


The mean, variance, and standard deviation are important statistical measures that can be used to understand the behavior of a discrete random variable. By calculating these measures for a gambler's winnings or losses, it is possible to understand the expected outcome and the variability of the outcome. This information can be used to convince a gambler to consider quitting from gambling.

The mean of the probability distribution represents the expected value of the outcome, which is the average outcome if the game were played many times. If a gambler consistently loses more than they win, the mean of their winnings will be negative, indicating that they are likely to lose money in the long run.

The variance of the probability distribution represents the variability of the outcome. A high variance indicates that the outcome can vary greatly from the expected value, while a low variance indicates that the outcome is more predictable. For a gambler, a high variance in their winnings or losses can be seen as a warning that the outcome may be more unpredictable and uncertain.

The standard deviation of the probability distribution is the square root of the variance and represents the spread of the distribution. A large standard deviation indicates that the outcome can vary greatly from the expected value, while a small standard deviation indicates that the outcome is more predictable. For a gambler, a large standard deviation in their winnings or losses can be seen as a warning that their outcome may be unpredictable and uncertain.

In conclusion, by using the concepts of mean, variance, and standard deviation, a gambler can understand the expected outcome and the variability of their winnings or losses. If these measures indicate that the gambler is likely to lose money in the long run and that their winnings or losses are highly variable, this information can be used to convince the gambler to consider quitting from gambling.

30. It always rains when Dave is around. Don’t invite Dave."" This is an example of _____ Select one: a. Post Hoc b. Gambler’s Fallacy c. Circular Argument d. Ad hominem.


Answer:

The statement "It always rains when Dave is around. Don't invite Dave." is an example of a Post Hoc Fallacy.

Inferring that Dave must be the cause of the rain just because it always rains when Dave is around is an example of Post Hoc Fallacy since there is no scientific basis that Dave causes the rain and is just mere coincidence.

Explanation:

Post Hoc Fallacy

This is characterized by statements that imply one thing caused the other just because the proposed cause is followed by the the proposed effect.

Gambler's Fallacy

This is characterize by statements that imply that if an event occurred many times already, it is unlike to happen in the future.

Circular Argument

This is characterized by statements starting with the reasoning that they want to prove to be true.

Ad hominem

This is characterized by statements rejecting statement not because of proper argumentation/reasoning but rather because of (being against) the personality of who is making the argument.
