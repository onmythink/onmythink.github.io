1 hour = 60 minutes1 day = 24 hours.

Formula on getting the elapsed time:

End time minus start time

SOLUTION (WITH SIMPLE EXPLANATION):

5:30 AM - 2:00 PM = CAN'T DIVIDE BECAUSE IT HAS DIFFERENT MERIDIEMS (Ante Meridiem is different from Post Meridiem)

Change the end time by adding 12 (2:00 PM = 14:00)

14:00 PM - 5:30 = CAN'T DIVIDE BECAUSE THE 30 IN 5:30 IS BIGGER THAN 0 OF 14:00.

Burrow an hour (14:00 = 13:60 [1 hour is equal to 60 minutes])

Proceed on solving:

13:60 - 5:30 = 8:30 or 8 HOURS AND 30 MINUTES

*THE ONE ON THE LEFT (30 IN THE EXAMPLE) IS KNOWN AS THE MINUTES PASSED AND THE ONE ON THE RIGHT (8 IN TTH EXAMPLE) IS KNOWN AS THE HOURS PASSED.

Etymology of Vigan

There are two known etymologies for the name of Vigan. According to one theory, the Chinese settlers who arrived in the city before the Spanish colonization gave the name Vigan. It originated from the Chinese phrase “bee gan” which means “beautiful shores”. When the Spaniards came, they changed the name Bee Gan to Vigan, to suit their language. Another theory states that the name Vigan came from the biga’a plant, which is abundant in the area.

Explanation:

29. What is Vigan history 


Answer:

History of Vigan

Here are the most important dates in the history of Vigan, Ilocos Sur:

Pre-Spanish era – the city of Vigan is thriving, with Chinese merchants arriving from China. June 13, 1572 – Juan de Salcedo reached the city of Vigan. Later on, they founded the Villa Fernandina de Vigan. 1574 – the evangelization of the Ilocos Region began. December 10, 1941 – Japanese forces began invading Vigan. Spanish Era – the city of Vigan is populated by a mix of Spanish and Filipino settlers, with the Filipinos paying for taxes and tributes. 1999 – the city was listed as a UNESCO heritage site. December 7, 2014 – Vigan is chosen as one of the New7Wonders Cities of the world.
