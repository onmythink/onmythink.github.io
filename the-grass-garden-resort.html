Grass is to soil as seaweed is to_______

A) Water      B) Garden          C) Air            D) Pot  

The correct answer is letter A. Water While there is no specific description of "seaweed," it is commonly found in the ocean and can be seen with the naked eye. The definition encompasses all underwater flowering plants in the water, such as eelgrass, and larger marine algae. It belongs to one of three types of multicellular algae: red, green, and brown. They constitute a polyphyletic group since they have no single multicellular ancestor. Furthermore, bluegreen algae (Cyanobacteria) are reported in seaweed literature on occasion.

Seaweed ecology is dominated by two environmental requirements. There are seawater (or, at the very least, brackish water) and enough light to support photosynthesis. Another common necessity is an attachment point, so seaweed prefers the littoral zone (nearshore waters) and, within that zone, prefers rocky shores over sand or shingle. Furthermore, there are a few genera (for example, Sargassum and Gracilaria) that do not exist attached to the sea floor and instead float freely. Seaweed fills a variety of ecological niches. They are only wetted by the tops of sea spray at the surface, but certain species may bind to a substrate several meters deep. Littoral seaweed colonies may stretch for miles out to sea in certain places. Any species of red algae are the deepest living seaweeds. Others have evolved to survive in tidal rock pools. In this environment, seaweed must tolerate rapid changes in temperature and salinity, as well as occasional drying. Macroalgae and macroalgal detritus have also been found to be an important food supply for benthic species, as macroalgae shed old fronds. Benthic species use these macroalgal fronds in the intertidal region by the sea. Alternatively, pneumatocysts (gas-filled "bubbles") will hold the macroalgae thallus floating while the fronds are carried from the shore into the deep ocean by wind and currents. It has been shown that benthic species at depths of up to 100 meters use macroalgae remnants. Since macroalgae consume carbon dioxide and release oxygen during photosynthesis, macroalgae fronds may lead to carbon sequestration in the ocean as they drift offshore into deep ocean basins and sink to the sea floor without being remineralized by species. The relevance of this method for Blue Carbon storage is currently being discussed by scientists.

Answer:

12.56 m² of grass will cover the whole lot.

Step-by-step explanation:

In finding the area of a circle, we use the formula:

Area= π×(radius)²

Therefore,

Area=3.14 × (2m)²

Area=3.14 × 4m²

Area= 12.56 m²

Hence, the area of the circular garden will be 12.56 m².
