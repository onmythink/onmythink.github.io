10. Write just if the action of a character is reasonable and unjust if it is not. Happy admitted that it is all right to lie for someone’s convenience.


Answer:

Just

Explanation:

We were taught that lying is as bad as a crime.  However, some circumstances may demand us to lie.

Lies are acceptable if the motive is not selfish. For example, if someone asked you if they are beautiful, you could say, Yes, because they simply are and you want them to feel better and happy about themselves.  But if you answered, Yes, just to finish the conversation, then that is when the wrong motive enters.

Lying can also be diverted to become a constructive criticism instead.  For example, if an employee exhibits a low performance at work, it would be better to say this, “You are just starting but you can surely do better”.  It does not sound demeaning, rather it could even boost the employee’s motivation.
