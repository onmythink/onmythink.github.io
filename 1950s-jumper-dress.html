3. The time male bungee jumper is freely falling 1.5s. What is the velocity of the jumper at the end of 1.5s


#BrainlyFast

Problem:

The time male bungee jumper is freely falling 1.5 s. What is the velocity of the jumper at the end of 1.5 s?

Given Data:

Vi (initial velocity) = 0 m/s (start of free fall at maximum height zero velocity)

a (acceleration g) = 9.8 m/s²

t (time) = 1.5 s

Vf (final velocity) = ? unknown

Solution:

To solve the problem, we can use the formula Vf = at since Vi = 0 m/s.

Vf = at

   = 9.8 m/s² × 1.5 s

   =  14.7 m/s

Answer:

The velocity of the jumper at the end of 1.5 s is = 14.7 m/s

During free fall, the velocity of the bungee jumper from the maximum height will increased by 9.8 m/s every second as it falls back to the ground. In 1.5 s, the velocity of the bungee jumper going down is 14.7 m/s.
