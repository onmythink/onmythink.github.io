17. A person wants to know the occupant of one condominium. Research or inquiry?


The statement of "a person wants to know the occupant of one condominium" including inquiry approach. Here are explanations about inquiry and research.

Science education necessitates both the acquisition of scientific knowledge and the development of scientific reasoning abilities. Inquiry is a learning technique that comprises a process of examining the natural or material world, which leads to asking questions, producing discoveries, and evaluating those discoveries in the pursuit of new understanding. Inquiry in science education should be as close to the enterprise of performing genuine science as feasible. The curiosity, question, desire, or enthusiasm to comprehend an observation or solve an issue drives the inquiry process.

There are five steps of the inquiry approach.

Orientation. Its goal is to inspire people's interest and curiosity in the topic in question. The learning topic is introduced by the environment, offered by the teacher, or defined by the learner at this phase.Conceptualization. It is the process of comprehending a notion or concept associated with the stated problem.Investigation. It is the stage at which curiosity becomes action in order to answer the given research questions or hypotheses.Conclusion. During this stage, students address their initial research questions or hypotheses and determine if they are answered or supported by the study's findings.Discussion is divided into two parts: communication and reflection. Communication can be viewed as an external process in which students present and convey their discoveries and conclusions to others, as well as receive feedback and comments from those same persons.

Research, on the other hand, is a method by which they can test their own and each other's hypotheses by employing antagonism to find a solution and develop knowledge. The goal of the research is to continually correct and refine theories, which should result in the acceptance of certain empirical facts.
