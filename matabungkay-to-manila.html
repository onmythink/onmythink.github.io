Answer:

73.4°F

Using °C = (°F - 32)(5/9), substitute °F as 73.4

°C = (73.4 - 32)(5/9)

Subtract.

°C = 41.4(5/9)

Convert the decimal into a fraction.

°C = 414/10(5/9)

Multiply.

°C = 2070/90

Divide.

°C = 23

73.4°F = 23°C

6. Solve the ff. word problems using linear systems of equations in two(2) variables.(Kindly put answer with solution)1. Ian Pablo drove his car from Naga City to Iriga City at 81 kph. His car broke down and he returned by bus at 54 kph. His total travelling time was 5 hours. How long was his trip by car?2. Peter can paddle in still water at 6kph. The Matabungkay River is flowing at 2 kph. How far downstream can Peter travel if he must return in two hours?3. An airplane travels 3000 miles with a headwind in 5 hours. Then it turns and travels 3300 miles with the tail wind in 3 hours. Find the speed of the airplane and the speed of the wind.4. Two investments are made totaling Php 8800. For a certain year, these investments yield Php 1326 in simple interest. Part of the Php 8800 is invested at 16% and part at 14%. Find the amount invested at each rate.5. A mixture of peas and peanuts sells for Php 40 a kilo. The peanut sells for Php 42 a kilo while the peas sell for Php 36 a kilo. How many kilos of each kind are used in 12 kilos of the mixture?​


Let x be the time traveled by Ian by car (in hours). Then, the time traveled by bus is (5-x). The distance from Naga City to Iriga City is the same for both trips. Thus, we have:

Distance = Rate x Time

81x = 54(5-x)

Solving for x, we get:

x = 2.5

Therefore, the time traveled by Ian by car is 2.5 hours.

Let d be the distance downstream that Peter can travel (in km). Then, the distance upstream that he can travel is also d. We have:

d/6 + d/2 = 2

Solving for d, we get:

d = 2 km

Therefore, Peter can travel 2 km downstream.

Let s be the speed of the airplane (in mph) and w be the speed of the wind (in mph). We have:

s - w = 3000/5 = 600

s + w = 3300/3 = 1100

Adding the two equations, we get:

2s = 1700

Solving for s, we get:

s = 850 mph

Substituting s into one of the equations above, we get:

w = 250 mph

Therefore, the speed of the airplane is 850 mph and the speed of the wind is 250 mph.

Let x be the amount invested at 16% and y be the amount invested at 14%. We have:

x + y = 8800

0.16x + 0.14y = 1326

Multiplying the first equation by 0.16 and subtracting it from the second equation, we get:

0.02y = 446

Solving for y, we get:

y = 22300

Substituting y into the first equation, we get:

x = 65700

Therefore, the amount invested at 16% is Php 6570 and the amount invested at 14% is Php 2230.

Let x be the number of kilos of peanuts and y be the number of kilos of peas used in the mixture. We have:

x + y = 12

42x + 36y = 480

Multiplying the first equation by 36 and subtracting it from the second equation, we get:

6x = 72

Solving for x, we get:

x = 12

Substituting x into the first equation, we get:

y = 0

Therefore, 12 kilos of peanuts and 0 kilos of peas are used in the mixture.
