2. 1. What is the difference between Charles Darwin and Haeckel's tree of life? (10pts)​


Charles Darwin and Ernst Haeckel are both associated with the theory of evolution, but they made contributions in different ways.

Charles Darwin is most famous for his theory of evolution by natural selection, which he presented in his book "On the Origin of Species" published in 1859. In this book, Darwin proposed that species evolved over time through a process of natural selection, where the fittest individuals are more likely to survive and pass on their genes to the next generation. This theory provided a scientific explanation for the diversity of life on Earth and challenged the prevailing religious views of the time.

Ernst Haeckel, on the other hand, was a German biologist and philosopher who lived from 1834 to 1919. He was a strong advocate for Darwin's theory of evolution and made significant contributions to the understanding of evolution in the late 19th century. One of Haeckel's most famous works was his "Tree of Life" illustration, which he published in 1866. This illustration showed the relationships between different groups of organisms and was one of the first attempts to visualize the theory of evolution.

So, in summary, Darwin's theory of evolution by natural selection provides a scientific explanation for how species evolve over time, while Haeckel's tree of life illustration provides a visual representation of the relationships between different groups of organisms.
