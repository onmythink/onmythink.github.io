In spite of these acknowledged advances, teachers have numerous reservations and objections against the adoption of AI-based applications. In order to facilitate the proper use and produce an instrument in AI-based applications selection for EFL teachers, this study attempts to identify and assess factors affecting teachers’ adoption based on technology acceptance theories.​


Answer:

The article by Yunfei Du and Han Gao published in Education and Information Technologies in 2022 explores the impact of Artificial Intelligence (AI) on the teaching of English as a Foreign Language (EFL) and the factors that affect teachers' adoption of AI-based applications.

The authors acknowledge the revolutionary impact of AI on EFL teaching, but note that many teachers have reservations and objections against the adoption of AI-based applications. To address these concerns and facilitate the proper use of AI-based applications, the study attempts to identify and assess the factors affecting teachers' adoption based on technology acceptance theories.

The study uses a mixed-methods approach, including surveys and interviews with EFL teachers, to identify the factors affecting the adoption of AI-based applications. The authors identify several factors, including perceived usefulness, ease of use, compatibility, trust, and social influence, that affect teachers' adoption of AI-based applications.

The authors conclude that the adoption of AI-based applications in EFL teaching can be facilitated by addressing the factors that affect teachers' adoption, such as improving the perceived usefulness and ease of use of these applications, increasing compatibility with existing teaching practices, building trust in AI-based applications, and leveraging social influence to promote adoption.

Overall, the study provides valuable insights into the factors affecting the adoption of AI-based applications in EFL teaching and emphasizes the importance of addressing these factors to promote the effective use of AI in education.
