30. What is the mass of a shopping cart that is pushed with a net force of 10 N to accelerate at the rate of 0.5 m/s2?


Answer:

The mass of the shopping cart is 20 kilograms.

Explanation:

Force

The definition of force is: it is the pull or push on the an object because of the interaction of that object with another. Force is greatly affected by the mass of the object and its acceleration.

The mathematical expression of force is

F = m*a  where F stands for force m stands for mass of the object a stands for acceleration of the object the SI unit of force is kg-m/s² or Newton (N)Given:F = 10 Na = 0.5 m/s²Required:mass (m)Solution:F = m*aBy rearranging the equation for force, we can get the equation for the mass.m = F ÷ am = 10 N ÷ 0.5 m/s² m = 10 kg-m/s² ÷ 0.5 m/s²m = 20 kg

We can see from the unit analysis, the unit m/s² will cancel out; leaving us with the unit of mass which is kilograms.

Therefore, the mass of the shopping cart is 20 kilograms.
