27. what is habitual diner?


Answer:

Another term is "regular customer"

Explanation:

A habitual diner in a restaurant or cafe is a regular customer because habitual means that there is regularity in the dining habits of the customer. It means that one could easily predict that their customer would be there at a certain time because there is a pattern. For example, the customer dines or eats daily, hence, they could already presume that tomorrow the customer will appear and order food from them again.

Let us break this down:

habitual - refers to an action being done on a regular basisdiner - someone who consumes food in a restaurant. A diner could also refer to the restaurant itself.

Sample Sentence:

Maria was a habitual diner at JF Chicken, until she passed way. Your menu here is excellent. I think I will be a habitual diner!

In English, this kind of word formation is a compound noun composed of an adjective and a noun. Habitual is the adjective while Diner is the noun. Below  are some examples of a compound noun in the same category.

blackboardregular employeelight sabercommon sensefree taste
