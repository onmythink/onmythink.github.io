Word Problem

Analyze the problem.

a. What is asked?

How much more soil did Jessa add than Rina?

b. What is/are the given?

⅚ bag of soil and 1 ⅛ bags of soil

c. What is the operation?

Subtraction

d. What is the number sentence?

1 ⅛ - ⅚ = N

e. What is the answer?

Jessa added ‌7⁄24‌ bag of soil more than Rina.

Step-by-Step Explanation:

Subtraction of Fractions

Fractions represent a part of a whole. It has a numerator which tells the number of parts taken from a whole and a denominator which tells the total number of equal parts. Notice that the given fractions in the problem are dissimilar. This means that their denominators are not the same. Below are the steps to subtract the fractions.

Change the mixed number to an improper fraction by multiplying the denominator and the whole number, then add the numerator. Find their least common denominator. Divide the least common denominator by the denominator, times the numerator.Subtract the numerators and copy the denominator.

Solution:

1 ⅛ - ⅚ = N

Step 1: Mixed number to improper

1 ⅛ = ‌9⁄8‌

Step 2 and 3: L C D

L C D = 24

‌9⁄8‌ = 27⁄24

⅚ = 20⁄24

Step 4: Subtract

27⁄24 - 20⁄24 = 7⁄24

Final Answer:

7⁄24 bag of soil

Problems Involving Fractions:

a. Asked: The amount of soil Jessa have more than that of Rina.

b. Given: 5/6 -  bag of soil Rina had added to her garden

             1 1/8 - bags of soil Jessa had added to her garden

c. Operations: subtraction

d. Number Sentence: 1 1/8 - 5/6 = N

e. Answer: 7/24 bags

Solutions:

Express 1 1/8 as an improper fraction.

1 1/8 = (8)(1) + 1/8 = 8 + 1/8 = 9/8

Express 9/8 and 5/6 as similar fractions using their Least Common Denominator.

8 - 8, 16, 24

6 - 6, 12, 18, 24

Therefore, the least common denominator will be 24.

9/8 = (3)(9)/24 = 27/24

5/6 = (4)(5)/24 = 20/24

Subtract 20/24 from 27/24.

27/24 - 20/24 = 7/24

Therefore, Jessa have 7/24 bags of soil more than that of Rina.

How to Subtract Dissimilar Fractions

The given fractions above are examples of dissimilar fractions. Dissimilar fractions are fractions with different denominators. The problem is asking for the amount of soil that Jessa added more than Rina. The clue words in the given word problem above is "how much more". This means that we have to subtract their bags of soil.

These are the steps in subtracting dissimilar fraction.

Find their LCD or least common denominator.Make the fractions similar by dividing the LCD by the denominator, times the numerator.Subtract the numerators and copy the denominator.Simplify, if possible.

Solution:

Step 1: LCD

1 ⅛ = 8, 16, 24

⅚ = 6, 12, 18, 24

Step 2: LCD ÷ denominator × numerator

1 ⅛: 24 ÷ 8 × 1 = 3 -> 1 ‌3⁄24‌

⅚: 24 ÷ 6 × 5 = 20 -> 20⁄24‌

Step 3: Subtract

1 ‌3⁄24‌ - 20⁄24‌ = 27⁄24‌ - 20⁄24‌ = 7⁄24‌

Jessa added ‌7⁄24‌ bags of soil more than Rina.

28. Rose will plant tomatoes 1 meter apart along the edges of an 8 m by 7 m rectangular garden.How manh tomato plants are needed by Rose?


Answer: PA BRAINLIEST

Rose will need 29 tomato plants to plant along the edges of the rectangular garden.

Step-by-step explanation:

First, let's calculate the perimeter of the rectangular garden:

Perimeter = 2(Length + Width) = 2(8m + 7m) = 30m

This is the total length of the edges of the garden where Rose will plant the tomato plants.

Since Rose is planting the tomato plants 1 meter apart, she will need one less plant than the number of spaces between the plants. Thus, the number of tomato plants needed is:

Number of plants = (Perimeter / spacing) - 1

= (30m / 1m) - 1

= 29

Therefore, Rose will need 29 tomato plants to plant along the edges of the rectangular garden.
