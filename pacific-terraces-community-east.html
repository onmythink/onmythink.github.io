11. Predict what would happen if a 10-km asteroid hit the middle of the Pacific Ocean East of the Philippines.


Answer:

If a 10-km asteroid hit the middle of the Pacific Ocean east of the Philippines, it would cause a catastrophic event with far-reaching effects. The impact would trigger massive earthquakes, tsunamis, and volcanic eruptions that could affect the entire Pacific Rim. The force of the impact would release a tremendous amount of energy, equivalent to several million nuclear bombs exploding at once, and create a crater hundreds of kilometers wide.

The initial impact would cause a massive shockwave that would travel across the ocean and cause devastating tsunamis that could reach as far as the west coast of the Americas. The resulting waves could be as high as several hundred meters and cause widespread destruction and loss of life.

The impact could also trigger massive volcanic eruptions, as the shockwaves travel through the Earth's crust and disturb the magma chambers beneath the surface. The resulting ash clouds and pyroclastic flows could affect the global climate and cause significant environmental changes.

Overall, the impact of a 10-km asteroid in the middle of the Pacific Ocean east of the Philippines would be a catastrophic event with far-reaching effects on the planet and its inhabitants.

This geological feature is an example of what type of plate boundaries? A. convergent B. divergent C. transform D. reverse​


Answer:

The Mariana Trench, in the South Pacific Ocean, is formed as the mighty Pacific plate subducts beneath the smaller, less-dense Philippine plate. In a subduction zone, some of the molten material—the former seafloor—can rise through volcanoes located near the trench.

The East Pacific Rise is a mid-oceanic ridge, a divergent tectonic plate boundary located along the floor of the Pacific Ocean. It separates the Pacific Plate to the west from (north to south) the North American Plate, the Rivera Plate, the Cocos Plate, the Nazca Plate, and the Antarctic Plate.
