3. Prove that ∆MNX ≅ ∆MYX When ∠XYP ≅ ∠XNP, ∠NMX ≅ ∠YMX ​


Answer:

To prove that ∆MNX ≅ ∆MYX, we need to show that they have the same shape and size. In order to do this, we will use the given information that ∠XYP ≅ ∠XNP and ∠NMX ≅ ∠YMX.

We can start the proof as follows:

Draw a line segment XY and a point P on one side of it.

Draw a line segment MN intersecting XY at point X.

Connect points P and N with a line segment PN.

Connect points P and M with a line segment PM.

Now, we can use the Angle-Angle-Side (AAS) congruence postulate to prove that ∆MNX ≅ ∆MYX. This postulate states that if two angles and the included side of one triangle are congruent to two angles and the included side of another triangle, then the triangles are congruent.

Here is the proof:

∠NMX ≅ ∠YMX (given)

∠XYP ≅ ∠XNP (given)

MX = MX (common side)

∆MNX ≅ ∆MYX (AAS congruence postulate)

Therefore, we have proven that ∆MNX ≅ ∆MYX.

8. In a race, Jen runs uniformly at a speed of 8.0 m/s. Myx, one of the competing runners, starts from rest and accelerates at 0.5 m/s^2. If the track is 400 m long, who wins the race?


The winner is Myrx

Further explanation  

Linear motion consists of 2: constant velocity motion with constant velocity and uniformly accelerated motion with constant acceleration  

Jen's motion is included in constant velocity motion

Formula

[tex]\tt \boxed{\bold{d=v\times t}}[/tex]  

d=distance,m

v=velocity,m/s

t=time,m

Myrx's motion is included in uniformly accelerated motion with constant acceleration.  

Formula :

vf = vi + at

vf² = vi² + 2.a.d  

vf = final velocity  

vi = vo = initial velocity  

d = displacement

Given

Jen's velocity : 8 m/s

Acceleration of Myrx = 0.5 m/s²

Distance = 400 m

Required

The race winner

Solution

The winner of the race is the one who can cover the distance in the least / fastest time

Jen's time :

t = d/v

t = 400 m / 8 m/s

t = 50 s

Myrx's time :

vi = 0(from rest)

vf = vi + at

vf = at

vf = 0.5t

vf² = vi² + 2.a.d  

(0.5t)²=2 x 0.5 x 400

0.25t²=400

t=40 s

Myrx traveled 400 m faster than Jen, so Myrx was the winner
