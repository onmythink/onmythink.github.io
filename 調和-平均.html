Explanation:

In music, there are notes and rests. Notes determine the sound, while rests determine the pause. Here are some of the common notes that you’ll find in musical sheets:

Whole note – it stands for 4 beats Half note – it stands for 2 beats Quarter note – it stands for 1 beat Eighth note – it stands for 0.5 beat Sixteenth note – it stands for 0.25 beat Thirty-second note – it stands for 0.125 beat

The rests also follow the same format:

Whole rest – it stands for 4 beat pause Half rest – it stands for 2 beat pause Quarter rest – it stands for 1 beat pause Eighth rest – it stands for 0.5 beat pause Sixteenth rest – it stands for 0.25 beat pause Thirty-second rest – it stands for 0.125 beat pause

In addition, if you add a dot at the end of each note or rest, it signifies half the value of that note or rest. For example, if you put a dot beside a whole note, it would mean 6 beats, since a whole note have 4 beats and half of it is 2. So 2 plus 4 is equal to 6 beats.
