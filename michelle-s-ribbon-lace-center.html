12. Annie has [tex]\frac{7}{10}[/tex] m of lace. She wants to make 5 ribbons out of the lace for her dolls. How long will each ribbon be?


Answer:

7/50 meters each

Step-by-step explanation:

[tex] \frac{7}{10} \div 5[/tex]

Place 1 as a denominator of the second fraction

[tex] \frac{7}{10} \div \frac{5}{1} [/tex]

Get the reciprocal of the second fraction then change the sign to multiplication

[tex] \frac{7}{10} \times \frac{1}{5} [/tex]

Multiply the numerators and denominators together

[tex] \frac{7}{50} [/tex]

The length of each ribbon would be 7/50 meters each.

14. a circular table top has a diameter of 32 centimiters. how many lace of ribbon is needed to decorate it's edge ​


Answer:

100.531cm or 1.005m

Step-by-step explanation:

[tex]d = 32cm \\ r = \frac{d}{2} \\ r = \frac{32cm}{2} \\ r = 16cm \\ [/tex]

FORMULA FOR CIRCUMFERENCE (edge of circle):

[tex]c = 2\pi \: r[/tex]

solving for C:

[tex]c = 2\pi(16cm) \\ c = 100.531cm[/tex]

The circular table top needs 100.531cm or 1.005m to decorate its edge.
