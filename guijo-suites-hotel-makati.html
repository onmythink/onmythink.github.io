20. Juan Dela Cruz paid the Club Tara Suite ₱20,000 on August 20, 2021 for a one-week hotel accommodation to be consumed starting September 4, 2021. Suppose you are tasked to give a financial report at the end of August 2021. Explain why accrual basis of accounting will provide a better measure of income for Club Tara Suite.


The accrual basis of accounting is a more accurate method of calculating income for Club Tara Suite because:

The Club Tara Suite does not have to wait for actual cash receipts from customers before recording the revenue/income in their books. This is especially important for companies that offer credit terms to their customers. Instead, revenue is recognized when the Company has provided the goods or rendered the services, the Customer has accepted the goods or generated the benefits, and the Company is entitled to the money but has not yet received it.The period when a company receives payment from a customer does not necessarily coincide with when goods or services are supplied - crucial for businesses providing construction services under long-term contracts. The Company may require down payments and payments on a predetermined schedule for such arrangements. Still, receipt of charge from the Customer does not signify delivery of the Company's products or services. For instance, a $1 million down payment was received, but the Company has not yet begun providing services to the Customer, failing to provide the promised deliverables. So cash timing does not correspond to the delivery timing.

Nevertheless, the accrual method of accounting has limitations:

Cash is king, as we all know, and the 2008 Financial Crisis proves this.The Club Tara Suite can expand and generate profits, but without financial inflow, it cannot continue. For instance, banks may invest in and hold good bonds related to homeowners' mortgages, and as the housing market grew, the bond values soared, resulting in profits for the Banks. However, the value of these bonds plummeted when homeowners defaulted on their mortgages and failed to make the required loan payments.The Company has sold its goods or services, but the Customer has defaulted on payment; consequently, the Club Tara Suite has lost revenue. There are no buyers on the market for the Company's Private Equity or illiquid Financial Instrument. Even though the investment is fantastic, it cannot be converted into cash.Moreover, the accrual accounting method is subjective - when do you recognize revenue? This may seem straightforward, but this is not always the case. A company that builds shipyards, oil rigs, bridges, etc., and recognizes revenue on an accrual basis during the contract duration is an example of a construction service company. But concerns remain - should the Company identify the income, and if so, when? Even though the Company has completed a percentage of the contract and recognized the payment, there is no assurance that the project will be completed on time and by the Customer's terms, conditions, and specifications at the end of the project, resulting in risks of rejection by the Customer and previously recognized revenue that should not have been identified.
