7. 1. A rabbit with a black fur mated with a rabbit with white fur. What will be the outcome of their cross when the dominant (homozygous) trait is the rabbit with white fur, and the black fur is a heterozygous dominant allele? Find the following using Punnett square.​


Answer:

Solution

Verified by Toppr

Correct option is C)

According to the law of dominance, a trait is represented by two contrasting factors of a gene in a heterozygous individual while the homozygous individual carries two copies of same factors. Thus, the genotype of heterozygous rabbit will be Bb and that of homozygous recessive will be bb. Law of segregation states that the two factors for a trait, present together in a heterozygous individual (Bb), do not get mixed and are separated and delivered singly during gametogenesis. Thus, the heterozygous individual produces two types of gametes: 50% gametes with “B” and 50% with “b”. The homozygous recessive individual will make all gametes with “b” allele only. Fertilization of gamete with “b” allele from a recessive parent with that carrying “b” allele from the heterozygous parent will produce homozygous recessive progeny (bb); not “Bb”. Thus, the correct answer is option C.

In Rabbits, Gray hair (G) is dominant to white hair (g). Also in rabbits, black eyes (B) are dominant to red eyes (b). If a male rabbit with the genotype GGbb is crossed with a female rabbit with the genotype ggBb, what would be its Phenotype? Genotypic ratio? and Phenotypic ratio?​


Explanation:

1.

A.) PP x pp

Phenotype: All offspring will have purple flowers.

Genotype: All offspring will be heterozygous Pp.

B.) Pp x Pp

Phenotype: 3/4 of the offspring will have purple flowers, and 1/4 will have white flowers.

Genotype: 1/4 of the offspring will be homozygous dominant PP, 1/2 will be heterozygous Pp, and 1/4 will be homozygous recessive pp.

C.) PP x Pp

Phenotype: All offspring will have purple flowers.

Genotype: All offspring will be heterozygous Pp.

2.

If the heterozygous pea plant is represented as Yy, and the homozygous recessive plant is represented as yy, then the Punnett square for their cross would be:

| Y | y

--|---|--

y | Yy| yy

The probability of an offspring having green seeds is the same as the probability of an offspring inheriting the recessive y allele from both parents, which is 1/2 or 50%.

3.

The male rabbit has the genotype GGbb, which means it is homozygous dominant for gray hair and homozygous recessive for red eyes. The female rabbit has the genotype ggBb, which means it is homozygous recessive for gray hair and heterozygous for black eyes.

The Punnett square for their cross would be:

| Gg | Gg

--|----|---

b | GgBb| Ggbb

b | GgBb| Ggbb

Phenotype: All offspring will have gray hair and black eyes.

Genotypic ratio: 1/2 of the offspring will be GgBb and 1/2 will be Ggbb.

Phenotypic ratio: All offspring will have the same phenotype of gray hair and black eyes, so the ratio is 4:0 or 100%:0%.
