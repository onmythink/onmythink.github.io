15. Show and explain the parts of greek theater


All the parts of the Greek Theater

Orchestra- where performers dance or where the performance goes on
Theatron- where you could see or view the place
Skene- these are huts or tents where performers change
Thymele- Altar in the center of orchestra
Paraskenia- these are the side stages
Proskenion- porch area in front of skene. lower front area of skene in hellenistic times
Proedria- front row seats for political and religious dignitaries
Parados- the entrances both sides of skene for chorus to access orchestra
Theologeion- The roof of skene or upper acting area where gods appear
Logeion- upper stage floor for episkenion
Episkenion- upper story of skene
Thyromata- openings in the episkenion front
Diazoma- crosswalks of the theatron
Klimakes- stairways from top to bottom of the theatron
kerkis- a section of seating in the theatron
Pinakes- flats used to paint scenery on and inserted between pillars
Periaktoi- three flats that can turn into scenery
Ekkyklema- a sliding platform for revealing bodies inside the scenes
Mechane- a machine that swung gods into the view of Theologeion
