1. There are 700 toothpicks in a​ regular-sized box. If a jumbo box is made by doubling all the dimensions of the​ regular-sized box, how many toothpicks will the jumbo box​ hold? The jumbo box holds nothing toothpicks.


Step-by-step explanation:

Referring to the problem, there are 700 toothpicks inside the regular box. This means that the volume occupied by the 700 toothpicks would be the volume of the box. Further, it says that a jumbo box is made by doubling the dimensions of the regular box. Say we have x length, y width, and z height of the regular box as its dimensions. Doubling the dimensions would give us 2x length, 2y width, and 2z height as the jumbo box' dimensions

Solving the volume of the regular box, we get xyz, since volume is just length x width x height

Thus, the volume of the jumbo box is (2x)(2y)(2z) = 8xyz

If a volume xyz can contain 700 toothpicks, then the volume 8xyz would contain

(8)(700 toothpicks) = 5600 toothpicks

Answer:

Problem: Song receive a balikbayan box from his mother if the box measures 4 feet long 3 feet wide and 6 feet height. What is the volume of the balikbayan box?

Answer: [tex] \bold{\color{green}{\large 72 \ \ ft^3}} [/tex]

Formula:

[tex] \large {\boxed{\rm{\color{darkgreen}{V = l \times w \times h}}}} [/tex]

Given/s:

The length of the balikbayan box is 4 ft, the width is 3 ft and the height is 6 ft.

length (l) = 4 ft is the length of rectangular prism.

width (w) = 3 ft is the width of rectangular prism.

height (h) = 6 ft is the height of rectangular prism.

Find the volume of the balikbayan box!

Solution:

The formula: [tex] \large {\boxed{\rm{\color{darkgreen}{V = lwh}}}} [/tex]

[tex] \large \rm V = lwh [/tex]

[tex] \large \rm V = (4 \: ft) (3 \: ft) (6 \: ft) [/tex]

[tex] \large \rm V = (12 \: ft^2) (6 \: ft) [/tex]

[tex] \large {\boxed{\rm{\color{darkgreen}{V = 72 \: ft^3}}}} [/tex]

Therefore, the volume of the balikbayan box is [tex] \bold{\color{green}{\large 72 \ \ ft^3}} [/tex] (cubic feet).

10. if a person is pushing a balikbayan box with a force of 400 n and it accelerates at 5m/s² what is the mass of the balikbayan box​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The mass of an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{m = \frac{F}{a}}[/tex]

where:

F = force

m = mass

a = acceleration

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the mass

[tex]m = \dfrac{F}{a}[/tex]

[tex]m = \dfrac{\text{400 N}}{\text{5 m/}\text{s}^{2}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{m = \text{80 kg}}[/tex]

Therefore, the mass of the balikbayan box is 80 kg.

15. a balikbayan box with dimension 24 inches by 24 inches by 36 inches needed to be increases in size to hold three times as much material as the current box. what is the function that represents the volume of the new box?what are the dimensions of the new box?​


In the given problem, apply your knowledge in algebraic expression and basic mathematics especially in solving word problem about volume of rectangular prism.

Given Statement

A balikbayan box with dimension 24 inches by 24 inches by 36 inches

To be increases in size to hold three times as much material as the current box.

What is the function that represents the volume of the new box?

V = lwh

Increase three times

V = 3lwh

New Volume (V) = 3lwh

What are the dimensions of the new box?

V = lwh

V = (24)(24)(36)

V = 20 736 in³

Increase three times

20 736 × 3 = 62 208 cu.

V = (l + x)(w + x)(h + x)

62 208 = (24 + x)(24 +x)(36 + x)

62 208 = (576 + 48x + x²)(36 + x)

62 208 = 20736 + 576x + 1728x + 48x² + 36x² + x³

x³ + 84x² + 2304x - 41472 = 0

Solving x using synthetic division, x = 12 inch

l = 24 + x, l = 36

w = 24 + x, w = 36

h = 36 + 12, h = 48

Answer: 36 inches by 36 inches by 48 inches

28. The area of cube balikbayan box measures(x³+3x²+3x+1) cubic,What is the measure of the sides of the balikbayan box?-step by step​


Answer:

The volume of the cube balikbayan box is given by x³+3x²+3x+1. We want to find the measure of the sides of the cube, which are all equal in length.

Let s be the length of one side of the cube. Then, the volume of the cube is s³.

We can set these two expressions equal to each other and solve for s as follows:

s³ = x³+3x²+3x+1 (substitute s³ for the volume)

s³ - x³ - 3x² - 3x - 1 = 0 (subtract x³+3x²+3x+1 from both sides)

We can recognize the left-hand side of this equation as a difference of cubes:

(s - x)(s² + xs + x²) - 3x(s + 1) - 1 = 0

We can simplify this equation by dividing both sides by (s-x) and rearranging:

s² + xs + x² - 3x - 1/(s-x) = 0

We now have a quadratic equation in s, which we can solve using the quadratic formula:

s = (-x ± sqrt(x² - 4(x² - x² - 12x - 4)))/(2)

Simplifying under the square root:

s = (-x ± sqrt(x² + 12x + 16))/2

s = (-x ± (x + 4))/2

s = -x/2 + 2 or s = -x/2 - 2

Therefore, the measure of the sides of the balikbayan box is either -x/2 + 2 or -x/2 - 2. We cannot determine which of these values is correct without additional information about the problem.
