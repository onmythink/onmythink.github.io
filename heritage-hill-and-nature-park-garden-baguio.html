6. A car parked at the top of a hill does not move down the hill due to what type of friction?


Answer:

Static friction is friction between two objects that are not moving relative to each other. This frictional force is what prevents a parked car from sliding down a hill, for example. Before an object at rest on a surface can move, it must overcome the force of static friction.

ASEAN Heritage Parks (AHP) are selected protected areas in the ASEAN region that are known for their unique biodiversity and ecosystems, wilderness and outstanding values in scenic, cultural, educational, research, recreational and tourism.[1] Its vision is "An ASEAN region whose biological is conserved, sustainably managed and used, and equitably shared for the well-being of its peoples."[2] The ASEAN Centre for Biodiversity (ACB) in the Philippines serves as the secretariat of the ASEAN Heritage Parks Programme.

The ASEAN Heritage Parks were established as the ASEAN national heritage parks and nature reserves on 29 November 1984 when only 6 countries: Brunei, Indonesia, Malaysia, Philippines, Singapore and Thailand were member countries.[3] They became known by the present name on 18 December 2003 after Cambodia, Laos, Myanmar and Vietnam joined the organisation between 1995 and 1999. Through declarations, ASEAN member countries agreed that, "common cooperation is necessary to conserve and manage the parks for the development and implementation of regional conservation as well as regional mechanisms complementary to national efforts to implement conservation measures.[4]

14. What is the Chinese garden on baguio and explain


Answer:

BAGUIO CITY -- The city government and the Filipino-Chinese community drew an even closer relationship as the latter unveiled on Monday a beautified Filipino-Chinese Friendship Park inside the Botanical Garden here.

“Po”, the lovable Panda from the movie Kung Fu Panda, welcomed visitors with its life-size sculpture in kung fu stance in the middle of the 1,600-square meter park in Botanical Garden.

“The panda is a symbol of welcome,” said Peter Ng, chairperson of the 18th Filioino-Chinese Friendship Day executive committee celebration in Baguio.

People's Republic of China Consul Zhao Yubien, who is based in Laoag, Ilocos Norte, and Mayor Mauricio Domogan led the unveiling of the marker, as well as the ribbon cutting that officially opened the park to the public.

Likewise, they also led the planting of a Norfolk tree that they hope would usher in a better relationship not just with the Fil-Chinese community but with the People’s Republic of China, as well.

“We hope to foster a better relationship between your community and our country,” said the consul.

The Fil-Chinese park offers a different ambiance akin to that of the Middle Kingdom when one visits the Garden. It now has different themes representing the culture of the country, which adopted portions of the former Imelda Park (after the former First Lady), now named Botanical garden.

Ng said that they have actually finished two of the three phases of development introduced within the 1,600-square meter portion.

The first phase of the area’s development involved the construction of a pavilion and improvement of the pond that is now home to lotus plants and a garden.

This time, they have paved the remaining portion of the park, make the life size ”Po” the Panda as the silent immobile “maitre d'“ with the 12 animals of the Chinese zodiac sign as his companions. The concrete likeness some four feet high of the 12 mythical creatures - Rat, Ox, Tiger, Rabbit, Dragon, Snake, Horse, Goat, Monkey, Rooster, Dog and Pig – help serve as sentinels in the park.

A marker for each of the animal also explains what they symbolized.

Ng said that their group has already spent close to PHP4 million in developing the park that will showcase the culture of their forebears.

A third phase is still being mapped out by the Fil-Chinese community here, which involves the construction of a three-storey pagoda-like structure that will serve as a view deck - not just for the park but the entire garden that is also called the Centennial Park as well, introduced in 2009 when Baguio celebrated its centennial.

During the program for the celebration, the Fil-Chinese group also donated a 400cc Kawasaki motorcycle complete with all accessories like sirens for the Baguio City Police Office.
