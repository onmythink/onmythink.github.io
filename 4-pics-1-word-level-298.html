4. 1. How many moles are in 4.0L sample of gas at 0.79 atm and 298 K?​


Solution:

Using the formula of ideal gas law:

[tex]PV=nRT[/tex]

where P is the pressure, V is the volume, n is the number of moles, R is the ideal gas constant, T is the temperature.

Given:

[tex]P=0.79\text{ atm}\\V=4.0\text{ L}\\T=298\text{ K}\\R=0.082057\text{ L-atm/mol-K}[/tex]

Solving for n, we get

[tex]n=\dfrac{PV}{RT} =\dfrac{(0.79\text{ atm})(4.0\text{ L})}{(0.082057\text{ L-atm/mol-K})(298\text{ K})}\\\\\boxed{n=0.129\text{ mol}}[/tex]
