17. if ^ ABC ~= ^ADC, find the m/BAC.​


Answer:

If triangle ABC is similar to triangle ADC, then their corresponding angles are congruent. Therefore, angle BAC is congruent to angle DAC.

We can use this fact to find the measure of angle BAC, since we know that the sum of the angles in a triangle is 180 degrees. Let m be the measure of angle BAC. Then, we have:

m + m(DAC) + m(ACB) = 180

But because triangle ABC is similar to triangle ADC, we know that m(DAC) = m(CAB). Substituting this in, we get:

m + m(CAB) + m(ACB) = 180

And since angles CAB and ACB are complementary (they add up to 90 degrees), we have:

m + CAB + (90 - CAB) = 180

Simplifying this equation, we get:

m + 90 = 180

Subtracting 90 from both sides, we get:

m = 90

Therefore, the measure of angle BAC is 90 degrees.

25. what is the meaning of BAC​


Answer:

Blood Alcohol Concentration

Blood Alcohol Concentration (BAC) refers to the percent of alcohol (ethyl alcohol or ethanol) in a person's blood stream. A BAC of . 10% means that an individual's blood supply contains one part alcohol for every 1000 parts blood. In California, a person is legally intoxicated if he/she has a BAC of . 08% or higher.
