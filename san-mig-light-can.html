23. Miguel knows that ∆MIG and ∆JAN, MI = JA, IG = AN, and MG = JN. Which postulate or theorem can he use to prove the triangles congruent? ​


Answer:

SAS Theorem (Side-Angle-Side)

By applying the Side Angle Side Postulate (SAS), you can also be sure your two triangles are congruent. Here, instead of picking two angles, we pick a side and its corresponding side on two triangles.

The SAS Postulate says that triangles are congruent if any pair of corresponding sides and their included angle are congruent.

30. Migs and Bert have a total of $80. After Migs spent $5.25 on chips, he is left with 4 times as much as Bert. How much does Bert have?


Answer:

$59.8

Step-by-step explanation:

Migs + Bert = $80

Migs spent $5.25 of the $80. Therefore subtract $5.25 from $80. The answer is $74.75

Migs = 1 and Bert = 4. The ratio given is now 1:4.

Add the digits in the ratio. 1 + 4 = 5

$74.75 divided by 5 is equal to $14.95. Migs = $14.95

Bert has 4 times as much as Migs. Therefore, $14.95 times 4. The answer is $59.8.

Therefore, Bert has $59.8.
