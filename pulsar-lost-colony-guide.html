11. what defines pre-colonial, colonial, post-colonial or contemporary literature?​


Post colonialism literature is the literature by people from formerly colonized countries.[1] It exists on all continents except Antarctica. Postcolonial literature often addresses the problems and consequences of the decolonization of a country, especially questions relating to the political and cultural independence of formerly subjugated people, and themes such as racialism and colonialism.[2] A range of literary theory has evolved around the subject. It addresses the role of literature in perpetuating and challenging what postcolonial critic Edward Said refers to as cultural imperialism.[3]

14. Talking about SPEED. Light: 1.Define Speed of light 2.How fast does can light travel? 3.Multiply it to 1 hour. Pulsar: 1. Whats difference between Pulsar and Quasar 2. How is pulsar formed? 3. What if two Quasar collided? Quasar Jet: 1. How fast Quasar Jets are? 2. Are they faster than light? 3. Define it.


Light:

1.The speed of light is the rate at which light travels in a vacuum. It is a physical constant that is denoted by the symbol "c" and has a value of approximately 299,792,458 meters per second.

2.Light travels at a speed of approximately 299,792,458 meters per second.

3.When multiplied by 1 hour, the speed of light is approximately 1,079,252,848.8 kilometers per hour.

Pulsar:

1.Pulsars and quasars are both astronomical objects, but they are different in several ways. Pulsars are highly magnetized rotating neutron stars that emit beams of electromagnetic radiation. Quasars, on the other hand, are extremely luminous active galactic nuclei that are powered by supermassive black holes.

2.Pulsars are formed from the remnants of supernova explosions. When a massive star runs out of fuel, it undergoes a catastrophic collapse that can result in the formation of a neutron star. If this neutron star is rotating and has a strong magnetic field, it can emit beams of radiation that can be detected by telescopes on Earth.

3.It is difficult to predict what would happen if two quasars collided, as these objects are extremely far apart and are not known to interact with each other in any significant way.

Quasar Jet:

1.Quasar jets are extremely fast, with some moving at speeds close to the speed of light. However, the exact speed of these jets can vary depending on the object in question.

2.While quasar jets can approach the speed of light, they cannot exceed it, as this would violate the laws of physics.

3.Quasar jets are narrow beams of highly energetic particles that are emitted from the centers of some galaxies. They are thought to be powered by supermassive black holes that are actively accreting matter.

By pre-colonial, I take it that you are referring to colonizing of North America. Circa 1500.

Science prior to about 1650 was the science of the ancient Greeks. They introduced rational thought, but testing was out of the question. No value was placed on experiments. It was all thought. The church picked up this method and included it in its search for knowledge. Galileo and others suffered because they challenged the church with their observations and experiments.

Modern science grew in the late 1600s until the 1700s by valuing experimental evidence and repeated observation and testing. The Royal Society’s motto was (and still is) Nullius in Verba. On the word of no man. Skepticism was introduced to the search for knowledge. It was a big leap from the positive logic of the Greeks. The Science of the 1700s and 1800s was hands-on, from Newton to Faraday.

The results of the Scientific Revolution are all around you. Massive increases in animal and plant husbandry (food). Climate controlled living environments (just try living in Tucson without air conditioning). Transportation, communication, health care (people used to live 40 years, now it is 80 years). This is what I have observed about science and technology from 1500 until about 1990.
