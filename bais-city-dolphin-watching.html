1.Like every mammal, dolphins are warm blooded. Unlike fish, who breathe through gills, dolphins breathe air using lungs. ... Whales and porpoises are also mammals. There are 75 species of dolphins, whales, and porpoises living in the ocean.

2.Dolphins give birth to a single baby; the baby is usually born tail first (unlike most mammals) to minimise the risk of drowning.

3.Although dolphins have been documented nursing their offspring for up to ten years after birth, most dolphins feed their babies milk for 2 to 3 years. Dolphin milk is extremely rich and fatty compared to human milk or cow's milk, and dolphin babies grow quickly.
