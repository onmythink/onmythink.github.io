3. A torchlight fell from a watch tower. The potential energy of the torchlight at the highest point compared to its kinetic energy at the lowest point is _______ A. lesser. B. equal. C. greater. D. not related.


B. Equal

Sol'n:

[NOTE: Here, we assume that there are no other factors or forces ]

Let us assume that the torchlight was initially at rest and fell from the tower. Since it was initially at rest, the kinetic energy at the highest point was 0 and its potential energy was at its maximum. Now consider it hitting the ground i.e. its lowest point. The potential energy would be 0 because there is no defining height to be considered. The kinetic energy however, would be at its maximum because as the torch falls, its potential energy decreases and its kinetic energy increases thus following the law of conservation of energy.

In mathematical terms, we can define the maximum point as PEx and KEx and the lowest point as PEy and KEy.

[tex] PE_{x} KE_{x} = PE_{y} KE_{y} [/tex]

At the maximum point, the velocity is 0 because it is initially at rest. Then that would mean that KEx = 0. Then think about the lowest point. Since there is no defining height to be considered at the lowest point, we also say that PEy = 0.

Therefore:

[tex] PE_{x} = KE_{y} [/tex]

Proving here that the PE of the torchlight at its maximum point is equal to the KE of the torchlight at its lowest point.
