23. zed is playing with a ball on top of a building but the ball fell and hits the ground after 2.6 seconds what is the final velocity of the ball just before it hits the ground and how high is the building?


For the sake of the question, we're gonna assume that the ball initially started from rest and fell freely. This makes our equations a lot more convenient since we're gonna have a zero initial velocity, or expressed in equation terms, [tex]v_i = 0[/tex]


Getting the final velocity:

For the first question, we're gonna use this equation:

[tex]v_f^2 = v_i^2 + gt[/tex]

where [tex]v_f[/tex] is the final velocity,

[tex]g[/tex] is the acceleration due to gravity, at [tex]9.8 m/s^2[/tex]

and [tex]t[/tex] is the time in seconds.

Now remember, since we're assuming the fall started from rest, we can cancel out [tex]v_i[/tex] from that equation. That leaves us with:

[tex]v_f^2 = gt[/tex]

we take the square root of both sides, and then plug in values:

[tex]\sqrt{v_f^2} = \sqrt{gt}[/tex]

[tex]v_f = \sqrt{gt} = \sqrt{(9.8 m/s^2)(2.6 s)} = 5.05 m/s[/tex]

Therefore, the ball was moving at a velocity of [tex]5.05 m/s[/tex] before hitting the ground.


Getting the height of the building:

For this one, we'll use this equation:

[tex]h = v_i t + \frac{1}{2}gt^2[/tex]

where, we already know all the variables to, so we go on to solve it right away.

Again, we keep in mind that [tex]v_i[/tex] is zero, so that leaves us with just:

[tex]h = \frac{1}{2}gt^2[/tex]

Now we plug in values to solve for height:

[tex]h = \frac{1}{2}gt^2 = \frac{1}{2}(9.8 m/s^2)(2.6 s)^2[/tex]

[tex]h =33.124 m[/tex]

So finally, the building was [tex]33.124 m[/tex] tall.
