2.The more practical definition of temperature is the average kinetic energy of all molecules in an object.Since molecules gaining thermal energy increases their speed of vibration and therefore kinetic energy,a gain of thermal energy is accompanied by gain in temperature.Since the water gains thermal energy by heat transfer,they become more violent in vibrating,thereby increasing both their thermal energy and temperature.

3.According to the laws of thermodynamics,energy always flow from higher entropy to lower entropy.In simple terms,a hot object has more entropy since its molecules are vibrsting violently and thereby scattered all around,and a cold object have lower entropy since its molecules are much slower moving and thereby much more organized.Since the laws of thermodynamics take effect on any energy transfer,the heat from the boiling water will be transferred into the cold metal via colliding with each other.The hot water molecules collide with cold metal's molecules,thereby transferring its energy until an equilibrium is reached or satisfied.

4.In density,the term refers to unit of mass per unit volume.It means the bigger the volume,the lesser the density and vice versa.When we heat up objects,the molecules vibrate much faster and therefore occupy more space,giving birth to a phenomenon called Thermal Expansion.Since the vibrating molecules occupy more space,more volume is added regardless of mass.In the case of air,the air absorbs energy from Sun's radiation and therefore vibrate more violently,undergoing thermal expansion and expanding its volume.We know that less dense objecsmts float above more dense objects.Since hot air has bigger volume,its density compared to the surrounding air is lesser,thereby it floats.On the other hand,the atmosphere lowers in temperature as altitude increases,so the once hot air becomes colder,and sinks back to the ground.After they reach the ground,they are heated up again and the cycle repeats,which gives birth to air circulation.

5.For a phase change to occur,a molecule must have absorbed enough heat to overcome the attraction of its neighboring molecules,thus breaking stronger cohesion into weaker cohesion.This happens by absorbing energy,in any way.Since molecules vibrate more violently as more energy is absorbed,it will eventually reach a limit where its cohesion will break,transforming it into other state of matter or phase.In a case of wet clothes,the water in the clothes is slow moving.After being exposed to sunlight,they absorb energy,and if long enough,they will gain enough energy to undergo phase change from liquid to gas.
