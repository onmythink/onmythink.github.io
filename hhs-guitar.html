26. What will be the hair type of offspring with parents having a straight hair (HH) and Kinky hair(hh)​


Answer:

If one parent has straight hair and the other parent wavy hair, what is the phenotype (and genotype) ratio for their offspring?

There are multiple factors that go into the way hair looks (phenotpe, which is how the genes are expressed, or “look"), but if straight hair is dominant and wavy hair is recessive, which is basically how it works, let's assign “H” for the straght hair gene and “h” for the wavy hair gene.

The wavy-haired parent would have to have a genotype of hh, since the phenotype (wavy hair is the result of a recessive gene, and they would have to have the genotype (one from each parent) be hh. The parent with the phenotype of straight hair could have a genotype of HH or Hh, because there only has to be one dominant gene to have straight hair. So the genotypic and phenotypic results possible are:

HH x hh = 100% Hh genotype, 100% straight hair phenotype.

Hh x hh= 50% chance would be Hh genotype and straight hair phenotype, 50 % hh genotype and wavy hair phenotype.

Curly hair (C) is incompletely dominant over straight hair (S). The heterozygous genotype expresses a wavy hair phenotype. A man with curly hair marries a female with straight hair. What is the genotypic and phenotypic ratio of their children?

Assuming it’s as simple as you suggest (I very much doubt it is, but for the sake of argument), then the curly-haired man would have to be CC, the straight-haired woman SS, so their children would all be wavy-haired.

For completeness, a wavy-haired person with a curly-haired person would produce half curly-haired and half wavy-haired children, and similarly a wavy-haired and straight-haired person would produce half wavy-haired and half straight-haired. Finally, two wavy-haired people would produce one quarter straight-haired, one quarter curly-haired and one half wavy-haired children.
