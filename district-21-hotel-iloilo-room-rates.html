5. The number of rooms at Hotel G is 10 less twice the number of rooms at Hotel H. if the total number of rooms at both Hotels is 425, what is the number of rooms at Hotel G?


Answer:

Since G is equal to 10 less than twice H, we get:

G = 2H - 10

Since the total number of rooms at G and H is 425, we get:

G + H = 425

H = 425 - G

Substituting H = 425-G into G = 2H - 10, we get:

G = 2(425-G) - 10

G = 850 - 2G - 10

3G = 840

G = 280

Answer:

Using the given information:

Number of occupied rooms = 80

Total number of rooms = 100

ADR = Php6,500

To calculate the Occupancy Percentage:

Occupancy Percentage = Number of occupied rooms / Total number of rooms x 100

Occupancy Percentage = 80 / 100 x 100

Occupancy Percentage = 80%

To calculate the Room Revenue:

Room Revenue = ADR x Number of Rooms Occupied

Room Revenue = Php6,500 x 80

Room Revenue = Php520,000

To calculate the Average Daily Rate (ADR):

ADR = Total Room Revenue / Number of Rooms Occupied

ADR = Php520,000 / 80

ADR = Php6,500

To calculate the Revenue per available room (RevPAR):

RevPAR = ADR x Occupancy Percentage

RevPAR = Php6,500 x 80%

RevPAR = Php5,200

18. The number of rooms at Hotel G is 10 less twice the number of rooms at Hotel H. if the total number of rooms at both Hotels is 425, what is the number of rooms at Hotel G?


Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

Define x:

Let the number of rooms in Hotel H be x

Hotel H = x

The number of rooms at Hotel G is 10 less twice the number at Hotel H:

Hotel G = 2x - 10

Given that the total number of rooms is 425:

x + (2x - 10) = 425

x + 2x - 10 = 425

3x - 10 = 425

3x = 435

x = 145

Find the number of rooms in Hotel G:

Hotel G = 2x - 10

Hotel G = 2(145) - 10

Hotel G = 280

Answer: There are 280 rooms in Hotel G.
