Suspensions mixture:

A suspension is a complex blend of a finely distributed solid in a liquid. In contrast to a salt solution in water, the solid does not dissolve in the liquid.

List:

- muddy water

- sand and water

- paint water

- oil and water

- vegetable soup

- gulaman

- flour and water

- pineapple shake

Colloids mixture:

In simple terms, we can describe colloids as a mixture where one of the substances is split into relatively minute particles dispersed during a second substance. Colloidal particles are the technical term for tiny bits of matter.

List:

- mayonnaise

- whipped cream

- yoghurt

- cheese

- peanut butter

- ice cream

True Solutions:

A suitable solution is a homogenous mixture of two or more substances in which the particulate size of the compound dissolved (solute) in the solvent is less than [tex]10^{-9}[/tex] m or 1 nm.

List:

- seawater

- apple juice

- cement

- soft drink

- vinegar

- stainless steel
