10. a multicab racing 1000 kg is moving at the speed of 5m what is the kinetic energy of the multicab​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The kinetic energy of a moving object is given by

[tex]\boxed{KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^{2}}[/tex]

where:

KE = kinetic energy

m = mass

v = speed

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the kinetic energy

[tex]KE = \dfrac{1}{2}mv^{2}[/tex]

[tex]KE = \dfrac{1}{2}(\text{1,000 kg})(\text{5 m/s})^{2}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{KE = \text{12,500 J}}[/tex]

Therefore, the kinetic energy of the multicab is 12,500 J.

19. why is there a need to establish multigrade classes?describe a multigrade class.​


Answer:

There is a need to establish multigrade classes in order to address the issue of small and isolated schools, particularly in rural areas, where it is not feasible to have separate classes for each grade level due to lack of resources and student population. Multigrade classes are classes where students of different grade levels are taught together by one teacher. This allows for more efficient use of resources and allows for students in these small communities to have access to education.

A typical multigrade class would have students from different grade levels, such as grades 1-3, in one class. The teacher would be responsible for teaching the curriculum for all of these grade levels, often using different teaching methods and strategies to cater to the diverse needs of the students. The teacher would also have to be proficient in different subjects and have the ability to differentiate instruction to help each student reach their full potential.
