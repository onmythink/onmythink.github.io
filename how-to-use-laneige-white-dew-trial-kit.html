20. How to conduct soil analysis using the Soil Test Kit?


Answer:

Put on garden gloves. Dig a hole into a dry area of the soil to be tested with a trowel to a depth of 4 inches. Do not touch the soil with your bare hands as it could contaminate the soil. Collect a small amount of soil from the side of the hole at least 3 inches from the surface.

2

Break up the soil with the trowel and your gloved hands to separate and dry the soil naturally. Remove any stones or plant material.

3

Fill the pH tube in the soil analysis kit with the amount of soil and distilled water specified in the instructions in a soil analysis kit. Compare the results with the soil analysis chart in the kit -- usually a color-based scale.

4

Fill nitrogen, phosphorus and potash testing tubes with soil mixed with distilled water, following the instructions in your kit. Compare the results with the soil analysis chart in the kit.
