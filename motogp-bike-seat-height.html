6. A cart is loaded with a brick and pulled with a mass of the loaded cart is 5.0 kg and the height of the seat top is 0.75 meters, which of the following is the potential energy of the loaded cart at the height of the seat top?​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The potential energy of an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{PE = mgh}[/tex]

where:

PE = potential energy

m = mass

g = acceleration due to gravity = 9.8 m/s²

h = height

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for PE

PE = mgh

PE = (5.0 kg)(9.8 m/s²)(0.75 m)

[tex]\boxed{PE = \text{36.75 J}}[/tex]

Therefore, the potential energy of the loaded cart is 36.75 J.

16. a wheelbarrow is loaded with gravel and pulled at a constant speed along an inclined plane to the height of a seat top. if the mass of the loaded cart is 5.0 kg and the height of the seat is 1.2 meters, what is the potential energy of the loaded wheelbarrow?​


POTENTIAL ENERGY WORD PROBLEMSProblem:

A wheelbarrow is loaded with gravel and pulled at a constant speed along an inclined plane to the height of a seat top. if the mass of the loaded cart is 5.0 kg and the height of the seat is 1.2 meters, what is the potential energy of the loaded wheelbarrow?​

Solution:

To solve for the potential energy of the loaded wheelbarrow, we can use the formula: PE = mgh, where: m stands for the the mass in kilograms, g stands for the acceleration due to gravity of the Earth equal to 9.8 meters per square second and h stands for the height of the body in meters. The unit for potential energy is joule (J).

Substituting the given values:

PE = mgh

PE = (5)(9.8)(1.2)

PE = 58.8

Therefore, the potential energy (PE) of the loaded wheelbarrow with a mass of 5-kg and height of 1.2 meters from the ground is 58.8 Joules.
