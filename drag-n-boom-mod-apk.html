Problem:The drag force on a boat varies jointly as a wetted area and the square of the velocity of a boat. If a boat going 6.5 mph experiences a drag force of 86 N when the wetted surface area is 41.2 ft², how fast must a boat with 28.5 ft² of wetted surface area goes in order to experience a drag force of 94 N?​

Solution:

F = force

A = Area of wetted surface

V = velocity of boat

k = constant of variation

F = 86N ; V = 6.5 mph ; A = 41.2 ft²

F = kAV²

86 = k(41.2)(6.5)²

[tex]\[\begin{array}{l}F = kA{V^2}\\\\\frac{{86}}{{(41.2){{(6.5)}^2}}} = k\\\\k = \frac{{86}}{{(41.2)(42.25)}}\\\\k = \frac{{86}}{{1740.7}}\\\\k = {\rm{0}}{\rm{.049405411616}}\end{array}\][/tex]

[tex]\[\begin{array}{l}F = kA{V^2}\\\\\frac{F}{{kA}} = \frac{{kA{V^2}}}{{kA}}\\\\{V^2} = \frac{F}{{kA}}\end{array}\][/tex]

[tex]\[V = \sqrt {\frac{F}{{kA}}} \][/tex]

if

A = 28.5 ft²

F = 94 N

k = 0.049405411616

V = ?

[tex]\[\begin{array}{l}V = \sqrt {\frac{F}{{kA}}} \\\\V = \sqrt {\frac{{94}}{{0.0494(28.5)}}} \\\\V = \sqrt {\frac{{94}}{{{\rm{1}}{\rm{.408054231056}}}}} \\\\V = \sqrt {{\rm{66}}{\rm{.758792}}} \\\\V = {\rm{8}}{\rm{.1706}}mph\end{array}\][/tex]

Answer:V = 8.17 mph

22. Prove that ∀n∈N, 10ⁿ + 3 × 4ⁿ + 5 ≡ 0 (mod 9).


Answer:

Note that we can rewrite 10ⁿ + 3 × 4ⁿ + 5 as:

[tex]\rm 10^n + 3 \times 4^n + 5 = (1 + 9)^n + 3 \times (1 + 3)^n + 5[/tex]

Binomials in the form (1 + x)ⁿ can be expanded as:

[tex]\begin{aligned} \rm (1+x)^n & =\rm \binom{n}{n} x^0 + \binom{n}{n-1}x^1 + ... + \binom{n}{2}x^{n-2} + \binom{n}{1}x^{n-1} + x^n \\ &\rm = x^n + \binom n 1 x^{n-1} + \binom n 2 x^{n - 2} + ... + \binom{n}{n-1}x^1 + \binom{n}{n}x^0 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Here, we can expand (1 + 9)ⁿ + 3 × (1 + 3)ⁿ + 5 as:

[tex]\begin{aligned} \rm (1+9)^n+3\times(1+3)^n+5 & =\rm 9^n+\binom{n}{1}9^{n-1}+\binom{n}{2}9^{n-2}+...+\binom{n}{n-1}9^1+\binom{n}{n}9^0\\&\rm \ +3(3^n+\binom{n}{1}3^{n-1}+...+\binom{n}{n-1}3+1)+5\\& =\rm 9^n + \binom{n}{1} 9^{n-1}+... +9n+1\\& \rm \ +3(3^n+\binom{n}{1}3^{n-1}+...+3n+1)+5\\& \rm = \big[9^n+\binom{n}{1}9^{n-1}+...+9n\big]+\big[3^{n+1}+\binom{n}{1}3^n+...+9n\big]+9\\& \rm=9\big[9^{n-1}+\binom{n}{1}9^{n-2}+...+n\big]\\& \rm \ \ +9\big[3^{n-1}+\binom{n}{1}3^{n-2}+n\big]+9 \end{aligned}[/tex]Since (1 + 9)ⁿ + 3 × (1 + 3)ⁿ + 5 is the sum of multiples of 9, 10ⁿ + 3 × 4ⁿ + 5 is divisible by 9.

When a number n is divisible by k, then n ≡ 0 (mod k).

∵ 10ⁿ + 3 × 4ⁿ + 5 is divisible by 9, ∴ 10ⁿ + 3 × 4ⁿ + 5 ≡ 0 (mod 9).
