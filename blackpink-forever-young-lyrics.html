5. "The young man was a freeman forever chained in poverty" What is the figurative language present in this statement? (please answer seriously i need help...)


The figurative language present in the statement "The young man was a freeman forever chained in poverty" is a metaphor.

A metaphor is a figure of speech that compares two unlike things by speaking of one thing in terms of the other, implying a similarity between the two. In this statement, the young man is being compared to a freeman who is forever chained in poverty, implying that he is free in name only, but in reality, he is limited and confined by his poverty.

Metaphors are used to create vivid and imaginative comparisons that can help the reader or listener to better understand the meaning of the text. In this case, the metaphor of the young man being a freeman forever chained in poverty effectively conveys the idea that despite being free in name, he is still limited by his poverty, which holds him back and restricts his ability to live life to the fullest.
