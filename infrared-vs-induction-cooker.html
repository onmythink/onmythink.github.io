1. what is induction cooker?​


Answer:

An induction cooker passes electrical energy into a metal vessel that has to be ferromagnetic by induction from a coil of wire. Under the cooking surface, the coil is mounted, and a high frequency alternating current is passed through it (e.g. 24 kHz). A complex magnetic field is generated by the current in the coil.

2. induction cooker. where electromagnetism is applied​


Answer:

An induction cooktop uses an electromagnetic field to heat food. When the cooktop is turned on, an electric current passes through a coil of metal, creating a magnetic field. This magnetic field then penetrates the metal of a cooking pan, inducing a current in the pan

21. How does an induction cooker differ from a gas range? How do they transfer heat for cooking?​


Answer:

1. A major difference between a gas and induction stovetop is that induction is significantly more efficient than gas – food being cooked with induction will receive 90% of the heat generated, as opposed to only 40-55% for gas. This keeps your kitchen much cooler and more comfortable while you prepare meals.

2. If you're heating a solid substance, this heat energy is transferred throughout the food through conduction, while liquids do so through convection. Microwave heat transfer usually cooks food faster than infrared radiation, as it is able to penetrate foods several inches deep.

22. A. Give brief descriptions of the following words: 1. Manifestoes 2. Arguments 3. Reasoning 4. Evidence 5. Appeal B. Compare and Contrast Deductive vs Inductive arguments ________________________________________________________ ​


A.

1. Manifestoes are public declarations of principles, policies, or intentions, often political in nature.

2. Arguments are statements or sets of statements that are presented in support or opposition to a proposition or claim.

3. Reasoning is the process of thinking about something in a logical, systematic way in order to form a conclusion or judgment.

4. Evidence refers to the data or information that is used to support a claim or proposition.

5. An appeal is a request or plea for help or support, often made to a higher authority or to the public at large.

B. Deductive arguments are those in which the premises are intended to provide conclusive support for the conclusion. In other words, the premises are supposed to guarantee the truth of the conclusion. Inductive arguments, on the other hand, are those in which the premises are intended only to provide strong support for the conclusion. The truth of the premises in an inductive argument only makes the conclusion more likely, but does not guarantee it.
