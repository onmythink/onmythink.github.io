11. Ashima, Bharti and camella are seated at A(3,1), B(6,4) and C(8,6) respectively. Do think they an seated is a line? Give reasons for your answer.​


Answer:

We're given with three points, A(3, 1), B(6, 4) and C(8, 6). If Ashima, Bharti and Camella are seated in a line then these given points A(3, 1), B(6, 4) and C(8, 6) must be COLLINEAR.

Basically, Collinear points are the points which lies on a same line. i.e. ( AB + BC = AC )

We've to find out the distance b/w them, and to find distance the formula is given by :

[tex]\bigstar\:\boxed{\sf{D = \sqrt{\Big(x_2 - x_1\Big)^2 + \Big(y_2 - y_1\Big)^2}}}[/tex]

⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀

⠀⠀⠀

◘ Distance of points AB, A(3, 1) and B(6, 4)

x₁ = 3

x₂ = 6

y₁ = 1

y₂ = 4

[tex]\dashrightarrow\sf \sqrt{\Big( \: 6 - 3 \: \Big)^2 + \Big( \: 4 - 1 \: \Big)^2} \\ \\ \\\dashrightarrow\sf \sqrt{ \Big( \: 3 \: \Big)^2 + \Big( \: 3 \: \Big)^2} \\ \\ \\\dashrightarrow\sf \sqrt{9 + 9}\\ \\ \\\dashrightarrow\sf \sqrt{18}\\ \\ \\\dashrightarrow \sf 3\sqrt{2}[/tex]

⠀⠀⠀

◘ Distance of points BC, B(6, 4) and C(8, 6)

x₁ = 6

x₂ = 8

y₁ = 4

y₂ = 6

[tex]\dashrightarrow\sf \sqrt{\Big( \: 8 - 6 \: \Big)^2 + \Big( \: 6 - 4 \: \Big)^2} \\ \\\\\dashrightarrow\sf \sqrt{\Big( \: 2 \: \Big)^2 + \Big( \: 2 \: \Big)^2 } \\ \\\\\dashrightarrow\sf \sqrt{4 + 4}\\ \\ \\\dashrightarrow\sf \sqrt{8} \\ \\\\\dashrightarrow\sf 2\sqrt{2}[/tex]

⠀⠀⠀

◘ Distance of points AC, A(3, 1) and C(8, 6)

x₁ = 3

x₂ = 8

y₁ = 1

y₂ = 6

[tex]\dashrightarrow\sf \sqrt{\Big(~8 - 3~\Big)^2 + \Big(~6 - 1~\Big)^2 } \\\\\\\dashrightarrow\sf\sqrt{\Big(~5~\Big)^2 + \Big(~5~\Big)^2 }\\\\\\\dashrightarrow\sf \sqrt{25 + 25}\\\\\\\dashrightarrow\sf \sqrt{50}\\\\\\\dashrightarrow\sf 5\sqrt{2}[/tex]

⠀⠀⠀

Therefore,

⠀⠀⠀

[tex]:\implies\sf AB + BC = AC \\\\\\:\implies\sf 3\sqrt{2} + 2\sqrt{2} = 5\sqrt{2} \\\\\\:\implies\sf \underline{\boxed{\frak{\pink{\pmb{ 5\sqrt{2} = 5\sqrt{2}}}}}}\;\bigstar[/tex]

⠀⠀⠀

∴ Hence, we can see that the given points are Collinear points therefore Ashima, Bharti and Camella are seated in a line.

22. 1) ABC company are selling an item for $800.00. Determine its cost if its mark-up is: a. 25% of the cost b. 35% of the selling price. 2) Jevong sold necklace at the mark-on rate of 20% for 5000 pesos? What is the initial cost? 3) Jose buys badminton rackets from a supplier of Php56. What is the retail price of the badminton racket if the manager applies a 42% markup 4) Camella Home had a grand sale last week. With all their items priced at P88, how much would the discounted price be if their items were all 30% off?


Answer:

1)

a. If the mark-up is 25% of the cost, then the cost can be found by dividing the selling price by (1 + markup percentage as a decimal).

Cost = 800 / (1 + 0.25) = $640.00

b. If the mark-up is 35% of the selling price, then the cost can be found by dividing the selling price by (1 + markup percentage as a decimal).

Let x be the initial cost, then the selling price is:

Selling price = x + 0.35x = 1.35x

We know that Selling price = $800.00, so:

1.35x = 800

x = 800 / 1.35 = $592.59 (rounded to the nearest cent)

2) If Jevong sold a necklace at a mark-on rate of 20% for 5000 pesos, then the initial cost can be found by dividing the selling price by (1 + mark-on rate as a decimal).

Let x be the initial cost, then the selling price is:

Selling price = x + 0.20x = 1.20x

We know that Selling price = 5000 pesos, so:

1.20x = 5000

x = 5000 / 1.20 = 4166.67 pesos (rounded to the nearest cent)

3) If Jose buys badminton rackets from a supplier for Php56 and the manager applies a 42% markup, then the retail price can be found by adding the markup percentage as a decimal to 1 and multiplying by the cost.

Retail price = cost x (1 + markup percentage as a decimal)

Retail price = 56 x (1 + 0.42) = Php79.52

Therefore, the retail price of the badminton racket is Php79.52.

4) If Camella Home had a grand sale last week with all their items priced at P88 and were all 30% off, then the discounted price can be found by multiplying the original price by (1 - discount percentage as a decimal).

Discounted price = original price x (1 - discount percentage as a decimal)

Discounted price = 88 x (1 - 0.30) = P61.60

Therefore, the discounted price of the items would be P61.60.
