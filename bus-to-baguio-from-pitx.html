8. Two hours after a bus left a station for Baguio at a speed of 40 kph, another bus left the same station traveling at a speed of 60 kph. How many hours will it take the second bus to overtake the first bus? Full solution


Answer:

4 hours

Step-by-step explanation:

What is being asked?  

How many hours will it take the second bus to overtake the first bus?

Given:

1st Bus :  

2 hours  

40 Kph

2nd Bus :  

60 Kph

Solutions:  

To get the total hours needed for the 2nd bus to overtake the 1st bus:

Know first the starting kilometer of each bus

For the 1st bus = 40kph x2hrs=80km for the 2hrs earlier.

2nd bus starting kilometer = 0 km

after 1 hour: 1st bus = 80km(2hrs earlier)+ 40km for the 1st hour= 120km

While, the 2nd bus = 0 + 60km = 60km

after 2 hours: 1st bus = 120km+40km = 160km

While, the 2nd bus = 60km+60km = 120km  

after 3 hours: 1st bus = 160km+ 40km = 200km

While, the 2nd bus = 120km+ 60km = 180km

after 4 hours: 1st bus = 200km+ 40km = 240km

While, the 2nd bus = 180km+ 60km = 240km

Therefore, after 4 hours the 2nd bus can now overtake the 1st bus.

Let:
x - traveling hours of bus

Note:
1st bus is traveling at a speed of 40kph and 2hours earlier than 2nd bus (40(x + 2))
2nd bus is traveling at a speed of 60kph (60x)


60x > 40(x + 2)
60x > 40x + 80
60x - 40x > 80
20x > 80
x > 4

After 4hours, the 2nd bus can overtake to the first bus.

19. The arche family will go to Baguio for three days vacation they arrived at the bus station at 7:55 am if the bus will leave at 8:30 am how many minutes will they wait


Step-by-step explanation:

To find out how many minutes the Arche family will wait for the bus, we need to calculate the difference between the time they arrived at the bus station and the time the bus will leave.

The time they arrived at the bus station is 7:55 am.

The time the bus will leave is 8:30 am.

To calculate the difference between these two times, we can subtract the minutes and the hours separately.

Subtracting the minutes:

30 - 55 = -25

Since we have a negative result, we need to borrow 1 hour from the hour component.

Subtracting the hours:

7 - 1 = 6

We have borrowed 1 hour from the hour component, so we need to subtract 1 from the hours of the initial time.

6 - 1 = 5

Therefore, the Arche family will wait for 35 minutes (from 7:55 am to 8:30 am).

Least Common Multiplier (LCM)

The LCM of a, b is the smallest positive number that is divisible by both a and b

20

= 2 · 10

= 2 · 15

15 divides by 3 15 = 5 · 3

= 2 · 3 · 5

26. The distance from Baguio to Metro Manila is 250 kilometers. How long will a bus travelling at 40km/hr to reach Baguio to Metro Manila?​


Distance, Speed, and Time

Formulas:

Distance = Speed × Time

Speed = Distance / Time

Time = Distance / Speed

________________________________

For the problem, we will use Time = Distance / Speed. Substitute the given into the formula.

Time = Distance / SpeedTime = 250 / 40Time = 6.25

Answer

Therefore, it will take 6.25 hours for the bus to reach Metro Manila from Baguio.
