3. which is a world class weaving industry in valencia,negros oriental made from abaca?​


SINAMAY WEAVING"Weaving the finest Negros abaca textile"

The world-class industry of sinamay weaving is located in Valencia, Negros Oriental. Abaca twine and a native plant like banana are used to make sinamay.

Although sinamay is frequently used for blocking hats and trims, the millinery industry didn't discover it until the 1990s. It is made in the Philippines from the abaca tree's stalks. Abaca fibers are incredibly durable and robust. Sinamay is frequently pre-stiffened during production to facilitate blockage. Additionally, because it is a natural substance, it takes dye quite well.

Sinamay is made from processed stalks of the country's native banana palm, the abacca tree. These are woven into a variety of items, including footwear, headgear, bags, and fashion accessories. In Carmen's sinamay items, a tradition of creativity and the virtue of diligence are portrayed.

25. What are the positive and negative implications of the value orientation of Filipinos on national development goals?​


Answer:

The value orientation of Filipinos has both positive and negative implications on national development goals. On the one hand, Filipino values such as family orientation, respect for elders, and close community ties can help promote harmonious social relations and a sense of responsibility among citizens. This can in turn lead to more effective and efficient governance and better implementation of development programs. On the other hand, Filipino values such as patriarchy, social stratification, and fatalism can reinforce existing inequalities and power imbalances in society. This can hinder progress towards national development goals by preventing marginalized groups from accessing opportunities and resources.
