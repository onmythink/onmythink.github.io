18. which of the following is not a factor to consider in salad preperation? a. contrast and harmony of colors b. quality of ingredients c. arrangement of food d. proper food combination


Answer:

Of the four factors given, (c) arrangement of food is the least considered in salad preparation.

Explanation:

Salad as an appetizer, is known as a mix of ingredients (usually cold) that are appealing to both the eyes and the palate.  The contrast and harmony of colors gives a salad it's visual appeal.  To be able to do that, chefs have to study proper food combination that will not only taste well together, but would also look inviting.  Since salads are often served cold and most ingredients are raw, food quality is quite essential as raw materials need to be kept at certain temperature and has to be served only at certain time to maintain its fresh quality in terms of taste and texture.  Moreover, ingredients in salads are normally  tossed together with the dressing which means that there hardly is any arrangement of food required.
