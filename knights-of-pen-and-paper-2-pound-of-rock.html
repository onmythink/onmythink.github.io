30. Imagine the biscuits are-rocks and you continue pounding or crashing them. What will happen to the rocks?​


Answer:

If we imagine the biscuits as rocks and continue pounding or crashing them, the rocks will break down into smaller pieces. The force applied to the rocks will cause them to fracture and shatter into smaller fragments. This process is similar to what happens when rocks are subjected to natural weathering and erosion over time. The end result of pounding or crashing the rocks will be a pile of smaller pieces, which may have different shapes and sizes depending on the force applied and the initial size of the rocks.

If the biscuits are imagined as rocks and they are continuously pounded or crashed, they will eventually break down into smaller pieces. The impact of the pounding will cause the rocks to fracture and crumble, resulting in smaller and smaller pieces until they become too small to break any further.

The phenomena of breaking down rocks or any other materials into smaller pieces due to the force applied to them is called fragmentation.
