6. During solution implementation, the problem solvers should be​


During solution implementation, the problem solvers should be performing TESTING.

Testing is a stage wherein software developers see how the developed system solve the current problems of the company or did become helpful to the company operations at all. Solution implementators are the system testers. System testers could be from the software development team or from the other teams. Evaluation will perform using series of test process. Processes are listed below:

Process 1: Unit Testing - in this phase or process, testers are checking if all menus, modules are part of the system is working normally. This is to ensure that no errors should arise during the use of the system.

Process 2: Integration Testing - this is the part wherein the system will be connecting to current system of the company (if there is any) or the system will combine to the current process of the firm.

Process 3: System Testing - This is the part where users will experience to use the system. This is the pre-conclusion should takes place. Users will evaluate if the system is really working on their end.

Process 4. Acceptance Testing - This is the part wherein the system is being accepted by the users. Contract and documents are signed and payments are released.

1. Define the problem.

In understanding and communicating the problem effectively, we have to be clear about what the issue is. Remember to focus on the behaviour instead of attacking the person.

If stakeholders are lacking cooperation in implementing a program, there are several steps that can be taken to address the issue:

Communication: Improving communication channels can help to foster better understanding and cooperation among stakeholders. Regular meetings, progress reports, and feedback sessions can provide opportunities for stakeholders to discuss challenges and find solutions together.

Incentives: Offering incentives to stakeholders can be an effective way to encourage cooperation. This could include financial incentives, recognition or awards for good performance, or other benefits that motivate stakeholders to work together towards a common goal.

Training and capacity-building: Providing training and capacity-building opportunities to stakeholders can help to build skills and knowledge, and foster a sense of ownership and commitment to the program.

Clear roles and responsibilities: Clearly defining roles and responsibilities for each stakeholder can help to avoid confusion and ensure that everyone understands their part in the program. This can help to improve accountability and promote cooperation.

Mediation: If conflicts arise between stakeholders, mediation can be used to help resolve disputes and find common ground. A neutral third party can help to facilitate discussions and find solutions that are acceptable to all parties involved.
