Explanation:

PAGASA forecasters use three domains where they monitor, analyze and forecast tropical cyclones: the Philippine Area of Responsibility, Tropical Cyclone Advisory Domain and the Tropical Cyclone Information Domain. These domains can be visualized as "three-layered zones" that enclose the country. If a tropical cyclone is present inside these domains, PAGASA is obliged to provide information about the weather disturbance to the public. Because most tropical cyclones come from the broad expanse of ocean east of the country, the eastern boundary of each domain is farther from the Philippine Islands than the western boundary.

Path of Oxygen

During gas exchange oxygen moves from the lungs to the bloodstream. At the same time carbon dioxide passes from the blood to the lungs. This happens in the lungs between the alveoli and a network of tiny blood vessels called capillaries, which are located in the walls of the alveoli.

Verbals are verbs that can be used differently in a sentence or idea aside from being a verb itself.

Pathway of food in the digestive system:

Mouth  →   Esophagus  →  Stomach  →  Small intestine  → Large Intestine

                                                                              Anus     ←  Rectum

The food will be first enters our mouth to be breaking down by chewing and start digesting with the help of the enzyme salivary amylase. As we swallow the food, it enters the esophagus and by peristaltic movement, it will goes to the stomach for digestion with the help of different enzyme and the stomach acid. The digestive food will then enter the small intestine which the actual site and final site of digestion. The food will then inter the large intestine for the absorption of water and other elements and then goes to the rectum and exit to the anus during defecation.
