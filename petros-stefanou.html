12. Fred's petrol engine takes in 8000 J chemical energy and does 400 J of work while Marco's petro engine takes in 6000 J energy and does 200 J of work. How much efficient both engines are? Who's engine is more efficient and why?​


Answer:

Marco's engine is more efficient.

Explanation:

Given:

=8000J =6000J

=400J =200J

Asked:

energy output x 100

energy output x 100Energy Efficiency = energy input

Solution:

Fred's:

8000J - 400J = 7,600

7,600 x 100

Efficiency = 8,000

= 0.9 × 100

= 90%

Marco's:

6000J - 200J = 5,800

5,800 x 100

Efficiency = 6,000

= 0.95 × 100

= 95%

Conclusion: Marco's percentage of efficiency of the engine is higher than Fred's percentage, therefore Marco's engine is more efficient than the latter.
