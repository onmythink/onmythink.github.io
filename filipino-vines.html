15. negative effects of vines?​


Answer:

Vines can have negative effects on various aspects of our environment, including:

Damage to infrastructure: Vines can grow rapidly and attach themselves to buildings, fences, and other structures. This can lead to damage to the infrastructure over time, especially if the vines are not properly maintained.

Decreased biodiversity: Some invasive vine species can outcompete and choke out native plants, reducing biodiversity in the area. This can also affect the food web and disrupt the ecological balance.

Increased risk of wildfires: Vines can act as fuel for wildfires, which can spread quickly and be difficult to contain. This is particularly a concern in dry areas where the vines can dry out and become highly flammable.

Decreased property value: Overgrown vines can make a property look unkempt and may decrease its value. They can also obstruct views and make it difficult to access certain areas of the property.

Health hazards: Certain types of vines, such as poison ivy, can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions in some people. Vines can also harbor pests such as ticks and mosquitoes, which can carry diseases.

Overall, while vines can be beautiful and beneficial in some cases, it is important to be aware of their potential negative effects and to properly maintain them to avoid any damage or harm.

23. What is succulents and vines


In botany, succulent plants, also known as succulents or sometimes fat plants, are plants having some parts that are more than normally thickened and fleshy, usually to retain water in arid climates or soil conditions. The word "succulent" comes from the Latin word sucus, meaning juice, or sap.[1] Succulent plants may store water in various structures, such as leaves and stems.

Meanwhile, vines are plants that has very long stems and that grows along the ground or up and around something (such as a wall or tree) ; a plant whose stem requires support and which climbs by tendrils or twining or creeps along the ground.
