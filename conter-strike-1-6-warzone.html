26. enumerate the 6 elbow strikes. describe each elbow strikes in your own words .​


Forward elbow strike: This is a basic elbow strike where the elbow is thrown forward in a straight line, usually towards the opponent's head or chest

Uppercut elbow strike: This elbow strike involves swinging the arm upwards in an arc, with the elbow leading the way, in an attempt to strike the opponent's chin or throat.

Downward elbow strike: This strike involves bringing the elbow down in a chopping motion, often aimed at the back of the opponent's neck or shoulders.

Rear elbow strike: This strike involves swinging the elbow backwards, often as a counter attack when an opponent is behind you.

Hooking elbow strike: This strike involves a circular motion with the elbow, often used to sweep an opponent's legs or to strike the side of their head or body.

Spinning elbow strike: This is a more advanced technique that involves turning the body and using the momentum to generate power for the elbow strike, often aimed at the head or upper body of the opponent.

30. why do we need to have knowledge about knowing the 6 striking techniques and 12 striking techniques in arnis


Answer:

Once you have mastered the basic stances of Arnis, you are now ready to learn the 12 basic striking techniques. These techniques are used with the stances. The effectivity of each strike heavily relies on mobility, which is why you must have a strong command of the basic stances before learning the 12 basic striking techniques.

Learning how to defend yourself is essential in today’s time because of the number of people with ill intentions in the streets. Arnis is the best form of martial arts to learn for self-defense against multiple attackers. It is also a great way to discipline your mind and improve your body. The techniques of Arnis are extremely lethal when learned correctly. It is necessary for you to master these 12 striking techniques to become an effective Arnis practitioner. 

These moves and basic stances are the basis for most of the advanced techniques. The principles used in Arnis are also the same principles used in most Eastern martial arts. This will make it easier for you to learn other Asian fighting arts.
