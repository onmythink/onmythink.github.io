Answer:

The rotary engine is an early type of internal combustion engine, usually designed with an odd number of cylinders per row in a radial configuration, in which the crankshaft remained stationary in operation, with the entire crankcase and its attached cylinders rotating around it as a unit.

18. 2 examples of rotary,oscillating,linear,reciprocating​


Rotary:

Electric motor: An electric motor is a machine that converts electrical energy into rotary mechanical energy. It typically uses a rotating magnetic field to turn a rotor, which then drives a shaft to perform useful work.

Washing machine: A washing machine is an appliance that cleans laundry by rotating it inside a drum. The drum rotates back and forth, using a combination of water, detergent, and mechanical action to agitate and clean the clothes.

Oscillating:

Electric fan: An electric fan typically has a motor that drives a set of blades to rotate back and forth in a circular motion, producing an oscillating breeze that circulates air around the room.

Pendulum clock: A pendulum clock is a timekeeping device that uses the swinging motion of a pendulum to regulate the movement of its gears. The pendulum swings back and forth in an oscillating motion, with each swing marking the passage of a certain amount of time.

Linear:

Elevator: An elevator is a vertical transportation device that moves people or goods up and down a building. It typically consists of a car that moves along a linear track, supported by cables and driven by a motor.

Conveyor belt: A conveyor belt is a mechanical device used to transport materials from one point to another. It typically consists of a belt that moves along a linear path, driven by a motor, and supported by rollers or other devices.

Reciprocating:

Internal combustion engine: An internal combustion engine is a type of engine that uses controlled explosions of fuel and air to produce a reciprocating motion of a piston, which then drives a crankshaft to produce rotary motion.

Air compressor: An air compressor is a machine that compresses air to a high pressure, typically using a reciprocating piston or a rotary screw mechanism. The compressed air can then be used for various purposes, such as powering pneumatic tools or inflating tires.
