Writyour answer on the blanks provided.1. Applied force2. Electric force3. Magnetic force4. Drag force5. Frictional force6. Normal force7. Gravitational force8. Spring force9. Tension​


Contact and Non-Contact Forces

1. Applied force - CF

2. Electric force - NF

3. Magnetic force - NF

4. Drag force - CF

5. Frictional force - CF

6. Normal force -CF

7. Gravitational force -NF

8. Spring force - CF

9. Tension​ - CF

Explanation:

Contact force is a type of force that acts between two objects that are in contact with each other. A non-contact force, on the other hand, acts between the bodies that are not in direct contact.

Examples of Contact Forces are:

Applied Force

When a certain amount of force is applied to an object, there is an applied force. One example is when a person pushed the chair, the person exerted applied force on the chair.

Drag Force

Drag Force is similar to frictional force where it opposes the movement of objects. However, this force is present in objects in fluid (liquid or gas).

Frictional Force

When we move an object across a surface, the frictional force exists. Friction is the force that opposes motion when the surface of one object comes in contact with the surface of another.

Normal Force

The normal force is the support force exerted upon an object that is in contact with another stable object. It is a force opposite the student's wight. For example, if you place your bag on the table, the table is exerting an upward force on your bag in order to support the weight of the book. This force is the normal force.

Spring Force

The force that is present on a compressed or stretched spring. The spring force is the force exerted by a compressed or stretched spring upon any object that is attached to it.

Tension

When two opposing forces are acted on the object, the tension force is present. One example is when teams in tug-o-war apply opposite force on the rope.

Examples of Contact Forces are:

Electric Force

Two charged bodies may exert repulsive or attractive interaction between them. The force present in charged bodies is the electric force. This force acts at a distance.

Magnetic force

As the name implies, the magnetic force is a kind of force that is present in magnets. It is a result of the electromagnetic force, which is caused by the motion of charges.

Gravity

Objects on earth experience a downward force, known as gravity. It is the force acting on the object towards the center of the earth. This is equal to the weight of the object.
