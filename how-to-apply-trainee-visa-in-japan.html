4. You have been appointed as the human resource manager in of a BIG Company XYZ. it proposes to select management trainees for its different departments. What sources should it explore and how should the trainees be selected?


As the human resource manager of Company XYZ, there are several sources you can explore to select management trainees for different departments. Here are some suggestions:

Campus Recruitment: You can partner with universities and colleges to recruit fresh graduates who have completed their management courses. This can be a cost-effective way to find young talent with fresh ideas and a strong educational background.
Referrals: You can ask your current employees to refer qualified candidates they know who would be a good fit for the company. This can be a great way to find people who already have a connection to the company and may be a good cultural fit.
Job Portals: You can advertise the management trainee positions on popular job portals such as LinkedIn, Glassdoor, Indeed, Monster, and so on. This will allow you to reach a wider audience and attract candidates who are actively looking for job opportunities.
Industry Associations: You can reach out to industry associations that are related to the departments you're hiring for. They may have a pool of candidates who have already completed training and have experience in the industry.
Once you have a pool of potential candidates, you can use the following methods to select the best management trainees for your company:

Aptitude Test: Conduct an aptitude test to measure the candidate's problem-solving skills, logical reasoning, and cognitive abilities. This will help you assess their potential to learn and perform well in the job.
Group Discussion: Conduct a group discussion to evaluate the candidate's communication skills, leadership potential, and team-working abilities. This will also give you an opportunity to observe their interpersonal skills and ability to persuade others.
Interview: Conduct one-on-one interviews to get to know the candidates better and understand their career aspirations, goals, and strengths. This will help you assess their fit with the company culture and the department they are applying for.
Assessment Center: Conduct an assessment center where candidates are given real-life scenarios and are evaluated on their ability to solve problems, lead a team, and make decisions. This can be a more comprehensive and objective way to assess a candidate's potential.
By using a combination of these selection methods, you can identify the most suitable management trainees who have the potential to contribute to the company's growth and success.

17. in TVET Training, how to be assured that a trainee is already competent?​


Answer:

1. Objective

In different sectors of industry and technical vocational institutes, centers and schools,

due attention is given to the performance of the craftsmen / apprentices / trainees /

students by applying both Competency Based Education and Training CBET as well as

Competency Assurance Management System CAMS. The purpose of these systems is to

provide guidelines to management to set valid and reliable controls in place with a view to

have reasonable assurance of meeting their business objectives by assuring that their

employees / apprentices / trainees / students are well trained, assessed, verified and

proved to be competent at work location and that they can discharge their responsibilities

in a safe and effective manner.

The strategy of Competency Based TVET is based around "Occupational competencies"

which are established for each career field and for each job title. A Competency Based

TVET System generally uses combinations of powerful techniques to ensure that the

needs of different Industry sectors are addressed (demand), courses or competency

based training programmes are developed, competency based assessments / verifications

are conducted, employees / apprentices / trainees / students are efficiently trained and

competent. In this regards, techniques such as Management Information System MIS,

Knowledge Management KM, Monitoring & Evaluation M&E and Competency Assurance

Management System CAMS are generally used.

2. Competency

The Competency is defined as the individual’s ability to use, apply and demonstrate a

group of related awareness, knowledge, skills and attitudes in order to perform tasks and

duties successfully and which can be measured against well-accepted standards (levels)

required in employment as well as assessed against provided evidences at work location.

The competency affects both individual’s job responsibility and performance on the job

and usually fall into two categories, namely technical and behavioral.

The key aspects of the definition of competency are:

 Any job / occupation can be effectively and sufficiently described in terms of the

Tasks that successful workers in that occupation perform.

 All Tasks have direct implications for the Awareness, Knowledge, Skills, and

Attitudes Competencies that workers / trainees must acquire in order to perform

the tasks correctly.

 Assessment is made on how the individual is actually performing work.

 An individual is incompetent no matter how much knowledge he has, as long as he

can’t apply his knowledge and skills appropriately at work location.

 The assessment must be objective by conducting it against defined Competency

Standards (Levels).

The definition of competency implies a more formal, objective process of assessing

performance by clearly knowing what is being assessed and how it is assessed. This  

approach is quite different than most of the past performance assessment practices

utilized in the industry.

3. Competence Based Occupational Standards

One of the most important and significant developments in TVET, was the development of

Competence Based Standards to support the design of training programmes and

curricula.

The move towards competence based standards started in the mid 1970s and represents

a response to criticisms that education and training programmes were failing to meet the

practical requirements of employment. In many occupational areas, employers found that

newly qualified graduates of vocational training programmes were not capable of meeting

the requirements of practice without substantial further education and training.

The drive to develop competence based standards started in mainly manual and craft

occupations where the practical requirements of employment were clear to see. Training

in these occupational areas was dominated by traditional ‘school based’ approaches

involving theoretical education combined with practical classes, often performed with out

of date equipment and methods. The curriculum base of such programmes was clearly

out of alignment with the rapidly changing needs of employment.

The conception of competence based standards aimed to reverse the traditional

educational model based on ‘inputs’ (the curriculum) and traditional educational practice

which generally attempt to approach academic education. The traditional curriculum

defined what the teacher should teach. The change in competence approach is the

movement towards a definition of what the student needs to learn in order to perform to

the standards required in employment.

The instrument developed to enable this change is the concept of an occupational or

educational ‘standard (level)’. The standard elaborates the level of competence required

to perform successfully in an occupation and that description is used, in turn, to develop

a closely aligned curriculum.

25. You have been appointed as the human resource manager in of a BIG Company XYZ. it proposes to select management trainees for its different departments. What sources should it explore and how should the trainees be selected?


Answer:

Sources to explore for selecting management trainees for different departments: campus recruitment, employee referrals, job boards, social media. Factors to consider when selecting trainees: educational qualification, relevant work experience, communication skills, problem-solving skills, teamwork and leadership potential, cultural fit, and assessment and evaluation

Answer:

As the human resource manager of Company XYZ, there are several sources that can be explored to select management trainees for different departments. Some of the sources are:

1. Campus Recruitment: The company can visit various colleges and universities to recruit fresh graduates who have the required skills and qualifications.

2. Employee Referrals: The company can encourage its current employees to refer potential candidates who can be trained as management trainees.

3. Job Portals: The company can post job openings on various job portals and social media platforms to attract potential candidates.

4. Professional Networks: The company can reach out to professional networks such as LinkedIn, industry associations, and business groups to find suitable candidates.

5. Recruitment Agencies: The company can partner with recruitment agencies to find potential candidates who fit the job requirements.

Once the sources have been identified, the next step is to select the management trainees. The selection process should involve the following steps:

1. Application Screening: The HR team should screen the applications received from various sources to shortlist the candidates who meet the job requirements.

2. Aptitude Test: The shortlisted candidates should be asked to take an aptitude test to assess their cognitive abilities, problem-solving skills, and logical reasoning.

3. Group Discussion: The candidates who clear the aptitude test should be invited for a group discussion to evaluate their communication skills, leadership qualities, and team management abilities.

4. Personal Interview: The final step is to conduct a personal interview with the candidates to assess their attitude, motivation, and fit with the company's culture.

5. Background Verification: Before offering the job, the HR team should conduct a background verification of the selected candidates to ensure that they have no criminal record or any other discrepancies in their profile.

By following these steps, Company XYZ can select the best management trainees who have the potential to become future leaders in the organization.
