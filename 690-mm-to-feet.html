11. A gas occupies a volume of 0.240 L at 490 mm of Hg and a 65 degree Celsius. If the pressure is increased to 690 torr, what is the temperature to maintain the same volume?​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P_1 & = \text{490 mmHg = 490 torr} \\ T_1 & = 65^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{338 K} \\ P_2 & = \text{690 torr} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final temperature (in kelvin) by using Gay-Lussac's law.

[tex]\begin{aligned} T_2 & = \frac{T_1P_2}{P_1} \\ & = \frac{(\text{338 K})(\text{690 torr})}{\text{490 torr}} \\ & = \text{476 K} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Convert the final temperature from kelvin (K) to degree Celsius (°C).

[tex]\begin{aligned} t_2 & = T_2 - 273 \\ & = 476 - 273 \\ & = \boxed{203^{\circ}\text{C}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the temperature is 203°C.
