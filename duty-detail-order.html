Enlightened Anthropocentrism is a philosophical perspective that suggests that human beings have a moral duty to balance their use of natural resources with the needs of future generations. This perspective is often seen as a step forward from traditional anthropocentrism, which prioritizes human interests over those of other species and the environment.

Whether this obligation is correct or not is subjective and depends on one's moral and ethical beliefs. Some people might argue that preserving the environment and protecting future generations is a fundamental moral duty, while others might argue that humans have the right to use resources as they see fit, so long as they do not cause harm to others.

Regardless of one's perspective, it is widely recognized that unsustainable use of natural resources can have negative consequences for both the environment and future generations, and many experts recommend taking a responsible and sustainable approach to resource use.

24. Moving forward should Filipinos base their moral beliefs on Duty-Based or Consequentialist Ethics in order to egress bettèr as a society?


Answer:

The choice between duty-based or consequentialist ethics as a basis for moral beliefs depends on individual preferences and context. Both approaches have their own strengths and weaknesses and can be used in different situations.

Duty-based ethics, also known as deontological ethics, emphasizes the inherent value of actions themselves and their conformity to moral duties or principles. This approach asserts that there are certain actions that are inherently right or wrong, regardless of their consequences. Examples of moral duties include honesty, respect for others, and fulfilling promises.

Consequentialist ethics, on the other hand, focuses on the outcomes or consequences of actions. It asserts that actions are right or wrong based on the resulting consequences, with the goal of producing the greatest good for the greatest number of people. Examples of consequentialist approaches include utilitarianism, which aims to maximize happiness or pleasure, and ethical egoism, which asserts that individuals should act in their own self-interest.

In terms of egressing as a society, both duty-based and consequentialist ethics can be useful. Duty-based ethics can help promote universal moral values and principles that can guide individuals towards ethical behavior, such as treating others with respect, honesty, and fairness. Consequentialist ethics, on the other hand, can help individuals and society as a whole make decisions that lead to positive outcomes and benefits for the greater community.

Ultimately, the key is to strike a balance between these two approaches, recognizing the importance of both principles and outcomes in ethical decision-making. Rather than choosing one over the other, Filipinos should strive to integrate both approaches in their moral beliefs and actions, taking into account the unique contexts and circumstances of each situation

Answer:

c.) Orders of the Master to the officer of the watch which he or she must comply with.

Explanation:

Standing orders are the orders given by the Master of a vessel to the officer of the watch, which he or she must comply with. These orders specify the responsibilities and duties of the officer of the watch, as well as the procedures to be followed in various situations, such as approaching other vessels, entering or leaving port, or responding to emergencies. The standing orders are designed to ensure the safe and efficient operation of the vessel, and they are an important component of the vessel's safety management system.
