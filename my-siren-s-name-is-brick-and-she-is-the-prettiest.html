How far does the brick fall during this time?​


Answer:

1)

a) Using the kinematic equation:

[tex]y=v_{i}t+\dfrac{1}{2}gt^2[/tex]

After 1.00 s,

[tex]y=(3.00\:\textrm{m/s})(1\:\textrm{s})+\dfrac{1}{2}(9.8\:\textrm{m/s}^2)(1\:\textrm{s})^2\\\\ y=7.9\:\textrm{m}\leftarrow\textrm{ANSWER}[/tex]

After 2.00 s,

[tex]y=(3.00\:\textrm{m/s})(2\:\textrm{s})+\dfrac{1}{2}(9.8\:\textrm{m/s}^2)(2\:\textrm{s})^2\\\\ y=25.6\:\textrm{m}\leftarrow\textrm{ANSWER}[/tex]

b)

Using the kinematic equation:

[tex]v_{f}^2=v_{i}^2+2gy[/tex]

[tex]v_{f}=\sqrt{v_{i}^2+2gy}[/tex]

After 1.00 s,

[tex]v_{f}=\sqrt{(3.00\:\textrm{m/s})^2+2(9.8\:\textrm{m/s}^2)(7.9\:\textrm{m})}\\v_{f}=12.8\:\textrm{m/s}\leftarrow\textrm{ANSWER}[/tex]

After 2.00 s,

[tex]v_{f}=\sqrt{(3.00\:\textrm{m/s})^2+2(9.8\:\textrm{m/s}^2)(25.6\:\textrm{m})}\\v_{f}=22.6\:\textrm{m/s}\leftarrow\textrm{ANSWER}[/tex]

2) Assuming the brick starts at rest, the initial velocity is zero.

a) Using the kinematic equation,

[tex]v_{f}=v_{i}+gt[/tex]

After 4.0 s, the velocity is

[tex]v_{f}=0+(9.8\:\textrm{m/s}^2)(4.0\:\textrm{s})=39.2\:\textrm{m/s}\leftarrow\textrm{ANSWER}[/tex]

b) Using the kinematic equation,

[tex]y=v_{i}t+\dfrac{1}{2}gt^2[/tex]

After 4.0 s, the fall height is

[tex]y=0+\dfrac{1}{2}(9.8\:\textrm{m/s}^2)(4.0\:\textrm{s})^2\\\\ y=78.4\:\textrm{m}\leftarrow\textrm{ANSWER}[/tex]
