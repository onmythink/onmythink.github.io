4. Consider the situation below and answer the question that follow. 1. What is the probability of tossing a head and choosing an ace from a standard deck of cards?​


Answer:

The probability of tossing a head and choosing an ace from a standard deck of cards is the product of the probability of each event.

The probability of tossing a head in a single coin toss is 1/2 or 0.5.

The probability of choosing an ace from a standard deck of cards is 4/52 or 1/13 since there are four aces in a deck of 52 cards.

Therefore, the probability of both events happening is:

0.5 x 1/13 = 1/26 or approximately 0.0385.

So the probability of tossing a head and choosing an ace from a standard deck of cards is 1 in 26 or approximately 0.0385.

18. A rectangular pool is 20 feet wide and 50 feet long. A deck used for sunning surrounds the pool. The deck is the same width all the way around the pool. The total area of the deck is 456 square feet. How wide is the deck around the pool


Answer:

3 feet

Solution:

pool = 20 x 50 ft.

The area of a rectangle is given as lw, which is the length times the width.

area of the pool = 1000 ft²

area of deck only = 456 ft²

We're looking for the width of the deck (x), and the easiest way to do that would be to get the dimensions (length and width) of the total area.

total area = area of the pool + deck

total area = 1456 ft²

We know that the dimensions of the total area can be represented as:

l = 2x + 50

w = 2x + 20

Now, let's solve for the dimensions.

A = lw

1456 = (2x + 50)(2x + 20)

1456 = 4x² + 140x + 1000

364 = x² + 35x + 250

0 = x² + 35x - 114

Factor out the polynomial.

(x - 3)(x + 38) = 0

x = 3, -38

Since we are dealing with measurements, we cannot use negative values, therefore, x = 3.

The deck is 3 feet wide.

PROBABILITY==============================

» Identify the probability of getting an event over the total number of outcomes.

[tex] \boxed{\sf P = \frac{No. \ of \ favorable \: outcomes}{No. \ of \ total \: outcomes}} [/tex]

[tex] \: [/tex]

[tex] \large \bold{\blue{Question \ 3:}} [/tex] A card is drawn at random from a deck of cards. Find the probability of getting the 3 of diamond?

[tex] \large \bold{\blue{Solution:}} [/tex] Remember that in every deck of cards, there are 52 of them (ignoring the jokers). 13 for each of the four shapes. Find the probability of getting 3 of diamonds that is only one.

[tex] \sf P = \frac{1}{52} \\[/tex]

[tex] \large \therefore \underline{\boxed{\tt \purple{P = \frac{1}{52} \: \: or \: \: 1.92\%}}} [/tex]

[tex] \: [/tex]

[tex] \large \bold{\blue{Question \ 4:}} [/tex] A card is drawn at random from a deck of cards. Find the probability of getting a queen.

[tex] \large \bold{\blue{Solution:}} [/tex] 52 cards in a deck. Four Queens for each deck.

[tex] \sf P = \frac{4}{52} \\[/tex]

[tex] \sf P = \frac{1}{13} \\[/tex]

[tex] \large \therefore \underline{\boxed{\tt \purple{P(Queens) = \frac{1}{13} \: \: or \: \: 7.69\%}}} [/tex]

[tex] \: [/tex]

[tex] \large \bold{\blue{Question \: 5:}} [/tex] A die is rolled, find the probability that the number obtained is greater than 4.

[tex] \large \bold{\blue{Solution:}} [/tex] There are 6 faces in a die. Identify what are the outcomes that is greater than 4: [tex] \sf \{ 5, 6 \} [/tex]

» Since there are two, find the probability of getting that two.

[tex] \sf P = \frac{2}{6} \\[/tex]

[tex] \sf P = \frac{1}{3} \\[/tex]
