23. what is your opinion of Cisternal Space​


Answer:

The cisternal space is a term used in neuroanatomy to describe the spaces between the membranes that surround the brain, specifically the subarachnoid space. This space contains cerebrospinal fluid, which is important for protecting and cushioning the brain and spinal cord, as well as removing waste products and providing nutrients.

The cisternal space is important in many neurological conditions, such as subarachnoid hemorrhage and hydrocephalus, as changes in the volume or pressure of cerebrospinal fluid in this space can have significant effects on brain function. Imaging techniques such as magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) can be used to visualize the cisternal space and detect abnormalities.

Overall, the cisternal space is an important aspect of the anatomy and function of the brain, and understanding its role in various neurological conditions can help in the diagnosis and treatment of these conditions.

Explanation:

The water does not enter the bottle because of the presence of air inside it. When the bottle is dipped inside water, it applies pressure on the air present inside so as to replace it. But the air present inside the bottle applies opposite force on the water as it tries to escape through the only opening present which is surrounded by water. So both the forces cancel each other and maintain an equilibrium due to which the neither the air gets out nor the water gets in.
