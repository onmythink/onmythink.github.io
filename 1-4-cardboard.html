2. ‍ 1/5 of the width and 1/4 of the length of a rectangular cardboard is cut off. What part of the original cardboard is the area of the remaining piece?


Answer:

Length of a rectangular piece = L Units

1/4 length is cut off …. remaining length = L - 1/4 L = 3/4 L Units

Width of the rectangular piece = W Units

1/5 width is cut off … remaining width = W - 1/5 W = 4/5 W Units

Area of remaining piece = 3/4 L x 4/5 W = 3/5 LW Unit^2

3/5 of the original area LW is the area of the remaining piece

Answer:: I observed that the coin drop straight into the glass.

Explanation:

So, according to the Laws of motion it says something like this: “An object at rest stays at rest unless an outside force is strong enough to make it move. An object that is moving, will continue moving at the same speed in a straight line unless an outside force is strong enough to speed it up, slow it down, stop it, or cause it to change direction.”

So, in the case of the coin, the coin is at rest sitting on top of the card. The coin will not move from that spot, unless acted by force ( law of inertia )When you flick the card out from under the coin, the coin fell straight into the glass ( law of acceleration ), you allow gravity to act on it and drop it into the glass. The bottom of the glass stops the penny from falling ( law of interaction ). However, if the penny were a lot heavier (had more mass) and were falling much faster, it could go right through the glass.

28. Ruel has a rectangular plece of cardboard whose dimensions inches, what is its perimeter? are in the ratio 4:5. If the area of the cardboard is 720 square​


Let's first use algebra to find the dimensions of the cardboard.

Let x be the common ratio of the dimensions. Then the length of the cardboard is 4x and the width is 5x.

The area of the cardboard is given as 720 square inches:

(4x) * (5x) = 720

20x^2 = 720

x^2 = 36

x = 6

So the length of the cardboard is 4x = 4 * 6 = 24 inches, and the width is 5x = 5 * 6 = 30 inches.

Now we can use the formula for the perimeter of a rectangle:

Perimeter = 2(length + width)

Perimeter = 2(24 + 30)

Perimeter = 2(54)

Perimeter = 108 inches

Therefore, the perimeter of the cardboard is 108 inches.
