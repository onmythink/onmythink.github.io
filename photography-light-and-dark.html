2. why do you think red light is used in photographi dark rooms?​


Answer:

It's because, Darkrooms used red lighting to allow photographers to control light carefully, so that light-sensitive photographic paper would not become overexposed and ruin the pictures during the developing process.

Answer:

Red lights are used in black and white darkrooms when making prints. It is not safe for most widely used fillms, either color film or panchromatic. Panchromatic means it is sensitive to all colors of light.

However with B&W films the negatives are only black or shade of gray transparent.

TO make prints the negative is exposed through with white light to cast an image onto photographically sensitive paper. Since the negative has no coloration no color information is lost with colorization of the exposing light.

And photographic paper, unlike the film is not panchromatic - it is insensitive to red light for the most part,or at least much, much less sensitive.

Thus in most B&W printing darkrooms, a dim red light is used so you can do most setup without having to feel your way around… setting the timer, setting the enlarger, handling negatives and paper, performing dodging and burning, manipulating the prints in the developer solutions.

Blank paper is kept in safes that cut out even the red light, but a few minutes exposure was no harm. THe enlarger exposures were usually a handful of seconds for easy printing control.

7. how to get the perfect lighting for photography?


Answer:

1. Use natural light whenever possible. Natural light is the best light for photography, and itâs free! Try to take photos during the golden hour, which is the hour before sunset and the hour after sunrise.

2. Use a reflector. A reflector is a great tool for bouncing light onto your subject. It can be used to fill in shadows and create a more even light.

3. Use a diffuser. A diffuser is a great tool for softening the light and creating a more even light.

4. Use a flash. A flash can be used to add light to a scene and can be used to create dramatic lighting effects.

5. Experiment with different angles. Different angles can create different lighting effects. Try shooting from different angles to see what works best for your scene.

6. Use a tripod. A tripod will help you keep your camera steady and will allow you to take longer exposures. This is especially helpful when shooting in low light.
