2. What is the future tense of bear and beat​


Answer: will bear & will beat.

Explanation: When we talk about the future tense, all we need to do is to keep the present tense of the verb, and add "will" before it. [ will + present form of the verb ]

For example, we have the word prepared. The present form is prepare, and to make it to its future form, we will add "will" before it. So, its future tense becomes will prepare. In this case, it's will bear & will beat.

10. Determine the momentum of 1000-kg car moving northward at 20 m/s.


The Momentum is 20,000 kg m/s.

Momentum formula =

momentum = velocity x mass.

It is in the proper units, so we need to substitute our data into the algorithm.

momentum = 1,000 kg x 20 m/s

                   = 20,000 kg m/s

Momentum equation is a property of a moving body that the body possesses by virtue of its mass and motion, and that is equal to the product of the body's mass and velocity. In a broader sense, Momentum is a feature of a moving body that determines the time required to generate it to rest when subjected to a constant force.

Momentum possesses both magnitude and direction. Therefore, it is a vector quantity. Momentum is measured in kg m/s or newtons seconds, N s.

Despite the similarity of these physical variables, there is a distinction between force and Momentum. In general, force is the external action exerted on a body, whether it be a pulling or pushing action. Momentum, on the other hand, is the quantity of motion contained in a moving body.
