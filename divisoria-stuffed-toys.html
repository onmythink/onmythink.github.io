1. A toy manufacturer makes bear and dog stuffed toys. It takes 30 minutes to sew a bearstuffed toy and 45 minutes to sew a dog stuffed toy. There is a total of 10 hours of sewingtime available to finish x bear and y dog stuffed toys. Write the inequality for this situation.​


Answer:  2x + 3y ≤ 40

Step-by-step explanation:

According to the given problem, the variables are,

x =number of bear stuffed toys

y =number of dog stuffed toys

The only constraint mentioned in the problem is the time, limited to 10 hours.

The total time taken to sew all the bear stuffed toys, is calculated as,

[tex]T_{b}[/tex] = (Time taken to sew 1 toy) × (No. of such bear stuffed toys)

    = (30 min)×(x)

    =30x

The total time taken to sew all the dog stuffed toys is calculated as,

[tex]T_{d}[/tex] = (Time taken to sew 1 toy) × (No. of such dog stuffed toys)

    = (45 min)×(y)

    =45y

The total time required to sew 'x' bear and 'y' dog stuffed toys,

[tex]T = T_{b} + T_{d}[/tex]

   = 30x + 45y

The time required for sewing of all (x+y) toys cannot exceed 10 hours => 600 minutes

⇒30x + 45y ≤ 600

Divide by 15 both sides, to obtain the inequality in its simple form,

⇒2x + 3y ≤ 40

Thus, the required inequality for the given problem is  2x + 3y ≤ 40

Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

Let's call the number of stuffed toys in each box "x".

If Rea Anne donated 4 boxes with the same number of stuffed toys inside, then the total number of stuffed toys she donated was 4x.

The number of stuffed toys left to her can be expressed as the difference between the total number of stuffed toys she collected and the number of stuffed toys she donated. This can be written as:

246 - 4x

So the algebraic expression to get the number of stuffed toys left to her is 246 - 4x.
