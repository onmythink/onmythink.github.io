6. It is a breaking down of rocks by oxygen in water, often giving iron rocks a rusty-colored weather surface.


Decomposition of Rocks by Oxygen in Water

The decomposition of rocks by oxygen in water, often making rust on iron rock is called oxidation. A loss of electrons, which typically results in an atom having a greater positive charge, is what defines oxidation. A gain in electrons, which typically results in an atom having a higher negative charge, is what defines reduction. We must determine which chemical is getting more positive in order to identify the one that is being oxidized.

Zn+2H+→Zn2++H2

Zinc has a zero beginning charge but develops a positive two charge as a finished product. Although it starts out with one positive charge, hydrogen eventually becomes neutral. If we divide the entire response into two half-reactions, the contrast becomes even more obvious.

Zn→Zn2++2e−

2H++2e−→H2

We can observe that hydrogen is receiving electrons while zinc is losing electrons. Consequently, zinc is oxidizing.
