18. Discuss the difference between DECATHLON. HEPTATHLON, and PENTATHLON?


Answer:

Decathlon is a male summer sporting event that consists of 10 track and fields for 2 days

Heptathlon is a female summer sporting event that consists of 7 track and fields for two days

pentathlon is an ancient athletics discipline, featuring five events: stadion, wrestling, long jump, javelin and discus.

Answer:

In Olympics, decathlon and heptathlon are track and field sports. Decathlon for men, heptathlon for women. In latin, “deka” stands for “ten”, and “hepta” stands for “seven”.

Decathlon contains:

1) 100 m dash

2) Long jump

3) Shot put

4) High jump

5) 400 m run

6) 110 m with hurdlers

7) Discus throw

8) Pole vault

9) Javelin throw

10) 1500 m (the final event)

Heptathlon contains:

1) 100 m with hurdlers

2) High jump

3) Shot put

4) 200 m dash

5) Long jump

6) Javelin throw

7) 800 m run (the final event)

As you see, the difference is, that the men have one more running event. They also have pole vault, and discus throw, which makes it ten.

Pentathlons contain:

The only “Olympic pentathlon” there is, is “modern pentathlon”. This one focuses on entirely different variation of sports, and has both, men’s and women’s category. “Penta” means “five” in latin. It contains:

Épée fencing (with a “sword”)

200 m swimming (freestyle)

Show jumping (you ride and jump on a horse in this one)

Shooting (10 m distance)

Running (3 km)

Quite recently, shooting and running have been merged into one event, where the athletes always shoot down 5 targets followed by 1 km run. (This repeats twice more in the race.)
