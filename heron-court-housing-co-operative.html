25. what is herons formula? ​


Answer:

In geometry, Heron's formula, named after Hero of Alexandria, gives the area of a triangle when the lengths of all three sides are known. Unlike other triangle area formulae, there is no need to calculate angles or other distances in the triangle first.

Answer:

Herons Formula

Step-by-step explanation:

Heron's formula, formula credited to Heron of Alexandria (c. 62 ce) for finding the area of a triangle in terms of the lengths of its sides. In symbols, if a, b, and c are the lengths of the sides: Area = Square root of√s(s - a)(s - b)(s - c) where s is half the perimeter, or (a + b + c)/2.
