21. Different types of microcomputers/personal computers1.)Laptops2.)Desktops3.)Netbook4.)Personal digital assistants(PDAs)5.)Tablet computers6.)Wearable computers​


DEFINITION OF EACH

TYPES OF MICROCOMPUTERS/PERSONAL COMPUTERS

1. LAPTOP - is a small, portable personal computer with a "clamshell" form factor, typically having a thin LCD or LED computer screen. Suitable for traveling.

2. DESKTOPS - a personal computer designed for regular use at a single location on or near a desk or table due to its size and power requirements.

3. NETBOOK - belongs to a class of small, lightweight, legacy-free, and inexpensive laptop computers introduced in 2007.

4. PERSONAL DIGITAL ASSISTANTS (PDAS) - are small networked computers which can fit in the palm of your hand.

5. TABLET COMPUTERS - that is intermediate in size between a laptop computer and a smartphone. Early tablet computers used either a keyboard or a stylus to input information, but these methods were subsequently displaced by touch screens.

6. WEARABLE COMPUTERS - also known as body-borne computer, is a computing device worn on the body. The definition of 'wearable computer' may be narrow or broad, extending to smartphones or even ordinary wristwatches.
