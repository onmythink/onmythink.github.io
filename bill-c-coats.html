8. _________8. It is applied last over the color or enamels.a. Base coatb. Ridge Fillersc. Top coatd. Cuticle Oil​


Answer:

A. Base coat

Explanation:

Enamel paint is paint that air-dries to a hard, usually glossy, finish, used for coating surfaces that are outdoors or otherwise subject to hard wear or variations in temperature; it should not be confused with decorated objects in "painted enamel", where vitreous enamel is applied with brushes and fired in a kiln. The name is something of a misnomer, as in reality, most commercially available enamel paints are significantly softer than either vitreous enamel or stoved synthetic resins, and are totally different in composition; vitreous enamel is applied as a powder or paste and then fired at high temperature. There is no generally accepted definition or standard for use of the term enamel paint, and not all enamel-type paints may use it.

25. it is clear polish applied to the nail plate before the application of colored polisha: base coatb: alcoholc: top coatd: cuticle solvent​


A. Base Coat

Further explanation:

The base coat is nothing but a layer of transparent paint before you start applying the nail color. Applying a Base Coat is an important step that is often overlooked. This is so that the nail polish formula does not touch the surface of the nail, as well as to make the nails stronger and neater. The base coat is clear so it won't disturb and damage the desired nail art design.

Base coat is a nail art item that usually contains vitamins that are used before you apply nail polish. This is so that your nails are protected from paint that has an unfavorable effect on the nails.

Explanation of other answers:

B. Alcohol

Further explanation:

The alcohol in nail art is found in nail polish remover products. This is to remove the nail polish stuck to the nail. If in the process of making nail art there is an untidy scratched nail polish, then you can remove it with a nail polish remover. Just drop the liquid onto a cotton swab and then wipe it over the nail polish you want to remove.

C. Top Coat

Further explanation:

Top Coat is a product that is used as a finishing product in nail art creations to make it last a long time and not easily peel off. This product is a product that is applied last when finished with nail makeup. Its function is to perfect the results of the nails that have been decorated. Your nail art will last longer and stick more if you use a top coat.

D. Cuticle Solvent

Further explanation:

A cuticle solvent, also known as a cuticle pusher, is a tool used to make your nails look clean at the tips. Before you start applying the base, use a cuticle pusher to push the cuticles in slightly.
