9. Many foreign companies have decided to offshore some of their processes to BPO companies in the Philippines. You have learned about the upside and downside of offshoring for both the clients and service provider. As somebody who is either working or intending to work in the IT-BPM industry, what are the opportunities and challenges in working in an offshore location (Philippines) but still provide service to a major international company based abroad?


Opportunities of Offshoring

1. economies of scale

When a business opens an office or a factory overseas, it is not only taking advantage of lower labor costs but also of scale efficiencies already in place. Rent, Internet access, and other cost factors may be cheaper in the host nation than in the place of origin.

2. close collaboration

Offshoring allows for closer physical collaboration, which provides the business more control. The benefit of shared space collaboration is that it allows for supervision of work without the filters or delays brought on by technology.

3. favorable government policies

Some countries offer corporations that invest in their economies unique privileges and incentives. These include tax breaks and easy access to finance, both of which could boost a company's bottom line. Exploring such cash flow improvements can significantly improve the upfront cost of outsourcing core functions.

Challenges of Offshoring

1. increase unemployment

The main complaint against businesses that outsource is that it raises unemployment in the local economy.

2. cultural and social differences

The client will become fully absorbed in the host nation's culture and social customs. Communication and productivity may be impacted by this. Time zone variations, in contrast to outsourcing, may act against the offshoring company because output may be delayed as a result of changes in workforce availability. This may increase unintentional costs for some organizations and job activities.

3. security issues

You run the risk of security lapses and data integrity compromises whenever you provide data to another entity. Even while working in a shared area, there will inevitably be data transfer problems. Sending sensitive data between two locations can still be risky, even if an offshore expansion is nominally a part of the parent firm.
