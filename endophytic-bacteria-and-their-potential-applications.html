Bacteria in medicine

Bacterial diseases have played a dominant role in human history. Widespread epidemics of cholera and plague reduced populations of humans in some areas of the world by more than one-third. Bacterial pneumonia was probably the major cause of death in the aged. Perhaps more armies were defeated by typhus, dysentery, and other bacterial infections than by force of arms. With modern advances in plumbing and sanitation, the development of bacterial vaccines, and the discovery of antibacterial antibiotics, the incidence of bacterial disease has been reduced. Bacteria have not disappeared as infectious agents, however, since they continue to evolve, creating increasingly virulent strains and acquiring resistance to many antibiotics.


6. A particular bacteria under study triples in population every 24 hours if left untreated. Once an organism gets contact with this bacteria, initially an average count of 8 may be acquired and is notable to be enough to potentially increase and harm the organism. If no treatment will be given for a week, how many bacteria are expected to grow in population in the organism?


Answer:

There will be 17496 bacteria after a week.

Step-by-step explanation:

Use the formula [tex]y=Ab^{t}[/tex], where [tex]A[/tex] is the initial number of bacteria, [tex]b[/tex] is the growth factor and [tex]t[/tex] is the number of hours. Based on the given, the initial number of bacteria is 8. So,  [tex]A=8[/tex].

Start by finding the value of [tex]b[/tex]. Since the bacteria triples every 24 hours, there will be [tex]3\cdot8=24[/tex] bacteria after 24 hours. Then, substitute [tex]A=8[/tex], [tex]t=24[/tex] and [tex]y=24[/tex] into [tex]y=Ab^{t}[/tex].

  [tex]24=8b^{24}[/tex]

[tex]8b^{24}=24[/tex]       Switch sides.

 [tex]b^{24}=3[/tex]         Divide both sides by 8.

    [tex]b=\left ( 3 \right )^{\frac{1}{24}}[/tex]   Raise both sides to the [tex]\frac{1}{24}[/tex] power.

Next, find the value of [tex]t[/tex]. Since there are 24 hours a day and 7 days a week, there are [tex]7\cdot24=168[/tex] hours in a week. So, [tex]t=168[/tex].

Finally, solve for [tex]y[/tex]. Substitute [tex]A=8[/tex], [tex]b=\left ( 3 \right )^{\frac{1}{24}}[/tex] and [tex]t=168[/tex] into [tex]y=Ab^{t}[/tex].

[tex]\begin{aligned}y&=8\cdot\left (3^{\frac{1}{24}} \right )^{168}\\&=8\cdot3^{\frac{168}{24}}\\&=8\cdot3^{7}\\&=8\cdot2187\\&=17496\end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, there will be 17496 bacteria after a week.
