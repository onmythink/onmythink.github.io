30. Paul's father will pay for his new car in 60-months payments. if his car loan cost 1,023,660,then how much will he pay for each month?​


Answer:

Computations based on the number of months

Step-by-step explanation:

Car loan as applicable for an interest rate which depends on the number of payable months.

Here are the possible cost based on the interest rates.

If the car loan cost 1,023,660.00

A. Payable for 12 months with 4% interest rate per annum or year

Php 1,023.660.00 multiplied by 0.04 is equal to Php 40,946.40

-Adding up the principal amount and the interest rate

1,023,660.00 + 40,946.40 = 1,064,606.40

-1,064,606.40 divide by 12 months is equal to Php 88,717.20 per month rate.

B.  Payable for 36 months with a 6% interest rate per annum or year

-Php 1,023.660.00 multiplied by 0.06 is equal to Php 61,419.60 interest rate per year/ multiply by 3 years (36 month) = Php 184,258.80 total interest

-Adding up the principal amount and the interest rate

1,023,660.0 + 184,258.80 = Php 1,0207,918.80

-Php 1,0207,918.80  divide by 36 months is equal to Php 33,553.30

per month rate.

C. Payable for 60 months with a 8% interest rate per annum or year

-Php 1,023.660.00 multiplied by 0.08 is equal to 81,892.80 interest rate

per year/ multiply by 5 years ( 60 months) = 409,464.00

-Adding up the principal amount and the interest rate

1,023,660.0 +409,464.00 = Php 1,433,124.00

Php 1,433,124.00 divide by 60 months is equal to Php 23,885.40

per month rate.

D. If Principal amount of Php 1,023,660.00 has no interest rate value.

- 1,023,660.00 divide by 60 months =Php 17,061 per month rate.
