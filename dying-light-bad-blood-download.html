1. Use Familiar Shapes. Colored buttons with shadows around them are the most familiar designs for users of websites, apps, or other platforms. This is because when they see something with dimensions, they will know that the image or button is clickable. Color selection is also very important. For example, use green or blue for actions related to consent.

2. Leave Some Space Around The Button. When designing a button, look at the space around the button. Make sure that the button doesn't blend into the surrounding text. This also relates to button placement. Try to put it in a strategic place where it is most likely to be seen and clicked by the user. Give space between text or images with the buttons provided, so that users can see them clearly.

3. Make Sure The Button Size Fits. The size of the design also affects the effectiveness of the button, you know. Often times, buttons that are too small will be difficult to click if you are using a cellphone. However, don't make buttons that are too big and take up too much space.

It's important to let the user know what the button can do. For example, there is a button to delete files.
