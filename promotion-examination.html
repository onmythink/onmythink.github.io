Branches of Anthrophology

Anthropology is the study of science promoting the holistic study of humans. Anthropology studies humans as biological and social beings

Following are the major branches of anthropology that a prospective student should be aware of :

Socio-cultural anthropology, Sociocultural Anthropology is the study of human individuals and societies, their behaviours and beliefs, and everything about human culture.Physical anthropology, Is the branch of antropology concerned that deals with the evolution of humans, their variability, and adaptations to environmental stresses. Using an evolutionary perspective, we examine not only the physical form of humans like bones, muscles, and organs. But also how it functions to allow survival and reproduction.Archaeological anthropology, Archaeology is similar to anthropology in that it focuses on understanding human culture from the deepest history up until the recent past. It differs from anthropology in that it focuses specifically on analyzing material remains such as artifact and architectural remnants.Linguistic anthropology, Linguistic anthropology studies the nature of human languages in the context of those cultures that developed them. Scholars in the field seek to understand the social and cultural foundations of language itself, while exploring how social and cultural formations are grounded in linguistic practices.Applied Anthropology, Is the branch of antropology who sometimes called the "fifth sub-field", applied anthropology is simply defined as the use of anthropological knowledge and skills to solve real-world problems.

9. 1. determine the situations or conditions that promote respect for others? 2. examine your practices of respect for others; and. 3. value the impact of respect in maintaining positive relationship with others? ​


Answer:

Situations or conditions that promote respect for others include: communicating in a way that is supportive and non-judgmental; honoring others’ perspectives, feelings, and beliefs; recognizing individual differences and understanding that those differences are valid; expressing appreciation for others' contributions and accomplishments; and avoiding gossip and unfounded rumors.

Examining one's own practices of respect for others means looking for ways to live up to the standards of respect as listed above. Taking a moment to ask oneself if one is speaking kindly, offering support, and showing appreciation to those in one's life can be a powerful way to focus on respect.

Valuing the impact of respect in maintaining positive relationships with others involves recognizing the importance of respect in foster healthy and supportive relationships. Respect helps foster connection and trust, while disrespect can lead to tension and discord. Taking the time to recognize and value respect can be an invaluable tool in maintaining positive relationships with those around us.

Scope:

A public-private partnership DOTS (PPMD) - Private sector engagement, including private practitioners, pharmacies, and hospitals, to implement NTP policies and recommendations and thereby support TB control efforts. 3. Program: PINGGANG PINOY Healthy food plate for Filipino adults

Scope:

Pinggang Pinoy is a new, simple-to-understand food guide that employs a familiar food plate paradigm to transmit the proper food group proportions on a per-meal basis to suit Filipino adults' energy and nutrient needs. Pinggang Pinoy is a visual tool that delivers effective nutritional and healthy lifestyle messages to assist Filipinos adopt healthy eating habits at mealtime.

Will the FNRI Daily Nutritional Guide (DNG) Pyramid be replaced with Pinggang Pinoy?

The "Pinggang Pinoy" can be used alongside, but not in instead of, the existing DNG Pyramid for Filipinos. Pinggang Pinoy, according to FNRI, is a quick and easy reference to how much to eat at mealtime, whereas the DNG Pyramid gives the entire day's food intake advice at a glance.

The “Pinggang Pinoy” and the DNG Pyramid for Filipinos are both based on the most up-to-date research on how our diet, drink, and activity choices effect our health.

14. 1. Examine the role of the state (government) in promoting a healthy ethical climate alongside economic growth.


Answer: The role of the state (government) in promoting a healthy ethical climate alongside economic growth is crucial for the sustainable development of a country. The government has a responsibility to ensure that ethical principles and values are upheld in all aspects of society, including the economy.

One way that the government can promote a healthy ethical climate is by enacting laws and regulations that encourage businesses to behave in an ethical manner. For example, the government can impose penalties on companies that engage in corrupt practices or violate ethical standards. This can include fines, loss of licenses, and even imprisonment for individuals involved in such activities.

Another way that the government can promote ethical behavior is by setting up oversight bodies and regulatory agencies to monitor and enforce compliance with ethical standards. These agencies can investigate complaints, monitor the behavior of companies, and ensure that ethical standards are being upheld in all aspects of the economy.

In addition, the government can also promote ethical behavior through education and awareness campaigns. By promoting ethical principles and values through the education system, the government can help to instill these values in future generations. Similarly, public awareness campaigns can be used to raise awareness about the importance of ethical behavior in all aspects of society, including the economy.

Finally, the government can play a role in promoting ethical behavior by leading by example. By adopting and adhering to ethical principles in their own actions and policies, the government can set an example for the rest of society to follow.

Overall, the government plays a critical role in promoting a healthy ethical climate alongside economic growth. By enacting laws and regulations, setting up oversight bodies and regulatory agencies, promoting education and awareness, and leading by example, the government can help to ensure that ethical principles and values are upheld in all aspects of society, including the economy. This, in turn, can contribute to sustainable economic growth and the well-being of all citizens.
