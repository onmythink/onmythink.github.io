18. A community covers 300 hectares of land. 1/8 of the land was reserved for common areas like parks and roads and the rest for residences. How many hectares were used for residences ?​


262.5 hectares were used for resedences.

because...

We divide 300 by 8 which yields 37.5

In order to cover the whole 300 hectares, we will add a fraction to 1/8 in order to get the whole number 1.

We can get the fraction by subtracting 1/8 from 1 which is equivalent to 1/7. Same as ratio proportion, 1:7 = 300.

We will then multiply 37.5 by 1 because it's 1/8 of the land was reserved for common areas.

And for the area reserved for the residences, we will have 7 since we already used 1 for the common areas.

So, 7×37.5=262.5

Answer:

262.5 hectares

Step-by-step explanation:

Since 1/8 of the land is reserved for common areas,

1 - 1/8 or 7/8 of the land is reserved for the residences.

To get the area, we multiply it to the total.

( 1 - 1/8 ) * 300 hectares

= (7/8) * 300 hectares

= 262.5 hectares

27. The town of Brookfield sent out a survey to its residents to determine what new landmark they should build in the City Center. Of the residents who were surveyed 2/5, of the residents voted for a park, 1/4 of the residents voted for a garden, and the remaining residents voted for a zoo. If a total of 14,000 residents were surveyed, how many residents voted for a zoo?


The proportion of residents who voted for a park is 2/5, and the proportion who voted for a garden is 1/4. Therefore, the proportion of residents who voted for a zoo is:

1 - (2/5 + 1/4) = 1 - (8/20 + 5/20) = 1 - (13/20) = 7/20

If 7/20 of the residents voted for a zoo, and a total of 14,000 residents were surveyed, the number of residents who voted for a zoo is:

(7/20) x 14,000 = 4,900

Therefore, 4,900 residents voted for a zoo.
