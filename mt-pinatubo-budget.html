10. Mt. Pinatubo in Luzon​


Answer:

Mount Pinatubo in Luzon

Mount Pinatubo is one of the 24 active volcanoes in the Philippines. This volcano is found in the island of Luzon, and it is a part of the volcanic belt influenced by the subduction of the Manila Trench. Long believed to be a part of the larger Zambales mountain ranges, the people in the Philippines did not expect the volcano to blow up in 1991. As early as April 1991, native Aetas who inhabit the volcano reported seismic activities, rock slides, and the emission of gases near the volcano. PHIVOLCS and USGS partnered in studying Pinatubo, and they came into a conclusion that it will blow up. Evacuations were conducted across Central Luzon, and on June 12, 1991, the volcano erupted. The climactic eruption happened on June 15, 1991, and to make matters worse, typhoon Diding (Yunya) mixed its winds and rains with the ash from Pinatubo.

Location of Mount Pinatubo

There are two ways on how we can locate Mount Pinatubo on the map – the first one is through absolute location, and the second one is through relative location.

Absolute location:

The absolute location of Mount Pinatubo is 15.1429° N, 120.3496° E

Relative Location:

North – the mountains of San Jose and Capas, TarlacEast – the cities of Mabalacat and Angeles, PampangaWest – the province of ZambalesSouth – Mount Negron and the provinces of Pampanga and Bataan

27. mt. pinatubo in science​


Answer:

Mount Pinatubo and Science

When it comes to science, we can say that Mount Pinatubo has a lot of contribution to it because it made people more aware about the dangers of a volcano, and it also increased the interest of the Filipinos when it comes to discussions about volcanic eruptions.

In addition, the eruption of Mount Pinatubo is the 2nd largest in the 20th century, next only to the eruption of Novarupta in Alaska. It is also considered as one of the best predicted eruptions in the history of mankind. Because of the partnership between USGS and PHIVOLCS, millions of lives have been spared.

28. mt Pinatubo history​


Answer:

The Pinatubo eruption on 15 June 1991 was the second largest volcanic eruption of the 20th century. Pinatubo is a complex of lava domes located 100 km NW of Manila city, Luzon Island, Philippines. Prior to the eruption, Pinatubo was a little known volcano and it had been dormant for 400 years.
