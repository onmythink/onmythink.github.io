-Food quality: This refers to the overall characteristics of food that determine its value and acceptability to consumers. Food quality is influenced by various factors such as taste, appearance, nutritional content, safety, and freshness.

-Street food vendors: These are individuals or groups of people who prepare and sell food on the street or in public places, typically from mobile food carts or stalls.

BACKGROUND OF THE STUDY:

Street food vending is a common practice in many cities and towns around the world, and it provides a source of income for many people. In many developing countries, street food is an important source of nutrition for urban residents. However, street food is often associated with poor hygiene practices, and it is a major cause of foodborne illness in many parts of the world.

Bonifacio Plaza in Pasig City is a popular destination for street food vendors. The plaza is located in the heart of the city and attracts many visitors every day. However, the hygienic practices of the street food vendors at Bonifacio Plaza have not been studied in detail.

This study aims to investigate the hygienic practices and food quality among street food vendors at Bonifacio Plaza Pasig City. The study will examine the food preparation practices of the vendors, the sources of water used in food preparation, and the handling and storage practices of the food. The study will also assess the microbiological quality of the food sold by the vendors.

The findings of this study will be useful for the local government, food safety regulators, and the street food vendors themselves. The results will help to identify areas where improvements in hygienic practices can be made and provide guidance for the development of food safety regulations. The study will also help the street food vendors to understand the importance of good hygiene practices in food preparation and storage, which will ultimately benefit the health of their customers.
