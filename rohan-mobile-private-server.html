23. What Are the Differences among the Types, Sizes, and Functions in the Following Categories: Personal Computers (Desktop), Mobile Computers and Mobile Devices, Game Consoles, Servers, Mainframes, Supercomputers, and Embedded Computers?


Personal Computers

Types:

There are two basic types—desktop and tower models—but several variants exist. Then there are portable computers, which are tiny enough to be carried. Portable computers include notebooks, subnotebooks, palmtops, PDAs, and handheld computers.

Size:

The mainframe computer is a massive computer covering around 1,000 sq ft. Microcomputers are the most popular general-purpose computers commonly utilized for day-to-day tasks. On the other hand, mini Computers seem to be mid-sized Computers.

Function:

Input, processing, storage & output are a computer system's 4 primary equipment functions.

Mobile Computers

Types:

There are six major categories of mobile computing devices: laptops, notebooks, tablets, personal digital assistants, smartphones & portable data terminals.

Size:

There are minicomputers, the microcomputer, the mainframe computer & the supercomputer. Mini Computers are computers of medium size. There are 4 sorts of computers, based on their size.

Function:

Mobility in terms of location. Because of this, customers can now work from any location as long as there is an active internet connection. Saves Time. Increased Effort and Productivity. Facilitation of Research Entertainment. Efforts Made to Simplify and Streamline Business Procedures

Mobile Devices

Types:

There are 4 major categories of mobile networks: cellular communication, 4G networking, Wi-Fi connections & Bluetooth connectivity.

Size:

16:9 is the most prevalent aspect ratio, and due to its substantial width, it is termed a panoramic format.

Function:

Everything is compact in portable size function.

Game Consoles

Types:

There are home consoles, handhelds, and hybrids. Most consoles are easy to use.

Size:

- Largest size: Early consoles used PCBs, ICs, and EPROMs. Dedicated consoles use CPU registers.

- Medium size: Then came semiconductors. Reduced chip size (nanometers) allows more transistors and components, improving circuit speeds, frequency, and heat dissipation.

- Smaller size: "Bit fights" dominated the 1980s and 1990s. Consoles have more storage and networking. The developer improved as PCs and consoles converged. Combinations save space and money. Consoles store less than PCs.

Function:

A video game console bridges the gap between arcades and PCs designed for video gaming at home. A PC is capable of running games and can be constructed with arcade-style controllers. Like a console and restricted to public usage, controllers characterize arcades.

Servers

Types:

Web server, Proxy server, Virtual machine, File transfer protocol server, Application server, File server, Database server, and Mail server.

Size:

The server's rack unit (U) height indicates the space required to house the equipment. The industry standard is 19 inches wide and 36 inches deep. There are three sizes of servers: 1U, 2U, and 4U.

Function:

A server's goal is to distribute data and share resources and tasks. A server computer can also serve its computer programs.

Mainframes

Types:

Mainframes are a type of machine characterized by their size, storage capacity, processing speed, and high level of dependability. Large enterprises utilize them mainly for mission-critical applications needing massive data processing.

Size:

During their peak physical size, a typical mainframe occupied between 2,000 and 10,000 sq ft (600 to 3000 sq m).

Function:

Mainframes are data servers built up to one trillion online daily transactions with the utmost security and dependability. They have high-performance computers with vast memory and processors that conduct billions of basic calculations and transactions in real-time.

Supercomputers

Types:

General-purpose supercomputers and special-purpose supercomputers are the two broad categories of supercomputers. The general-purpose supercomputers are vector processing supercomputers, densely coupled cluster computers, and bargain computers.

Size:

A supercomputer, in contrast, has a storage capacity of 200 to 300 gigabytes or more.

Function:

Such jobs as quantum physics, weather prediction, climate science, oil and gas exploration, molecular dynamics, and physical simulations have been accomplished by supercomputers.

Embedded Computers

Types:

Embedded computers include Rugged Industrial Box PC, Panel PC, Mini PC, Industrial Rackmount Server, in-Vehicle Computer, and IoT Gateway.

Size:

Small-scale embedded systems frequently employ a microcontroller with little more than 8 bits. Medium-sized embedded systems use the larger one (16-32 bits) and frequently interconnect microcontrollers.

Function:

An embedded computer is not utilized independently but as a larger device or system component. An embedded computer can perform a highly particular function. Its uses include industrial automation, in-vehicle computing, digital signage, robotics, and more.
