18. Of 240 students, 176 are on the honor roll, 48 are members of the varsity team, and 36 are in the honor roll and are also members of the varsity team. What is the probability that a randomly selected student is on the honor roll or is a member of the varsity team?


Given:

Students on the honor roll (H) is 176

Varsity team members (V) are 48

Students on the honor roll and are also members of the varsity team (H and V) = 36

These are not mutually exclusive events. Hence,

Probability (H or V) = Probability (H) + Probability (V) – Probability (both H and V)

= 176/240 + 48/240 – 36/240= 188/240 or 47/60

Probability (H or V) = 47/60 or 78.33%

OF 240 students, 176 are on the honor roll, 48 are members of the varsity team and 36 are in the honor roll, and are also members. of the varsity team what is the probability a randomly selected student is on honor roll is a member of the varsity team. Mutually Exclusive or Not Mutually Exclusive


The events "on the honor roll" and "a member of the varsity team" are not mutually exclusive since there are 36 students who are both on the honor roll and a member of the varsity team.

To find the probability that a randomly selected student is on the honor roll and is also a member of the varsity team, we can use the formula:

P(on honor roll and varsity) = P(on honor roll) x P(varsity | on honor roll)

where P(on honor roll) = 176/240 = 11/15 is the probability of being on the honor roll, and P(varsity | on honor roll) = 36/176 = 9/44 is the probability of being a member of the varsity team given that the student is on the honor roll.

Therefore,

P(on honor roll and varsity) = (11/15) x (9/44)

P(on honor roll and varsity) = 99/660

P(on honor roll and varsity) = 0.15

So the probability that a randomly selected student is on the honor roll and is also a member of the varsity team is 0.15 or 15%.
