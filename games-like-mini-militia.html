Answer:

Explanation:

Like playing cards, there are a lot of ways to play dominoes. The most famous one is blocking which is player between two players. In this mode, you link dominoes of the same number, and the first person to have no dominoes wins, or the person who cannot play a move loses.

There is a scoring variant of the game which allows 2 to 4 players. The game is played similar to blocking, but there are scores given to players.

7 domino tiles are distributed to each player, and the rest are put in a pool, facedown. The player with the highest double domino plays first and puts that domino in the middle, then draws from the pool to replace his domino tile (if possible). The next player then puts another domino such that adjacent pairs of tile ends must match, and draws one from a pool. If one player cannot put a tile, he must draw another domino until he/she can lay a domino. If there are no more dominoes to draw, that player passes. The round ends when one player has no more tiles, or no more players can continue. The winner of that round is the one who has no more tiles to play, or the one that caused the block.

This is similar to blocking, but there is a scoring at the end of each round. If the round ends when one player has no more tiles, the other players lay down there tiles, and the winner scores points with the value of all the other players' tiles. If the round ends that no players can continue linking, the player scores the same, not counting what he/she had. The next round begins. The overall winner of the game is the first one to reach an agreed amount of points (usually 100, 200, etc.)
