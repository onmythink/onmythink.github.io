1. what is the correct formula in determining maximum heart rate (MCR)​


Answer:

Your maximal heart rate may be calculated based on your age. Subtract your age from 220 to get an estimate of your maximal age-related heart rate. The predicted maximum age-related heart rate for a 50-year-old person, for example, would be 220 – 50 years = 170 beats per minute (bpm).


2. Isosceles CAR is inscribed E.. if mCR=130 fin


Problem:

Isosceles ∆CAR is inscribed in circle E. If m of arc CR = 130⁰, find m ∠CAR.

Step-by-step explanation:

inscribed angle = ½ intercepted arc

m ∠CAR = ½ m arc CR

m ∠CAR = ½(130⁰)

m ∠CAR = 65⁰
