[tex]\huge\pink{ANSWER}[/tex]

a) The distance between station B and station C is 48 km.

b) The time when the freight train left station B is 10:35 a.m.

[tex]\huge\pink{SOLUTION}[/tex]

a) Let x be the distance between stations B and C. Then the distance from station C to station A is [tex]\displaystyle(148 - x)(148−x)[/tex]m. By the time of the meeting at station C, the express train travelled for [tex]\displaystyle\frac{148-x}{80}+\frac{10}{60}[/tex] hours and the freight train travelled for [tex]\displaystyle\frac{x}{36}+\frac{5}{60}[/tex] hours. The trains left at the same time, so: [tex]\displaystyle\frac{148 - x}{80} + \frac{1}{6} = \frac{x}{36} + \frac{1}{12}[/tex]. The common denominator for 6, 12, 36, 80 is 720. Then

[tex]\displaystyle{9(148 - x) +120 = 20x}[/tex]

[tex]\displaystyle{1332 - 9x + 120 = 20x}[/tex]

[tex]\displaystyle{29x = 1392}[/tex]

[tex]\displaystyle{x = 48}[/tex].

Therefore the distance between stations B and C is 48 km.

b) By the time of the meeting at station C the freight train rode for [tex]\displaystyle\frac{48}{36} + \frac{5}{60}[/tex] hours, i.e. [tex]\displaystyle{1}[/tex] hour and [tex]\displaystyle{25}[/tex] min.

Therefore it left station B at [tex]\displaystyle 12 - (1 + \frac{25}{60}) = 10 + \frac{35}{60}[/tex] hours, i.e. at 10:35 am.

11d = 675

d = 675%2F11 = 61.364.

Rate x Time = Distance

Train A's rate is 70 mph, so: 70 mph x 1 hr = 70 miles

Train B's rate is 60 mph, so: 60 mph x 1 hr = 60 miles
