11. Explain the concept of entropy​


Answer:

Entropy, the measure of a system's thermal energy per unit temperature that is unavailable for doing useful work. Because work is obtained from ordered molecular motion, the amount of entropy is also a measure of the molecular disorder, or randomness, of a system.

15. (ENTROPY AND THE SECOND LAW OF THERMODYNAMICS) A 4.50 KG ICE AT -15 OC IS PLACED ON THE TABLE WHICH HAS A CONSTANT TEMPERATURE OF 28.25 OC. A. CALCULATE THE CHANGE IN ENTROPY OF ICE AS IT MELTS AND ABSORBS 3.05X106J OF HEAT? B. WHAT IS THE CHANGE IN ENTROPY OF THE TABLE? C. WHAT IS THE CHANGE IN ENTROPY IN THE UNIVERSE?


A. To calculate the change in entropy of the ice as it melts and absorbs 3.05x106J of heat, we can use the formula: ΔS = Q/T

Where ΔS is the change in entropy, Q is the heat absorbed, and T is the temperature. In this case, we are given that Q = 3.05x106J and T = 28.25 OC = 301.4K. So, ΔS = (3.05x106 J) / (301.4K) = 10157.5 J/K

B. The change in entropy of the table is zero, as the table is assumed to be a constant temperature heat reservoir, that means it does not change its own entropy as it takes in or releases heat.

C. The change in entropy in the universe is the sum of the changes in entropy of the ice and the table. Since the change in entropy of the table is zero, the change in entropy in the universe is equal to the change in entropy of the ice, which is 10157.5 J/K

Note that the above calculations are based on the assumption of the system being at equilibrium and the heat absorbed by the ice is used only for melting it and not for any other processes.

Eutrophication most commonly arises from the oversupply of nutrients, most commonly as nitrogen or phosphorus, which leads to overgrowth of plants and algae in aquatic ecosystems. 19. stages of eutrophication​


Answer:   EXCESS NUTRIENTS: First, farmers apply fertilizer to the soil. Then, excess nutrients run off from the field into the water.

ALGAE BLOOM: Next, the fertilizer rich in nitrate and phosphate spark the overgrowth of algae in water bodies.

OXYGEN DEPLETION: When algae forms, it blocks sunlight from entering water and uses up oxygen. Eventually, water becomes oxygen-depleted.

DEAD ZONES: Finally, water that is completely depleted of oxygen becomes a dead zone and can no longer support life.

29. results of eutrophication​


Explanation:

Eutrophication can have serious, long-term effects. The most notable effect of eutrophication is algal blooms. When a bloom occurs, the stream, river, lake or ocean becomes covered with algae, which is usually bright green. In addition to looking pretty ugly, it also blocks light from reaching the water. This prevents the aquatic plants from photosynthesizing, a process which provides oxygen in the water to animals that need it, like fish and crabs


30. Eutrophication solution?​


Answer:

Eutrophication can be minimized by regulating the nutrient sources, reducing the use of fertilizers, proper soil management practices, implementing mathematical models, phytoremediation etc. Among these, public awareness of eutrophication can play an important role in preventing the eutrophication of water bodies.
