10. E. A man standing on the side looking at the top of the tower and it forms an angle of 15°. The tower is 120 m high. Find the distance between the man and the tower.


Answer:

447.76 meters

Step-by-step explanation:

In that problem, we are given that a man is standing on the side of a tower and looking at the top of the tower. The angle formed between the line of sight of the man and the ground is 15 degrees. The height of the tower is also given as 120 meters.

We are asked to find the distance between the man and the tower. This can be found using trigonometry, specifically the tangent function.

Let x be the distance between the man and the tower. We can then use the tangent of 15 degrees to find x:

tan(15) = opposite/adjacent = 120/x

We can then solve for x by multiplying both sides by x and dividing by tan(15):

x = 120 / tan(15)

Using a calculator, we can find that tan(15) is approximately 0.268, so:

x = 120 / 0.268 ≈ 447.76 meters

Therefore, the distance between the man and the tower is approximately 447.76 meters.
