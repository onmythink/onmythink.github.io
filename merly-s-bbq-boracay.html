13. Bringing the dolls poem by merlie alunan


The poem bringing the doll written by Merlie Alunan is about a mother's realization that in one's moving on, one need not bring only those considered important. The predominant image of the poem is the doll. It can initially be found in the first 3 lines, which describr the doll as mangled.


Two dolls in rags and tatters,
one missing an arm and a leg,
the other blind in one eye—I grabbed them from her arms,
“No,” I said, “they cannot come.”Each tight baggage
I had packed
only for the barest need:
no room for sentiment or memory
to clutter with loose ends
my stern resolve. I reasoned,
even a child must learn
she cannot take what must be left behind.And so the boat turned seaward,
a smart wind blowing dry
the stealthy tears I could not wipe.
Then I saw—rags, tatters and all—
there among the neat trim packs,
the dolls I ruled to leave behind.Her silence should have warned me
she knew her burdens
as I knew mine:
her clean white years unlived—
and paid my price.
She battened on a truth
she knew I too must own:
when what’s at stake
is loyalty or love,
hers are the true rights.
Her own faiths she must keep, not I.

14. coliform bacteria contamination in Boracay in the 1990's​


Boracay is a popular tourist destination in the Philippines known for its beautiful beaches and crystal clear waters. In the 1990s, there were reports of coliform bacteria contamination in the waters of Boracay. Coliform bacteria are a type of bacteria that are commonly found in the environment, including in soil, water, and plant material. They are used as indicator organisms to test the quality of water because their presence can indicate the presence of other harmful bacteria.

In the 1990s, high levels of coliform bacteria were found in the waters of Boracay, indicating that the water was not safe for swimming or other activities. This was likely due to a combination of factors, including inadequate sewage treatment and pollution from commercial and residential development. The contamination of the waters in Boracay led to concerns about the health and safety of tourists and residents, and efforts were made to address the problem.

In the years since, the Philippine government and local authorities have implemented measures to improve water quality and reduce pollution in Boracay. These efforts have included the construction of new sewage treatment facilities, the enforcement of stricter regulations on development and waste management, and the implementation of programs to educate the public about the importance of protecting the environment. As a result, the waters of Boracay have improved significantly, and it is now considered a safe and enjoyable destination for tourists.

16. Victor sold BBQ seasoning. He spent $9.03 to make the BBQ seasoning. After he sold all the BBQ seasoning, he had $43.42. How much profit did Victor make? A. $34.39 B. $9.03 C. $34.64 D. $43.42


Answer: A. $34.39

Step-by-step explanation:

Revenue = $43.42

Cost = $9.03

Profit = Revenue - Cost

Profit = $43.42 - $9.03

Profit = $34.39

Therefore, Victor made a profit of $34.39 from selling the BBQ seasoning.
