4. background of the study of terrarium making?​


Background of the study of terrarium making?​

The first terrarium was created in 1842 by Dr. Nathaniel Bagshaw Ward, an English botanist. He found the terrarium by accident when rearing moth pupa in a sealed glass container. Ward observed that the moss and ferns flourished in the environment he had created for the moths. A terrarium, also known as a glass garden, a Wardian case, or a vivarium, is a glass-enclosed enclosure with glass sides and typically a glass top that is used to house plants or semi-terrestrial animals. Plant or animal proliferation, decoration, or scientific observation are all possibilities. A terrarium is a glass-enclosed indoor garden that allows you to keep an eye on your plant collection. A terrarium is a glass container that holds soil and plants and may be opened to maintain the plants inside. A terrarium, on the other hand, can be open to the atmosphere rather of being sealed. Terraria are usually kept as decorative or ornamental items. Closed terrariums have translucent walls that allow both heat and light to enter, producing a unique environment for plant growth.

29. what is terrarium plants? ​


A terrarium (plural: terraria or terrariums) is usually a sealable glass container containing soil and plants, and can be opened for maintenance to access the plants inside. However, terraria can also be open to the atmosphere rather than being sealed. Terraria are often kept as decorative or ornamental items. Closed terraria create a unique environment for plant growth, as the transparent walls allow for both heat and light to enter the terrarium. The sealed container combined with the heat entering the terrarium allows for the creation of a small scale water cycle. This happens because moisture from both the soil and plants evaporates in the elevated temperatures inside the terrarium. This water vapour then condenses on the walls of the container, and eventually falls back to the plants and soil below. This contributes to creating an ideal environment for growing plants due to the constant supply of water, thereby preventing the plants from becoming over dry. In addition to this, the light that passes through the transparent material of the terrarium allows for the plants within to photosynthesize, a very important aspect of plant growth.
