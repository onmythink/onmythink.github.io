Percent Volume

The concentration of a substance can be expressed in several quantities such as moles, percent (%) weight/volume,), molarity, molality, parts per million (ppm), or mole fraction. The concentration shows the amount of solute in a unit of the amount of solvent.

Volume of agua oxigenada are in a 400mL bottle of this solution: 12 ml

Further explanation  

There are several concentration units in percentages that can be used.

Percent Volume (% V / V): total volume (ml) of solute in 100 ml of solution.  

Can be formulated:

[tex]\tt \%volume=\dfrac{volume~solute}{volume~solution}\times 100\%[/tex]

A bottle of hydrogen peroxide also known as agua oxigenada is labeled 3%

(v/v) in a 400mL bottle

Required

Volume of agua oxigenada

Solution

3% v/v indicates that there are 3 ml of agua oxigenada  in 100 ml of solution

So for 400 ml of solution, volume of agua oxigenada:

[tex]\tt =\dfrac{3}{100}\times 400\:ml\\\\=\boxed{\bold{12\:ml}}[/tex]

20. Rio and Kim had the same amount of money at the start. When Rio gave Kim 300 pesos to Kim. The ratio of Rio's money to Kim's is 2:3. How much money did they have at the start?


Original amount of money:

Rio: x
Kim: x

New amount of money with the ratio of 2:3.

Rio: x - 300   (gave 300 to Kim)
Kim: x + 300 (add 300 the amount from Rio)

Solution:

(x - 300) : (x + 300) = 2:3

Equate the product of means to the product of extremes:

2 (x+300) = 3 (x - 300)
2x + 600 = 3x - 900
3x - 2x = 600 + 900
x = 1,500

ANSWER: The original amount of money each has is 1,500 pesos.

Check:

Rio's money to Kim's money = 2:3

(1,500-300) / (1,500 + 300) = 2/3
1,200 / 1,800 = 2/3

Divide the numerator 1,200 and denominator 1,800 by their GCF 600 to simplify to the lowest term:

(1,200/600) / (1,800/600) = 2/3
2/3 = 2/3  (True)
