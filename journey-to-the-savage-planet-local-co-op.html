21. read three newspaper or any journal article op-eds (local or international) discussing globalization. Then, write 50-word summaries of each op-ed. Identify the underlying and definitions of globalization the op-ed writers use.​


Globalization

The term "globalization" is used to describe how technology and international trade have boosted connectedness and interdependence. The ensuing societal and economic changes are also covered by the definition of globalization. It may be pictured as the threads of a huge spider web that has expanded in size and reach over a long period of time.

Further explanation

Technology, cross-border trade in goods and services, as well as movements of money, labor, and information have all contributed to the growing interconnectivity of the world's economies, cultures, and people. This phenomenon is referred to as "globalization." Nations have formed economic alliances over many years to support these initiatives.

Concepts, information, know-how, goods, and services traverse national and international borders as a result of globalization. In the business sector, the term is used to describe interconnected economies that promote free commerce, the free flow of money across countries, and easy access to foreign resources, particularly labor markets, in order to increase profits and benefit society as a whole.

Countries focus on the products and services that provide them a competitive advantage in the global market. This frequently refers to what they are able to produce and provide more affordably than other countries while utilizing less resources. In theory, global production should be more effective, prices should be lower, economic progress should be universal, and every nation should prosper if it concentrates on what it does best.

Policies that promote free trade, open borders, and international cooperation are the main drivers of economic globalization. They provide businesses access to more expansive, growing global labor markets, less costly raw material and component markets, and cheaper raw material and component markets.

Globalization has an impact on national governments, international corporations, and other institutions like colleges and universities. Globalization has an influence on a government's ability to implement certain economic policies as well as a university's capability to diversity and grow its student body.

The extent to which international assignments have been put on hold because of the COVID-19 pandemic has been illustrated by the sharp downturn in the activities of global recruitment companies.

With visa application centres closed across the world and with intra-company transfers on hold, major recruitment consultants have been cutting their international staff as companies postpone expansion and hiring plans.

Page Group, which employs about 7,500 staff across 36 countries, has laid off more than 100 workers in Australia, China and Britain, while the Robert Walters group has already furloughed staff in the UK but has so far avoided redundancies across its international workforce of almost 4,000.

“It’s savage for the recruitment market,” founder Robert Walters told City A.M. He said recruitment was still occurring in “pockets” via video interviews, but that most firms had paused hiring because of the pandemic.

But Mr Walters added, “I am confident that we will emerge from this period of uncertainty very well placed to benefit from operational gearing to quickly take advantage of the inevitable market opportunities.”

Impact of COVID-19 will drive increased demand in STEM skills

Mark Dorman, chief executive of SThree, which specialises globally in science, technology, engineering and mathematics (STEM) recruitment, accepted that the coming months "will not be easy" but said that in the longer term, the impact of COVID-19 would drive increased demand for STEM skills.

“However severe the current disruption proves to be, the demand for STEM skills and the trend to flexible working will accelerate as a result of the changes we are seeing in social behaviour, travel and working patterns,” he said.
