Suppose that you were overall in charge of the seating in the ASEAN convention wherein the ASEAN country representatives. They are the prime ministers/presidents of the countries of Brunei, Cambodia, Indonesia, Laos, Malaysia, Myanmar, Philippines, Singapore, Thailand, and Vietnam, and a special guest representative from the USA. 1) If the seating arrangement is to be circular, how many seating arrangements are possible? 2) If the USA representative and the host country's president (say the Philippines) are to be seated next to each other, how many seating arrangements are possible? 3) Create your own seat plan for these 12 leaders based on your knowledge of their backgrounds. Discuss why you arranged them that way.pahelpp​


Answer:

1) If the seating arrangement is to be circular, the number of seating arrangements is (n-1)! where n is the number of people in the group. In this case, there are 10 ASEAN country representatives and 1 special guest representative from the USA, so there are a total of 11 people. Therefore, the number of seating arrangements is (11-1)! = 10! = 3,628,800.

2) If the USA representative and the host country's president are to be seated next to each other, there are two ways they can be seated: the USA representative can be seated to the left of the host country's president, or the USA representative can be seated to the right of the host country's president. Once the USA representative and the host country's president are seated next to each other, there are 9! ways to arrange the other people. Therefore, the number of seating arrangements is 2 x 9! = 725,760.

3) Here is a sample seating plan based on the backgrounds of the leaders:

| Brunei | Laos | Indonesia | USA |

| Thailand | Vietnam| Cambodia | Myanmar|

| Singapore| | Philippines | Malaysia|

In this seating plan, the leaders from Brunei, Laos, Indonesia, and the USA are seated together to represent their shared interests in economic growth and development. Thailand and Vietnam are seated next to each other to represent their strategic alliance in the Mekong subregion. Cambodia and Myanmar are seated next to each other to represent their shared experiences with political instability and conflict. The Philippines, as the host country, is seated in the center of the table, flanked by the USA representative and Malaysia to represent their close diplomatic ties. 28. A concert is held in a hall with a seating capacity of 800 seats. There are three types of seats: S seats, A seats, and B seats. There are 200 more A seats than S seats and 4 times as many B seats as S seats. The price per seat is 80 dollars for S seats, 70 dollars for A seats, and 50 dollars for B seats. If all seats are sold out, the total sales will be ______ dollars.


To properly analyze the problem,

Let x = S seats with 80 dollars per seat
y = A seats with 70 dollars per seat
z = B seats with 50 dollars per seat
T = seating capacity = 800 seats

Condition 1: There are 200 more A seats than S seats

y = x + 200

Condition 2: 4 times as many B seats as S seats

z = 4x

Solving for the number of S seats through substitution,

800 = x + (x+200) + 4x
800-200 = 6x
600 = 6x
x = 100 seats = S seats

Finding for the number of A seats, given Condition 1:

y = 100 + 200
y = 300 seats = A seats

Finding for the number of B seats, given Condition 2:

z = 4(100)
y = 400 seats = B seats

Now that you have all the number of seats for S, A, and B, respectively. You can now solve for the total number of seats sold:

Seats sold in Dollars = 100(80) + 300(70) + 400(50)
Seats sold in Dollars = $49,000

Therefore, the 800 seats were sold for $49,000.
