13. A demolition ball is vertically lifted 4m above the ground. The balls potential energy is 752000 J. what is the mass of the demolition ball?


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The mass of an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{m = \frac{PE}{gh}}[/tex]

where:

m = mass

PE = potential energy

g = acceleration due to gravity = 9.8 m/s²

h = height

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the mass

[tex]m = \dfrac{PE}{gh}[/tex]

[tex]m = \dfrac{\text{752,000 J}}{(\text{9.8 m/}\text{s}^{2})(\text{4 m})}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{m = \text{19,183.67 kg}}[/tex]

Therefore, the mass of the demolition ball is 19,183.67 kg.

25. How much money would you need to deposit today at 9% annual interest compounded monthly to have P12000 in the account after 6 years?


Answer:

An approximate principal amount of Php7,007.00 would yield a maturity or future value of Php12,000 at 9% interest compounded monthly.

Step-by-step explanation:

Problem:

How much money would you need to deposit today at 9% annual interest compounded monthly to have P12000 in the account after 6 years?

Find the maturity value or final amount after 6 years using the formula:

[tex]A = P(1+\frac{r}{n} )^{nt}[/tex]

Where:

A = final amount or maturity value (12,000)r = compound interest rate (9% or .09)n = number of compounding period per year, (monthly = 12)t = time elapsed in years (6 years)P = Principal amount, Unknown

Solution (substitute the given values):

12,000 = P [1 + (0.09/12)⁽¹²⁾⁽⁶⁾

12,000 = P [1 + 0.0075]⁷²

12,000 = P (1.0075)⁷²

12,000 = P (1.7126)

P = 12,000/1.7126

P = 7006.89

or

P ≈ 7007.00

An approximate principal amount of Php7,007.00 would yield a maturity or future value of Php12,000 after 6 years at 9% interest compounded monthly.
