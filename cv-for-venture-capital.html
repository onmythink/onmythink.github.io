20. ENTREPRENEURSHIP Direction: Prepare the Financial Report of your business venture. 1. Income Statement 2. Capital Statement 3. Balance Sheet PLEASE ANSWER


Answer:

Here

Explanation:

1.  Step 1

Income statement is one of the important components of financial statement which is used by the company in order to evaluate its performance over a specific period of time

This statement helps the company in order to get to know information of revenue as well as expenses of the company over a particular period of time

Companies are required to submit its income statement to security and exchange commission (SEC)

There are four main components in the income statement of the company

1) Revenue

2) Expenses

3) Gain

4) Losses

Step 2

Revenue: The income which is derived by the company by conducting the regular, ordinary business is known as operating revenue

While the income which is earned by the company from other sources of activities which is not the part of regular business is known as non-operating revenue

Expenses: This is the cost that s incurred by the company in order to earn the operating profit such as purchase of raw material, machinery etc

Step 3

Gain: These are the incomes which are earned by the company from non-operating business such as sale of plant & machinery or any other long term assets.

Losses: Organisation always follows the concept of conservatism, if there is any chances of loss it shall be recorded in the books.

Structure of income state

3. Balance Sheet is made up of two parts one is assets and second is liabilities and capital. In Assets, liquid assets like cash, inventory are placed before illiquid assets. Same with the liabilities side, liquid liabilities like trade payables are placed before other liabilities.

Main purpose of Balance sheet is:

Management can judge cash flows and liquidity position by balance sheet.

Investors can analyse financial soundness of the business by balance sheet.

Bankers can analyse Balance sheet to decide about how much debt it can provide to the business as per their performance.
