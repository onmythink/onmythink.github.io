23. what figure of speech is used in the line "the best of men is like water"?​


Answer:

Simile

Explanation:

A simile (/ˈsɪməli/) is a figure of speech that directly compares two things. Similes differ from metaphors by highlighting the similarities between two things using words such as "like", "as", or "than", while metaphors create an implicit comparison (i.e. saying something "is" something else).

27. 10. Which of the fallowing lines is an example of assonance? A. "Each beach beast thinks he's the best beast" B. "Sally sells seashells by the seashore" C. "warm, beating, frantic, winged; music and blood" D. "On the fifteenth of May, in the jungle of Nool" More in photos


Answer:

A. "Each beach beast thinks he's the best beast"

Each beach beast thinks he's the best beast. Notice the assonance taking place when two or more words that are close to one another repeat the same vowel sound but start with different consonant sounds, i.e. reach, east, beast, beach, west, each, and best.
