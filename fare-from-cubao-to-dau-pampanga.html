2. The jeepney fare for the first 4 kilometer is P9.00 and an additional P1.00 for every kilometer. Euri will go to Cubao from Antipolo and the distance is about 28 km. How much does he need to pay What is the correct expression to find the total amount of fare? ​


Answer:

The jeepney fare for the first 4 kilometer is P9.00 and an additional P1.00 for every kilometer. Euri will go to Cubao from Antipolo and the distance is about 28 km. How much does he need to pay What is the correct expression to find the total amount of fare? 

Solution:

In 4 kilometers is P9.00 after that 5 to 28km will add additional P1.00

4 (first four km) × 9 (Php of jeepney fare)

= 36 (Php for only 4km)

28 (km distance between Cubao from Antipolo) - 4 (first 4km with only P9.00)

= 24 (km that is left)

24 (km remaining) × 10 (P10.00 for every km)

= 240 (Php)

36 + 240 = 276 (the total amount Euri need to pay)

3. tree diagram there are 6 roads leading from antipolo to cubao and 3 roads leading from cubao to batangas. How many possible routes from antipolo to batangas through cubao


Answer:

18 possible routes

Step-by-step explanation:

In this problem, there are various of ways to solve this. One would be this.

First we label the places as

A = Antipolo

B = Batangas

C= Cubao

When there are x ways to do something and y ways to do another thing dependent to the first event, then we simply multiply these two values. That is xy = (6)(3)=18. Therefore there are 18 possible routes.

ALTERNATIVE EXPLANATION:

From A to C, we have 6 choices.

From C to B, we have 3 choices.

Now we have to find the number of possible routes from A to B but of course passing through C.

x = {1,2,3,4,5,6 }= routes from A to C

y={a,b,c}= routes from C to B

We start from A, we can take route 1 out of 6, then when we reach C, we can take route a. That is one possible route, namely route 1a.

Now for everytime we use the route 1, there are 3 possible routes to pair up with route 1, that is, route a, b, or c. So we have routes 1a, 1b, 1c.

It means that for every route from A to C, there are 3 possible routes. There are six routes, which makes it 6 x 3 = 18.

Listing all the elements and getting the cartesian product:

x·y= {1,2,3,4,5,6} · {a,b,c}

x·y= {1a,1b,1c,2a,2b,2c,3a,3b,3c,4a,4b,4c,5a,5b,5c,6a,6b,6c} = 18 routes.

Christmas time in San Fernando, Pampanga is celebrated with the famous giant lantern festival, a parade of up to 10 giant parols or Christmas lanterns that has put this province north of Manila, firmly on the Philippine Christmas map. San Fernando is home to the country’s best parol makers. Parol making as a craft is taken so seriously here that every year some 10 barangays would parade like a badge of honor about 10 giant lanterns – some standing as high as a two-storey building – each lit by anywhere from 3,000 to 5,000 light bulbs, blinking in time to music.
