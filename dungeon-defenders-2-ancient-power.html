8. Ancient Modes of Transportation for, Manpower, Animal Power and Wind Power and discuss each.​


Ancient Modes of Transportation

People walked, rode animals, and subsequently invented wheeled vehicles to get around in ancient times. For transportation, they relied on existing streams and dirt roads. People developed more sophisticated modes of transportation over time.

Ancient Modes of Transportation for Animal Power

Human employment of non-human working animals (also known as "beasts of burden") for the movement of people and commodities is referred to as "animal-powered transport." Humans can ride on the backs of some of the larger of these creatures, employ them as pack animals to transport goods, or harness them individually or in groups to pull (or haul) sleds or wheeled vehicles.

Ancient Modes of Transportation for Wind Power

Wind energy has been utilized to propel ships, pump water, and grind grain for ages. Persia employed simple windmills with sails for rotors to grind maize and irrigate crops as early as the 1st century AD. Windmills were present throughout Asia at the same time. As early as 5,000 BC, people used wind energy to propel boats along the Nile River. Simple wind-powered water pumps were employed in China by 200 BC, and windmills with woven-reed blades were utilized in Persia and the Middle East to grind grain. Wind energy was finally used in new ways all around the world.

12. Explain why tyrants came into power in ancient Greece AND how tyrants’ rule led to the development of Oligarchy and Democracy governments in ancient Greece.


Answer:

The emergence of tyrants in ancient Greece was a direct result of the breakdown of traditional systems of governance previously known as the ‘arete system’ in which aristocrats, who had proven their excellence, were granted a certain amount of authority in their respective cities. In times of crisis or social unrest, citizens would select one person who they entrusted with increased powers, creating a tyrannical government. As the power wielded by the tyrant increased, they often abused their authority to the point where they had almost absolute control.

This tyranny eventually led to the development of other forms of government in ancient Greece, namely oligarchy and democracy. Oligarchies existed when the power was produced and maintained by only a few individuals in society. Democracy, on the other hand, was based on the principle of ‘equality’ where all citizens had an equal say in the governing of the state and discussed matters in the Assembly (an open-air forum). The tyranny of the few led to the citizens of Greece to seek out alternative forms of government in order to establish a more egalitarian and fair system. Thus, it can be said that the emergence of tyrants in Greece ultimately led to the development of oligarchy and democracy governments in ancient Greece.

Wind-powered machines used to grind grain and pump water, the windmill and wind pump, were developed in what are now Iran, Afghanistan and Pakistan by the 9th century.[8][9] The first practical windmills were in use in Sistan, a region in Iran and bordering Afghanistan, at least by the 9th century and possibly as early as the mid-to-late 7th century. These Panemone windmills were horizontal windmills,[note 1] which had long vertical driveshafts with six to twelve rectangular sails covered in reed matting or cloth.[10] These windmills were used to pump water, and in the gristmilling and sugarcane industries.[11] The use of windmills became widespread across the Middle East and Central Asia, and later spread to China and India.[12] Vertical windmills were later used extensively in Northwestern Europe to grind flour beginning in the 1180s, and many examples still exist.[13] By 1000 AD, windmills were used to pump seawater for salt-making in China and Sicily.[14]

29. identify the type of reasoning of the following items : write Deductive ot inductive as your answer and defend it by justifying. 1 knowledge is power 2. researchers doing an experiment.​


Answer:

1. Inductive reasoning

2. Deductive reasoning

Explanation:

1. “Knowledge is power" is an example of inductive reasoning. This statement is a generalization that is based on specific observations and experiences. It implies that having knowledge leads to power, but it doesn't provide a logical proof or deductive argument for this claim. Instead, this statement is based on the observation that people who have knowledge tend to have more opportunities and advantages in life, which leads to the conclusion that knowledge is a form of power.

2. "Researchers doing an experiment" is an example of deductive reasoning. Deductive reasoning is a type of logical reasoning that starts with a general principle or premise and uses it to draw a specific conclusion. In this case, the general principle is that researchers conduct experiments to test hypotheses and gather data. Therefore, it is logical to conclude that if someone is conducting an experiment, they must be a researcher. This conclusion follows logically from the general principle, and is therefore an example of deductive reasoning.
