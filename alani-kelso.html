2. Read the song by alanis morissette ironic and identify at least 10 samples of their classification as to verbal irony, situation irony or dramatic irony​


Answer:

Below are sample lyrics from Morissette’s song that you can use to assess students’

understanding of irony.

Line from the song Is it

ironic?

If Yes – Which type and why?

If No – Why not?

“An old man turned ninety

eight/He won the lottery

and died the next day…”

No It is purely coincidence that the old man

dies the next day. While many might think

this is irony, one would expect a ninetyeight year old man to die simply because

of his old age.

“It’s like rain on your

wedding day…”

No Again, it is coincidence that it rains on

someone’s wedding day unless the

forecast calls for a sunny day and it then

begins to rain.

“It’s meeting the man of

my dreams/And then

meeting his beautiful

wife…”

Yes Situational Irony – Someone would not

expect to meet the man of their dreams

only to find out that the man is married.

“It’s a death row pardon

two minutes late…”

Yes Situational Irony – Someone would not

expect for someone who is on death row,

and has most likely undergone numerous

appeals, to be pardoned for their actions.

“A traffic jam when you’re

already late…”

No When someone is late to work, they may

be more likely to run into traffic. The fact

that they are late and run into a traffic jam

is simply coincidence.

“A no-smoking sign on

your cigarette break…”

No Unless the employee had no knowledge of

the sign, this is not ironic. It is, again, a

coincidence. However, if the employee did

not see the sign and we did, it would be

dramatic irony. If the employee had always

smoked and then saw that the sign had

just been hung, it would be situational

irony, as the expectation is based on the

fact that they have always been able to

smoke, but are now no longer permitted to

do so.

The song title, “Ironic.” Yes Situational Irony – Because the song is

titled “Ironic,” it is expected that the song

would include actual examples of irony

when, for the most part, it merely

discusses unfortunate coincidences.

8. Alanie noticed that there are pieces of small stones in her rice grains. What techniques will she use to separate small stones from rice grains and why?


I think the recommended method to separate or remove small stones from rice grains is hand-picking, although it requires a lot of time when using that method, it is still recommended. In using the hand-picking method, we spread first the rice grains containing the small stones on a flat container, then we pick and remove or throw away the small stones by hand.

Another thing is that when we use other methods like winnowing, this method is only used to separate husk from various types of grains like wheat and rice. We cannot separate small stone particles from wheat by the process of winnowing. This is because stone particles are quite heavy which cannot be carried away by wind to a greater distance.
