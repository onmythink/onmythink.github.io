6. Ways of protecting and caring a dragonfly


Answer:

Answer:

A pond that is too shallow can overheat or dry up, and the deep areas provide refuge from predators like our dragonflies. Put plants in and around the pond. Plants, both submerged and floating, add oxygen to the water and remove carbon dioxide.

What do dragonflies need to survive?

Dragonflies in their aquatic stage eat many kinds of small animals: aquatic insects, tadpoles, small fish, and other invertebrates. Adult dragonflies eat flying insects, especially mosquitoes and other true flies, but also aphids, smaller dragonflies, damselflies, and just about any other insects they can grab.

How to take care of dragonflies:

1.Dragonflies aren’t readily available for sale, so many people who raise them acquire them while helping clean pools and ponds. Thus, first, find a likely place where there may be nymphs, such as an unused pool, still pond, muddy rice field, or stream. The nymphs are easiest to find from May through July. You can use a net to catch the nymph.

2. See the websites below for pictures, but once you’ve found one, put it in a container (a bucket is fine) and carry it quietly home. Since the nymphs get “seasickness” if they are carried around in just water, put wet grass, preferably a water plant/grass, in with the water.

3. When you get home, be sure to place the dragonfly nymph in a prepared aquarium within 24 hours. The case should have 4-5 centimeters (1.6 inches to 2 inches) of rocks in the bottom. Also, make sure there is grass/water-plants and a stick that comes out of the water in the aquarium.

4. If the aquarium is fairly small, install an air filtration system. This isn’t necessary if the aquarium is big, but it’s still a good idea.

5. Since the nymphs typically live in dirty water, don’t worry too much about changing water. It may be a good idea to change it once every two weeks or so, though. This is especially true if you are keeping the aquarium in the house. The water will probably begin to stink, particularly if there’s any left over food.

6. Dragonfly nymphs don’t like the heat. Thus, make sure the water they are in doesn’t go above 25 degrees Celsius (77 degrees Fahrenheit).

7. Feed the nymphs a little bit each day. Living food is highly recommended (and probably necessary). Use a net to take out uneaten food. Be sure to feed them not too much (the water will get dirty), but also not too little (the nymphs will die, or, if there is more than one nymph in the aquarium, they will eat each other!)

8. If the dragonfly nymph isn’t eating much, that probably means that they are almost ready to become a dragonfly. Make sure you’ve placed a stick in the aquarium which sticks out about 10 cm (3.9 inches) from the surface of the water. The nymph will climb up this stick to emerge as a dragonfly.

9. Once the dragonfly has emerged as an adult and its wings are dry, be sure to release it outside. Since dragonflies prefer to fly around and catch their own food, it isn’t practical to keep an adult as a pet.

10. Enjoy having observed this fascinating insect grow up and congratulate yourself!

7. A spider has 8 legs. A dragonfly has 6 legs. 28 spuders and dragonflies have 200 legs altogether. How many spiders and how many dragonflies are there?


Answer:

There are 16 spiders and 12 dragonflies.

Step-by-step explanation:

Word Problem

Let us analyze the given problem above.

What is asked?

The number of spiders and dragonflies.

What are the given?

number of spider's legs - 8 legs

number of dragonfly's legs - 6 legs

total number of spiders and dragonflies - 28

total number of legs - 200

Equations:

Let x be the number of spiders.

Let y be the number of dragonflies.

x + y = 288x + 6y = 200

Solution:

Number of spiders:

Multiply 6 to the first equation to eliminate y. Then subtract it from the second equation.

8x + 6y = 200

- 6x - 6y = 168

2x = 32

2x = 32

2x/2 = 32/2

x = 16

There are 16 spiders.

Number of dragonflies:

x + y = 28

16 + y = 28

y = 28 - 16

y = 12

There are 12 dragonflies.

Checking:

Substitute the values.

16 + 12 = 28

28 = 28 ✔

8(16) + 6(12) = 200

128 + 72 = 200

200 = 200 ✔

Hummingbirds are birds native to the Americas and comprise the biological family Trochilidae.
