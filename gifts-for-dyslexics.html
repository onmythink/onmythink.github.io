9. can y'all help me like for real. how do you solve frequency? ( I'm dyslexic and i have a hard time to understand science lately) ​


Of course, I'd be happy to help you with that. In science, frequency usually refers to the number of occurrences of a particular event or phenomenon within a set period of time.

To calculate frequency, you need to divide the total number of occurrences of the event or phenomenon by the amount of time the observations were made. For example, if you observe a particular bird species flying overhead 20 times in 1 hour, the frequency of the bird species would be 20/1 = 20 times per hour.

Here's the formula to calculate frequency:

Frequency = Number of occurrences / Time

Just remember to make sure that the units of measurement for both the number of occurrences and time match.
