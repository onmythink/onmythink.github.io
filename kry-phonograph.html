4. A phonograph record rotates at 45 revolutions /minute. What is its angular speed in rad/s? Through many degrees does it rotate in 0.2 s?


Angular Velocity

Find the phonograph's angular speed

Given that a phonograph turntable rotates at 45 revolutions per minute.

We must determine the phonograph's angular speed in rad/s.

We are aware of this, 1revolution means 2π radian and 1 minute is equal to 60 seconds.

Solution:

[tex]45 rev/min = \frac{40 * 6.28}{60}=1.5 \pi /s[/tex]

Degrees rotate in 0.2 s

Solution:

[tex]1.5\pi /rad * 2s=3\pi rad[/tex]

Rotation degree = [tex]3 * \pi 180/\pi=54^{o}[/tex]

The pace at which an object rotates or revolves around an axis is known as its angular velocity. The Greek letter omega (occasionally ) stands for angular velocity. The SI unit for angular velocity is radians per second because it is expressed as an angle per unit of time. Every point on an object that is revolving about an axis has the same angular velocity. The tangential velocity of points distant from the axis of rotation is, nevertheless, different from that of points closer to the axis of rotation.

19. A phonograph needle whose point is 0.1 mm in radius exerts a downward force of 0.02 N. What is the pressure on the record groove? How many atmospheres is this?​


Answer:

378 atmospheres

Explanation:

Solution :

Here, forceo n the phonogram needle, `F=1.2N`

radius of the circular needle, `r=0.1mm=0.1xx10^-3`m

area of cross-section of the needle, `A=pir^2`

`=3.142xx(0.1xx10^-3)^2=3.142xx10^-8m^2`

pressure exerted on the record in Pa. `P=(P)/(A)`

`=((1.2N))/((3.142xx10^-8m^2))=382xx10^5(N)/(m^)`

pressure exerted on the record in atm

`(382xx10^5(N)/(m^2))/((1.013xx10^5(N)/(m^2))/(atm))`

`=378atm`

26. How does the phonograph works


How does a phonograph work?

Sound is collected by a horn that is attached to a diaphragm. The sound causes vibrations in the air that progress down the horn causing the diaphragm to vibrate.
