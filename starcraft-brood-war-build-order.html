Explanation:

BROODING

DAY 2

TYPES OF BROODING

Brooding can be classified into natural and artificial brooding.

Natural brooding It is done with the help of broody hens after hatching, up to 3 to 4 weeks of age.

Artificial brooding In artificial brooding large number of baby chicks are reared in the absence of broody hen. Equipment used for brooding are called brooders. Brooder comprises of three elements: Heating source,

Reflectors and Brooder guard

Heating source may be electrical, gases like natural gas, solid fuel like coal etc.

Proper temperature/heat must be maintained if the chicks are to thrive. During the first few weeks, chicks are cold blooded; that is, unable to maintain their body temperature. As they age, they become warm blooded; that is, able to maintain constant body temperature under normal environmental conditions.

The temperature of 30-35 degrees Celsius is ideal in the first 3 days of poultry days of life and thereafter temperature should be reduce by 2-3 degrees Celsius per day until room temperature of 21 is reached.

Place room thermometers around the brooding ring at chick height level for accurate measurement of brooding ring temperature.

SOURCES OF HEAT

1) Charcoal stove

Where electricity is not available, ordinary charcoal stoves are used to provide supplementary heat to chicks. These stoves are covered with plate / pans to dissipate the heat.

2) Gas brooder:

Natural gas is connected to heating element which is hanged 3 to 5 feet above the chick to provide heat.

3) Electrical brooder:

It is also thermostatically controlled heating system that spread required amount of heat uniformly above large area, this avoid crowding of chicks under brooder directly.

4) Infra-red bulbs:

It is a self-reflecting bulb. One 250 watts bulb can provide brooding for about 150 to 250 chicks.

5) Reflectors:

These reflectors are called Hovers. Flat type hover These hovers are provided with heating element, heating mechanism and pilot lamp and in some cases, thermometer is also there in order to record the temperature.
