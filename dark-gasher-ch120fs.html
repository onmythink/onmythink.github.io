MoodIn literature, mood is a literary device that reveals to the reader the overall feeling or attitude of a story. Mood is changeable; it does not have to be the same throughout the entire plot. Mood is communicated through the descriptions of places, characters, and situations in a story and how those elements connect to the plot. For example, a detective story might have a mood of mystery and foreboding, while a romance might have a mood of whimsy and excitement.

16. Which of these can prove Assertions?A) truth or dareB) Light or dark C) Black or white statementsD) True or False ☺️​.


Answer:

D) True or False statements can be used to prove assertions.

Explanation:

An assertion is a statement that is made as a fact or claim, without providing any evidence or proof. In order to prove an assertion, it is necessary to provide evidence or support for the claim being made.

True or false statements can be used as a type of evidence or proof to support or refute an assertion. For example, if someone makes the assertion that the Earth is flat, a true statement that supports this assertion might be "There is no evidence that the Earth is not flat." A false statement that refutes this assertion might be "The Earth is clearly round, as demonstrated by the fact that it casts a circular shadow on the Moon during a lunar eclipse."

Other types of statements, such as light or dark, black or white, or truth or dare, do not provide evidence or proof and cannot be used to prove or refute an assertion.
