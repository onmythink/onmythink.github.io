[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{PROBLEM}:} [/tex]

The ratio of puppies to kittens in a pet shop is 8:3. If there are 18 kittens, how many are puppies?

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER}:} [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \LARGE \:\: \rm{48 \: puppies} [/tex]

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:} [/tex]

» Let x be the number of puppies. Determine the proportion between the two ratios.

[tex] 8\::\: 3 = x \::\: 18 [/tex]

» Multiply the means and the extremes.

[tex] (3)(x) = (8)(18) [/tex]

[tex] 3x = 144 [/tex]

[tex] \frac{\,3x\,}{3} = \frac{\,144\,}{3} \\ [/tex]

[tex] x = 48 [/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] There are 48 puppies in the pet store.

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

21. . Imelda has 15 pet dogs. There were 9 dalmatians and 11 were puppies. How many of her pet dogs are dalmatian puppies?​


Answer:

5 Dalmatian puppies

Step-by-step explanation:

Let x be the number of Dalmatian puppies.

(9-x) is the number of Dalmatians that aren't puppies.

(11-x) is the number of puppies that aren't Dalmatians.

15 is the total number of her pet dogs.

x + (9-x) + (11-x) = 15

x + 9 - x + 11 - x = 15 (Remove the parentheses.)

x - x - x + 9 + 11 = 15

-x + 20 = 15 (Combine like terms.)

-x = 15 - 20

-x = -5

x = 5 (Change both signs so x will be positive.)
