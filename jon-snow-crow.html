Linear motion consists of 2: constant velocity motion with constant velocity and uniformly accelerated motion with constant acceleration

the speed of vo = v = constant  

acceleration = a = 0  

Δx = vt or x = xo + vt  

An equation of constant velocity motion  

[tex]\tt d=v.t[/tex]  

d = displacement

an average speed = total distance traveled divided by the elapsed time

[tex]\tt v_{avg}=\dfrac{total~distance}{total~elapsed~time}[/tex]

Known

v= 2.22 m/s

t = 1.5 h = 1.5 x 60 s = 90 s

Average speed :

[tex]\tt total~distance(d)=v_[avg)\times t\\\\d=2.22\times 90\\\\d=\boxed{\bold{199.8~m}}[/tex]

[ Answer ]

Answer: x²

Base on the problem, Nathan is 'twice' as Jon's old. So that means, we need to apply multiplication. But we can't just do "x×2". We need to do it in exponent form. So the answer is x² or we can read it as "x is raise to the power of two". In short x² is another way of multiplying x and 2.
