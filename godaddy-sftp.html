21. If 2.00 mol of gas occupies 4.50 l at stp, how much of the same gas will occupy 3.0 l at stp


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & V_1 = \text{4.50 L} \\ & n_1 = \text{2.00 mol} \\ & V_2 = \text{3.00 L} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final amount of gas by using Avogadro's law.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n_2 & = \frac{n_1V_2}{V_1} \\ & = \frac{(\text{2.00 mol})(\text{3.00 L})}{\text{4.50 L}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{1.33 mol}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, 1.33 mol of the same gas will occupy 3.0 L at STP.
