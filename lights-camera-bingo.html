1. what part of a camera controls the amount of light that enters the camera?​


Answer:

aperture

The aperture, sometimes called the diaphragm or iris, is the opening through which light enters the camera. Typically located in the lens, this opening can be widened or narrowed to control the amount of light that strikes the film.

Answer:

B.

Explanation:

The flash is a device that emits light momentarily. You can use the flash's light to compensate for the lack of brightness when shooting in dimly lit situations like indoors or night scenes. Thanks to the instant exposure, using the flash can also be effective to prevent camera shake and subject blur.
