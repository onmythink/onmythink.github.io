11. There are 120 animals in Mr. Alfonso’s farm consisting goats, cows, and carabaos in the ratio of 5:3:2, respectively. How many more goats are there than carabaos?


Question:

There are 120 animals in Mr. Alfonso’s farm consisting goats, cows, and carabaos in the ratio of 5:3:2, respectively. How many more goats are there than carabaos?

Given:120 animals 5:3:2 the ratio of goats, cows, and carabaos

Operation:AdditionDivision Multiplication Subtraction

Solution:

→ Find the value of x.

[tex]\tt \large 5x+ 3x + 2x = 120[/tex]

[tex]\tt \large 10x = 120[/tex]

[tex]\tt \large \frac{\cancel{10} x}{\cancel{10}} = \frac{120}{10}[/tex]

[tex]\tt \Large \color{lime}{x = 12} [/tex]

→ Find the number of goats.

[tex]\tt \large = 5x[/tex]

[tex]\tt \large = 5(12)[/tex]

[tex]\tt \Large = \color{lime}{60} [/tex]

→ Find the number of carabaos.

[tex]\tt \large = 2x[/tex]

[tex]\tt \large = 2(12)[/tex]

[tex]\tt \Large = \color{lime}{24} [/tex]

→ Find the difference.

[tex]\tt \large = 60 - 24[/tex]

[tex]\tt \Huge = \: \: \color{lime}{36} [/tex]

Final Answer:

[tex]\Large \sf The \: number \: of \: goats \: is \: \color{lime}{36} \: \color{black}{more \: than} \\ \Large \sf \: the \: number \: of \: carabaos.[/tex]
