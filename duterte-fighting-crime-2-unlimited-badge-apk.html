17. describe the role of the U.N, interpol, and the aseanapol in the member countries fight against crimes​


Answer:

The United Nations (UN) plays a role in providing a global platform for member countries to come together and discuss issues related to crime and law enforcement. It also provides assistance in terms of technical expertise and resources to help member countries fight crimes such as drug trafficking, terrorism, and organized crime.

Interpol, on the other hand, is an international organization that helps member countries with law enforcement cooperation and coordination. It provides a global database and communication network that allows member countries to share information and intelligence on criminals and criminal activities.

The ASEANAPOL, which stands for the ASEAN Police Cooperation, is a regional organization that aims to promote cooperation and coordination among the police forces of ASEAN member countries. It focuses on issues such as transnational crime, terrorism, and drugs, and provides a platform for sharing information and resources to help member countries fight these crimes.

Explanation:

Answer:

The United Nations (UN) is an intergovernmental organization that aims to promote international cooperation and maintain international peace and security. One of the ways it does this is by working with member countries to combat crimes that affect people around the world.

INTERPOL, on the other hand, is an international organization that facilitates police cooperation between member countries. It helps member countries share information and resources to fight crimes such as terrorism, cybercrime, and organized crime.

The ASEANAPOL is a regional organization that brings together law enforcement agencies from the Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN) member countries. Its main purpose is to promote cooperation among these agencies in order to combat transnational crimes and maintain regional security.

In short, the UN, INTERPOL, and the ASEANAPOL all play important roles in helping member countries fight against crimes, though their specific roles and functions differ.
