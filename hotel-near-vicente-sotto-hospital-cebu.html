14. What did the bloggers and a group of professors file against Senate Majority Floor Leader Vicente Sotto III?​


Explanation:

Thirty-seven complainants, including bloggers, university professors, and members of a "free-thinking" group, on Tuesday filed an ethics complaint against Senate Majority Floor Leader Vicente Sotto III for alleged plagiarism in his speeches. In a 16-page complaint, the complainants asked the Senate committee on ethics and privileges to impose the appropriate disciplinary action on Sotto for allegedly violating section 193 of Republic Act 8293 or the Intellectual Property Code of the Philippines by lifting parts of his speech from different authors without proper attribution. "Ang aming hinihiling ngayon sa Senado ay disiplinahin ninyo ang inyong kasapi sapagkat klarong siyam na beses siyang lumabag sa prinsipyo ng pagbabawal ng pangongopya," said Dr. Antonio Contreras, former dean of De La Salle University's College of Liberal Arts, after filing the complaint. Section 193 says the author of a work shall have the right "to require that the authorship of the works be attributed to him, in particular, the right that his name, as far as practicable, be indicated in a prominent way on the copies, and in connection with the public use of his work."

8. Ang pahayagang El Pueblo ay itinatag ni _____.

A. Antonio Luna

B. Pedro Paterno

C. Sergio Osmeña

D. Vicente Sotto

Explanation:

Antonio Narciso Luna de San Pedro y Novicio Ancheta (Spanish: [anˈtonjo ˈluna]; 29 October 1866 – 5 June 1899) was a Filipino army general who fought in the Philippine–American War before his assassination in 1899.

Regarded as one of the fiercest generals of his time, he succeeded Artemio Ricarte as the Commanding General of the Philippine Army. He sought to apply his background in military science to the fledgling army. A sharpshooter himself, he organized professional guerrilla soldiers later named the "Luna Sharpshooters" and the "Black Guard" with Senyor Michael Joaquin. His three-tier defense, now known as the Luna Defense Line, gave the American troops a difficult endeavor during their campaign in the provinces north of Manila. This defense line culminated in the creation of a military stronghold in the Cordillera.[1]

Despite his commitment to discipline the army and serve the Republic which attracted the admiration of people, his temper and fiery outlashes caused some to abhor him, including people from Aguinaldo's Cabinet.[2] Nevertheless, Luna's efforts were not without recognition during his time, for he was awarded the Philippine Republic Medal in 1899. He was also a member of the Malolos Congress.[3] Besides his military studies, Luna also studied pharmacology, literature and chemistry.[4]

26. Why should we vote for Vicente Tito Sotto? List his contribution


Vicente Tito Sotto is currently serving as a Senator of the Philippines, having been elected in 1992, 1998, 2010, 2016, and 2019. He has also served as the Senate President of the Philippines from 2018 to 2020.

Throughout his career as a public official, Sotto has authored and sponsored various bills and laws that aim to address issues and concerns of the Filipinos. Some of his notable contributions include:Republic Act No. 10627 or the "Anti-Bullying Act of 2013" - This law aims to prevent and address bullying in schools by imposing penalties to individuals who commit such acts.Republic Act No. 11166 or the "Philippine HIV and AIDS Policy Act" - This law seeks to improve the quality of life of individuals living with HIV/AIDS in the Philippines by providing access to prevention, treatment, care, and support services.Republic Act No. 11313 or the "Safe Spaces Act" - This law aims to protect individuals, especially women and children, from sexual harassment and other forms of gender-based violence in public places and online platforms.Republic Act No. 11210 or the "Expanded Maternity Leave Act" - This law provides working mothers with 105 days of paid maternity leave and an option for an additional 30 days without pay.Republic Act No. 10754 or the "An Act Expanding the Benefits and Privileges of Persons with Disability" - This law provides additional benefits and privileges to persons with disabilities, such as discounts on medical and dental services, transportation, and basic necessities.

These are just some of the contributions of Senator Vicente Tito Sotto to the government and society as a public official. Ultimately, it is up to the voters to evaluate the track record and platforms of each candidate and make an informed decision during elections.
