17. Bao can eat 12 chicken wings in 3 minutes. She eats the chicken wings at a constant rate how many chicken wings can Bao eat in 12 minutes?


Answer:

Bao can eat 48 chicken wings in 12 minutes.

Step-by-step explanation:

Ratio and Proportion

Directly Proportional Word Problem

The given problem above is an example of directly proportional word problem. Meaning, the items in the question are proportional to each other in a way that when one item increases, the other item increases also; when one item decreases, the other item decreases also.

Let us analyze the problem

What is asked?

The number of chicken wings that Bao can eat in 12 minutes.

What are the given?

12 chicken wings in 3 minutes

12 minutes

What is the equation?

Let us identify the knowns and unknowns and represent them as ratio in fraction form.

Based on the given data, we have two items in the problem, the number of chicken wings and the minutes. We can represent our ratio as chicken wings to minutes.

Let x be the missing number.

12 chicken wings in 3 minutes = ‌12⁄3‌

how many chicken wings in 12 minutes = x/12

The equation is the one below since they are directly proportional.

12⁄3‌ = x/12

Solution:

To find the missing number in a proportion, we need to cross multiply.

12⁄3‌ = x/12

3 • x = 12 • 12

3x = 144

3x/3 = 144/3

x = 48

(This symbol • is the same as the multiplication sign.)

Final Answer:

Bao can eat 48 chicken wings in 12 minutes.

Checking:

Let us check if the answer is correct. Cross multiply.

‌12⁄3‌ = ‌48⁄12‌

3 • 48 = 12 • 12

144 = 144 ✔

Determine the weights and arms of all mass within the aircraft.

Multiply weights by arms for all mass to calculate moments.

Add the moments of all mass together.

Two types of bones make up the bird skeletal system:

Pneumatic: These bones (say it: new-matic) are hollow and connected to the respiratory system via the air sacs. Examples of pneumatic bones are the skull, collar bone (clavicle), pelvis, and bones of the lower back.

Medullary: These bones, including the leg bones, ribs, and shoulder blades, serve as a source of stored calcium for the hen to tap into to make strong egg shells. Bone marrow filling the centers of the medullary bones makes red and white blood cells.

26. In fruit flies, the allele for vestigial wings is recessive to the allele for normal wings. In a generation of fruit flies, 18 males and 15 females with vestigial wings are produced, and 43 males and 44 females with normal wings are produced. Which of the following is the best conclusion you can make about the parents of this generation?


Answer:

Both parents phenotypically have normal wings. Both also have the genotype Ww .

Explanation:

Let us say that the two types of alleles that can make up the gene locus for wing type of fruit flies are represented by a capital W (normal wings for the dominant allele) and a lowercase w (vestigial wings for the recessive allele).

To have an idea about the genotype of the parents, we can get clues by knowing the phenotypic ratio within the generation. Although the question gives us the number of males and females with normal and vestigial wings, the trait does not seem to be sex-linked because the occurrences of normal and vestigial wings between male and female are not quite far off.

Frequency of fruit flies with vestigial wings = (males with vestigial wings + females with vestigial wings) / total population = (18 + 15) / (18 + 15 + 43 + 44) = 33 / 120 or approximately 25% of the generation.

Frequency of fruit flies with normal wings = (males with normal wings + females with normal wings) / total population = (43 + 44) / (18 + 15 + 43 + 44) = 87 / 120 or approximately 75% of the generation.

We therefore conclude that 25% of the generation has vestigial wings with genotype ww, and 75% have normal wings with genotype WW or Ww. The phenotypic ratio of fruit flies with vestigial wings to fruit flies with normal wings is 25:75 or 1:3.

If each of the two parents have 2 alleles for wing type, this means that during reproduction 4 alleles in total can do recombination. What combination of alleles will produce 25% ww and 75% WW or Ww? The only way to get 25% ww is if both parents have the recessive allele. Thus, both parents phenotypically have normal wings, but both also have the genotype Ww .

Checking our answer: Ww x Ww = 25% WW, 50% Ww, and 25% ww. Our genotypic ratio is 1:2:1, and our phenotypic ratio is 1:3
