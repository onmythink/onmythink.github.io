2. Which of following is the purest form of love?A. AgapeB. ErosC. PragmaD. Storge​


Answer:

A. AGAPE

Explanation:

Agape, Greek agapē, in the New Testament, the fatherly love of God for humans, as well as the human reciprocal love for God. In Scripture, the transcendent agape love is the highest form of love and is contrasted with eros, or erotic love, and philia, or brotherly love.

Eros (/ˈɪərɒs/ or /ˈɛrɒs/; Ancient Greek: ἔρως érōs "love" or "desire") is a concept in ancient Greek philosophy referring to sensual or passionate love, from which the term erotic is derived. Eros has also been used in philosophy and psychology in a much wider sense, almost as an equivalent to "life energy".

Pragma is a kind of practical love founded on reason or duty and one's longer-term interests. Sexual attraction takes a back seat in favour of personal qualities and compatibilities, shared goals, and "making it work". In the days of arranged marriages, pragma must have been very common.

Storge (/ˈstɔːrɡi/, from the Ancient Greek word στοργή storgē) or familial love refers to natural or instinctual affection, such as the love of a parent towards offspring and vice versa. In social psychology, another term for love between good friends is philia.

21. Why do professional historians once treated diplomatic sources as the purest, the "best" source of history?​


Because, whatever a country’s official “public” line is regarding another country (nations often pretend to be friends), diplomats’ internal papers and correspondence are supposed to be a reflection of reality. They’re transmitted via “diplomatic pouch,” which means they’re not scrutinized or seized when entering or leaving a country. On occasion, however, diplomatic communications have been sent publicly, and they have been intercepted. In 1900, just prior to the Boxer Insurrection, the U.S. Legation in Beijing sent a telegram to the U.S. Department of State referring to the Chinese government as “two-faced.” To let the Americans know they had noticed this, the Zongli Yamen, China’s foreign ministry, sent a note to the American minister informing him that the Imperial Telegraph would be charging “two-faced” as two words, rather than one.

Then, of course, there was the famous Zimmerman Telegram, which laid bare Germany’s intentions with regard to submarine warfare during the First World War, and which encouraged Mexico to attack the United States in order to recover some of its lost territories. The telegram was intercepted and decoded by British Intelligence.
