28. The temperature inside a sauna is 85.0°C. If 235.0g of solft drink straight from refrigerator (assumed to be at a temperature of 50.0°C) is brought into the sauna, how much heat will be absorbed by the soft drink? Assume that the specific heat of the soft drink is the same as that of water, 4.19kJ/kg°C.


The heat absorbed by soft drink = 34462.75 J

Further explanation  

The law of conservation of energy can be applied to heat changes, i.e. the heat received/absorbed is the same as the heat released  

Q abs= Q released  

Heat can be calculated using the formula:  

Q = mc∆T  

Q = heat, J  

m = mass, g  

c = specific heat, joules / g ° C  

∆T = temperature difference, ° C / K  

mass of soft drink = 235 g

c = 4.19 J/g ° C  

∆T = 85 - 50 = 35 ° C  

Heat absorbed :

[tex]\tt Q=m.c.\Delta t\\\\Q=235\times 4.19\times 35\\\\Q=\boxed{\bold{34462.75~J}}[/tex]
