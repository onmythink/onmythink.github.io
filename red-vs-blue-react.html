6. what happen to the red litmus paper once it reacts with acid?


Answer:

red litmus paper remains red when in contact with acidic or neutral solutions.

Explanation:

Red litmus contains a weak diprotic acid. When it is exposed to a basic compound, the hydrogen ions react with the added base. The conjugate base, formed from the litmus acid, has a blue color, so the wet red litmus paper turns blue in alkaline solution.

BRA - blue litmus paper turns red when in contact with acid solutions

16. which of the following is TRUE about acids? a. acids taste bitter. b. acids feel slippery. c. metals react with acids to form hydrogen gas and salt. d. red litmus paper turns to blue when acid is added.


Answer: D

Explanation: Acids react with most metals to form a salt and hydrogen gas

The statement that is TRUE about acids is:

c. metals react with acids to form hydrogen gas and salt.

Acids are substances that release hydrogen ions (H+) when dissolved in water. They have a pH value of less than 7 and can react with certain metals to produce hydrogen gas and a salt. This is known as a single displacement reaction and is a common characteristic of acids.

Option a is false because acids do not taste bitter. In fact, many acids have a sour taste.

Option b is false because acids do not feel slippery. Bases, on the other hand, feel slippery due to their basic nature.

Option d is false because red litmus paper turns blue in the presence of bases, not acids. Acids, on the other hand, turn blue litmus paper to red.
