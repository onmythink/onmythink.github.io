8. Sentro ng imperyong Songhai ang Gao Tama o Mali?​


Answer:

Tama

Ang Imperyong Songhai (isinasalintitik din bilang Songhay) ay isang estado na nangibabaw sa kanluraning Sahel sa ika-15 at ika-16 na siglo. Sa tugatog nito, ito ay naging isa sa mga pinakamalaking estado sa Aprikanong kasaysayan. Ang estado ay nakikilala sa historyograpikal na pangalan nito, na nagmula mula sa nangungunang pangkat etniko at mga namumunong mga piling tao nito, ang Songhai. Itinatag ni Sonni Ali ang Gao bilang ang kabisera ng imperyo, bagaman ang isang estadong Songhay ay umiral na loob at paligid ng Gao noon pang ika-11 siglo. Ang iba pang mahahalagang mga lungsod sa buong imperyo ay ang Timbuktu at Djenne, na nasakop noong 1468 at 1475 ayon sa pagkakabanggit, kung saan ang mga nakasentro sa lungsod na kalakalan ay yumabong. Sa una, ang imperyo ay pinamahalaan ng dinastiyang Sonni (c. 1464-1493), ngunit mamaya ito ay pinalitan ng dinastiyang Askiya (1493-1591).

9. Text2. How to Cook Nian Gao (Tikoy)Text Type:​


Answer:

Nian Gao or more popularly known as Tikoy in the Philippines is a traditional Chinese new year’s cake made out of glutinous rice flour. This sweet sticky dish can be eaten all year round but is most popular during the Chinese new year.Let’s start cooking Nian Gao or tikoy. First, slice tikoy in small thin 1”x 2” portions. Then, beat an egg in a bowl.

To cook, heat a pan and add cooking oil for deep frying. Once the oil is hot, get a slice of tikoy and dip it in the egg before putting them in the pan . Do not overcrowd the pan. Fry tikoy until it turns golden brown in color. Remove from heat and drain excess oil. Serve and enjoy.CAN WE FRY THE TIKOY WITHOUT DIPPING IT IN EGG?

Tikoy can be fried without egg but it will be hard to do so because they tend to stick together or stick to the tongs if not coated.

WHAT CAN WE USE AS SUBSTITUTE TO EGG FOR COATING?

Flour or bread crumbs are sometimes used to coat the tikoy before frying it.

TIPS ON COOKING THIS RECIPE

Brush your knife with some oil before using it to slice the tikoy. It will help preventing the tikoy from sticking to the knife.

Make sure to drain the cooked tikoy from excess oil before serving them. During frying they tend to soak up much oil.Ingredients

500 grams tikoy (nian gao)

1 pc egg

cooking oil for frying

Instructions

Slice tikoy in small thin 1”x 2” portions.

In a bowl, beat egg.

Heat a pan and add cooking oil for deep frying.

Once oil is hot, get a tikoy slices and dip it in the egg before putting them in the pan . Do not overcrowd the pan.

Fry until golden brown in color.

Remove from heat and drain excess oil.

Transfer to a plate, serve and enjoy.
