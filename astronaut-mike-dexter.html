10. Why are astronauts in space use radios to communicate with other astronauts even abreast instead of talking face to face?​


Answer:

Because there is nothing out in space (like an atmosphere), the sound waves from one astronaut's whistling can't travel over to the other astronaut's ears. That's why the astronauts use radios to communicate—even if they're floating in space right next to each other!

15. One of two twins is an astronaut, while the other is areal estate broker. the astronaut embarks on high speed space travel and is gone for several years. Upon the astronaut return, the two twins reunite and observed their physical appearances. the observation will be that a. both have aged the same b. the astronaut has aged less c. the astronaut more d. the real estate broker has aged less


Answer:

b. the astronaut has aged less

Explanation:

This is due to the effects of time dilation. Time dilation is a consequence of Einstein's theory of special relativity, which states that as an object approaches the speed of light, time appears to slow down for that object. In this scenario, the astronaut twin would have experienced time at a slower rate compared to the real estate broker twin, who remained on Earth. Therefore, the astronaut twin would have aged less than the real estate broker twin.
