2. Differentiate a Descriptive design from Explanatory design. Use 203 sentences. Sana po may sumagot. ☺️.


Explanation:

Descriptive research design is a type of research design that involves collecting and analyzing data in order to describe a phenomenon or group. It is primarily concerned with describing what exists and how it is distributed, rather than explaining why it exists or how it came to be.

On the other hand, explanatory research design is a type of research design that involves collecting and analyzing data in order to explain the relationship between two or more variables. It is concerned with understanding the underlying causes of a phenomenon or relationship, and is often used to test hypotheses and theories about how things work.

One key difference between descriptive and explanatory research design is the focus of the study. Descriptive research design is focused on describing the characteristics of a group or phenomenon, while explanatory research design is focused on understanding the underlying causes or factors that contribute to a relationship or phenomenon.

Another difference is the type of data that is collected and analyzed. Descriptive research design typically involves collecting data through observation or survey methods, such as interviews, questionnaires, or focus groups. This data is then analyzed to describe the characteristics of the group or phenomenon. Explanatory research design, on the other hand, often involves collecting data through experimental methods, such as controlled experiments or randomized controlled trials, in order to manipulate variables and test hypotheses.

A third difference between the two types of research design is the level of control that is exercised over the study. Descriptive research design is typically less controlled, as it is focused on describing what exists rather than understanding why it exists. Explanatory research design, on the other hand, is typically more controlled, as it involves manipulating variables in order to test hypotheses and understand cause and effect relationships.

Overall, the main difference between descriptive and explanatory research design is the focus of the study and the type of data that is collected and analyzed. Descriptive research design is focused on describing what exists, while explanatory research design is focused on understanding why things exist and how they relate to one another.

Design elements and principlesLine

I’m sure we’re all aware of what lines are, but just to be sure, lines can be defined as any linear marks. So, when you think about it, lines make up just about everything. Even these words and letters you’re reading now are made up of thousands of curved, angled and straight lines.

Scale

Scale is a large part of design, sometimes literally. In a very basic definition, scale is the deliberate sizing of individual elements.

Colour

Colour is paramount. Colour creates specific moods, atmospheres, channels emotions and each shade has certain specific connotations associated with it. In short, colour can make or break your design.

Symmetry

As species, human beings are scientifically proven to be drawn to symmetry. We find symmetrical faces, patterns and designs generally more attractive, effective and beautiful.

Symmetry is used a lot in logos in order to create a harmonious and balanced design. Some examples of large brands with symmetrical logos are Target, McDonald’s, Chanel, Starbucks, etc.

Transparency

Also occasionally known as ‘opacity’, transparency refers to how ‘see-through’ an element is. The lower your opacity, the lighter and less noticeable your element is, and the higher it is, the more solid the element is.
